General Summary/Overview Statement

Mass General Brigham’s Enterprise Research IS (ERIS http://rc.partners.org is seeking a Data Science Application Specialist. This position works within multi-disciplinary teams that build data processing pipelines, computational based analytics jobs and web-based reporting tools. Using your knowledge of distributed computing systems and software development, you will specialize in data analytics tools and data science methods to facilitate healthcare research. Primarily this roll provides expertise to researchers who develop pipelines on the ERISOne HPC Linux Cluster and the ERISXdl Linux GPU Cluster providing users with both implementation and consultation assistance. This roll could be extended to support users across multiple projects and platforms within ERIS computational services.

We are seeking someone with the proven ability to help teams and projects successfully accomplish their technology objectives. Key characteristics are an eagerness to learn and apply new technologies, versatility and breadth in technical skills, and communication and customer service skills. Candidates are not expected to be deeply knowledgeable in all areas but must demonstrate the ability and desire to learn and to support a large and diverse environment in its use of technology. Ideal candidates thrive on variety and innovation in their daily work, on interaction with customers who are world-renowned leaders in their scientific field, and on working with a wide range of technologies in a decentralized non-standard environment (academic).

ERIS enables and supports the highly successful and innovative research programs of the largest teaching hospitals in the nation-Massachusetts General (MGH), Brigham and Women’s (BWH), McLean and Spaulding Rehabilitation Hospitals-with their more than 3200 grant-sponsored programs in the biomedical sciences, from basic to clinical and applied research


Principal Duties and Responsibilities

  • Enable Technical Customers: provide support and consulting to scientific researchers who use a broad spectrum of applications from diverse fields (genetics, epidemiology, drug development, natural language processing, and high-resolution imaging).
  • Participate in a multi-disciplinary team to design and build next generation analytics and collaboration platform solutions for research and clinical departments across the Mass General Brigham System.
  • Serve as a consultant to the researchers, reducing the complexity of the technology (HPC, GPU Cluster, and other systems) with the scientific groups.
  • Work closely with research clinicians and researchers to help implement their advanced processing pipelines on the ERIS computational platforms.
  • Understand performance characteristics of underlying server, network and storage hardware.
  • Audit systems and processes for compliance with security and data integrity requirements.
  • Provide expertise in data management, analytics and optimum data structuring to users for their large or diverse datasets using a Data Science approach.
  • Analyze and resolve customer and technical problems: Troubleshoot analytical problems when issues arise. Propose, maintain and enforce polices, practices and security procedures.
  • Create and close tasks within the service tracking tool using established standards
  • Develop and maintain system documentation as well as training and reference materials for the community using knowledge management tools.
  • Perform other duties as required by the situation and circumstances

  • BA/BS/engineering degree required or equivalent combination of skills/experience. Advanced degree in a computational science or analytical discipline preferred.
  • 6+ years minimum experience in working with systems or programming environments in Linux environments for scientific computing. Linux Administration experience not required.
  • 6+ years of active experience in the full programming lifecycle with related open-source software platforms and languages (at least one of: Python, C++, R).
  • Minimum 3 years’ experience working beyond the college level in a related position.
  • Experience installing and debugging Linux applications for science and healthcare research usage.
  • Demonstrated written communication skills and interpersonal skills required.
  • Ability to deeply understand application functionality and technical documentation required.
  • Highly desired:
    • Experience with machine learning and/or other data science technologies.
    • Experience with statistical methods and analytical tools (R, Matlab or domain-specific tools)
    • Experience with deep learning tools and GPU frameworks.

Skills/Abilities/Competencies Required

  • Must be capable of contributing within a multi-disciplinary team, exhibit a high level of initiative, and have an eagerness to learn new technologies.
  • Excellent interpersonal and communication skills with a demonstration of a professional and collaborative demeanor
  • Experience with Python/Anaconda environments customization.

  • Demonstrated abilities in self-organization, self-motivation and collaboration.
  • Candidate must possess advanced knowledge and understanding of Linux.

  • Ability to multitask and prioritize work requirements, keeping team and management informed.
