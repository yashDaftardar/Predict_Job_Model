Full Job Description

JPMorgan Chase & Co. (NYSE: JPM) is a leading global financial services firm with assets of $2.5 trillion and operations in more than 60 countries. The firm is a leader in investment banking, financial services for consumers, small business and commercial banking, financial transaction processing, asset management, and private equity.

Model Risk Governance & Review (MRGR) is a Corporate Risk team within JPMC with the responsibility of developing and implementing the Firmwide Model Risk Management (MRM) framework across the firm. The Central Challenger Team (CCT) within MRGR has a firm-wide purview for independently reviewing forecasting approaches or Qualitative Models or QMs (models relying on business assumptions) that are used in the Firm's capital stress testing, resolution planning, risk management and BAU forecasting processes.

Job Summary:

The model review lead is expected to manage a global team of reviewers (technical experts) in delivering independent validation of QMs developed within the Corporate Investment Bank (CIB) and Chief Investment Office (CIO).

Core Responsibilities:

  • Lead a team of model reviewers to:
    • Develop an understanding of the drivers of the behavior of balances/fees/losses of different products and businesses using a combination of research and liaising with business lines.
    • Analyze forecasting frameworks including but not limited to regression approaches to ensure they make business sense and satisfy statistical feasibility criteria
    • Stay abreast of macroeconomic, regulatory and industry landscape and bring this to bear in benchmarking analysis
    • Identify innovation opportunities to enhance forecast effectiveness and efficiency
    • Develop independent analytical calculators to challenge LOB results (Statistical modeling experience is considered a strong plus)
    • Maintain knowledge of capital markets products/market risk concepts and their applications in forecasting
  • Manage direct staff and other members of review team to support career development and provide mentorship
  • Partner with senior management on team-wide efforts that enhance the ability to effectively complete reviews as well as the overall work environment
  • Develop and maintain relationships with LOB stakeholders and partners across MRGR and Risk, including senior management

The team partners with the Corporate Capital Stress Testing team, Recovery & Resolution team, Lines of Business, Regulatory Policy, Financial Reporting, Quantitative Research and various other Finance and Risk teams on various firm-wide initiatives. The ability to work efficiently and communicate effectively across these boundaries will be one of the keys to success.

The specific role and responsibilities of the successful candidate will evolve based on qualifications, prior experience and demonstrated skills.

  • A minimum of 10+ years of experience in banking / financial services industry; experience in a risk management function preferred
  • Experience managing a team of technical experts
  • Advanced degree in Economics, Math/Statistics or related quantitative discipline preferred
  • Strong quantitative, analytical skills and flair for independent research & problem solving
  • Knowledge of capital markets, products, and pertinent regulatory processes & calculations is strongly preferred. Specifically, experience in modeling/validating models related to trading balances & revenue, and/or related to counterparty credit risk/market risk space is a plus
  • Strong organizational, communication (verbal and written) and negotiation skills
  • Attention to detail
  • Self-starter who is able to perform effectively in a fast paced, results driven environment
  • P ractical and pragmatic risk manager with a "risk and controls" mindset who is able to identify model risk issues and/or controls gaps and provide sign off on model validation reports

Additional qualifications/experience considerations:
  • Background or experience with various models (including pricing, capital, and/or financial forecasting models) and the model development and maintenance life cycle.
  • Experience in statistical modeling software is a plus (SAS, EViews, R, Matlab etc.)
  • Experience and knowledge in regulatory capital rules (Basel and CCAR)
