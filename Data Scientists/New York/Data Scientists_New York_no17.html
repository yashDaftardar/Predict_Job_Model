Full Job Description

The Faculty and Staff of Columbia University School of Nursing welcome you to a tradition of excellence in nursing education, clinical practice and research that spans more than a century. We represent the very best in quality and innovation within an academic environment. Our focus is on discovery and building evidence for effective practice and translating that evidence-based knowledge to practice and policy. Ranked fourth in National Institutes of Health funding among Schools of Nursing, our quantitative research includes clinical trials, implementation science, health services research, informatics, data science, and “omics”.

We are currently recruiting for an exceptional computational scientist for a non-tenure-track position in our Division of Scholarship & Research. As a research team member, you will be providing data science and statistical support to junior and senior federally-funded scientists as well as postdoctoral fellows and predoctoral students. In addition, you will establish standard analytic pipelines to support efficient integration and analysis breadth of heterogeneous data sources.

Incumbents will receive a non-tenure-track faculty appointment as Associate or Full Professor of (field of doctorate) (in Nursing) depending on experience and qualifications. Diverse candidates are encouraged to apply.

Columbia University offers rich opportunities with an infrastructure that provides the foundation for scholarly success. Our Faculty thrive in our new state-of-the-art building located in New York City. Columbia Nursing is a close-knit and collaborative team that partners on research, education and clinical experiences, and shares a vision for the future of healthcare in this country. We are internationally known and clinically unrivaled with recognition for excellence in research and curricular advancements as part of one of the world’s most renowned medical centers.

Columbia University offers a competitive salary & outstanding benefits package. The selected individual will receive an academic appointment commensurate with credentials & qualifications.
