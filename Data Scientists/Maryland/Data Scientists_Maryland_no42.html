Position Summary:

The Senior Data Scientist will work on a high-profile project to expand Johns Hopkins’ ability to analyze and present data to hospital and government leadership. This project is responsible for deeply understanding and simulating scoring metrics used by payers, regulators, and benchmarking agencies. It will allow health system leadership transform patient care, prioritize patient safety risks, and create a compass to find the most meaningful and impactful interventions. This project is critical for Johns Hopkins’ ability to optimize resources, maximize reimbursement, and produce the best possible outcomes for up to a million patients per year.



The Senior Data Scientist will have the following resources and challenges available:

  • Tens of billions of rich time-series data – containing key insights and actionable opportunities
  • A set of unmapped and novel data sources that have:
  • Categorical data with optimization & patterning problems
  • Exploratory modeling needs and prediction opportunities
  • Well defined, business specific endpoint analyses
  • Comprehensive access to data science platforms on Microsoft’s Azure cloud


In support of this project, the Senior Data Scientist will be working with the Precision Medicine Analytics Platform team to define and build the analytical tools, environments and infrastructure for operational research and analysis. Beyond this specific project, this position will be a member of the Precision Medicine initiative’s data science team which is at the vanguard helping to translate new science into clinical practice across a range of health conditions.

The Senior Data Scientist will be part of the Johns Hopkins Medicine Technology Innovation Center and will work full-time with the Precision Medicine and CaseMix teams (as well as clients, data stewards, project/program managers, and other IT teams). The position will turn data into critical information and knowledge that can be used to make sound organizational decisions. Other responsibilities will include providing data that is congruent and reliable, proposing innovative ways to look at problems by mining available data, and mentoring analytics staff on tools and frameworks.

Specific systems, applications, projects, environments, on which the position will be responsible to develop or maintain:

  • Precision Medicine Analytics Platform – guiding requirements development of hospital administration and operations segment of the Platform, including regulatory and hospital finance
  • CaseMix/Regulatory Finance Analytics – using the enriched data source and creating the next stage of analysis for hospital quality scoring and reimbursement
  • Precision Medicine Delivery Platform – contributing to clinical decision support applications intended to bring cutting edge science into clinical care.

Additional Knowledge, Skills & Abilities (KSAs):

  • Experience leading data science projects that demonstrate operational value and savings, increased organizational capacity preferred.
  • Experience with automated analyses and reproducible analytic products preferred.
  • Programming skills required (subset of SQL, Databricks, Jupyter, Python, R)
  • Experience leading teams and mentoring analytics staff preferred.

Examples of clients supported and degree of client interaction:

  • The Senior Data Scientist will be working with a community of engaged stakeholders and executives up to the C-suite, who are currently generating questions and hypothesis to make decisions that lead the direction of the health system.
  • Degree of supervision – how work is assigned, carried out, and reviewed:
  • In support of this project, the Senior Data Scientist will be working closely with both the CaseMix and Precision Medicine Analytics Platform teams:
  • To perform regular analyses for Quality Improvement and Finance leadership
  • To define and build the analytical tools, environments and infrastructure for operational research and analysis.
  • This will be a highly collaborative role, with regular weekly meetings.

Education:
Masters in mathematics, statistics or computer science or related field; PHD degree preferred.

Experience:
Requires 7 or more years of relevant quantitative and qualitative research and analytics experience.

Requires 5 years of complex, programming experience
