Position Overview

Adaptive is seeking a thoughtful Data Scientist who is enthusiastic about statistics, genetics, and improving human health. We are looking for someone who excels at analyzing genetic data, building statistical models, and communicating results to diverse audiences.

Data Scientist II (biostatistics/computational biology) contributes to essential experimental design and analysis activities that support molecular product development of in-vitro diagnostic products.

This role is part of a dynamic Molecular Product Development division that works with diverse functional groups at Adaptive Biotechnologies, creating robust immunosequencing applications to significantly improve patient care.


Responsibilities

  • Lead the statistical analysis design process supporting IVD-level products and QC method development studies while adhering to appropriate regulatory guidance, including CLSI and Design Control.
  • Collaborate with wet lab scientists and cross-functional teams to design experiments and analyze data.
  • Perform quick and thorough analyses of DNA sequencing data to understand outliers, make descriptive figures, and address key statistical and biological questions.
  • Build and evaluate probabilistic models of experimental processes for data generation.
  • Present results to a wide variety of stakeholders, from wet lab scientists to clinicians to executive team members.
  • BE A PROBLEM SOLVER: communicate well, ask questions, proactively eliminate bottlenecks, and be the person people go to when they want a job done right.
  • Job duties may include mentorship and supervision of more junior members of the team.

Requirements

  • Graduate degree (PhD or MS) in Statistics, Biostatistics, Computational Biology, or related field (2+ years with PhD and 10+ years of experience with MS).
  • Experience in analyzing high-throughput DNA sequencing data.
  • Proficiency in at least one common coding language (R, python, etc.).
  • Demonstrated ability to work on multidisciplinary teams.
  • Experience in presenting technical material to diverse audiences.
