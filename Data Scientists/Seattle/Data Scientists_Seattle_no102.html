The Modern Life Machine Learning team is looking for a Principal Data Science Manager to manage and grow our team of applied researchers. Our multi-disciplinary team of data scientists works across a broad portfolio of Microsoft consumer products, representing a $400B market opportunity, to develop innovative ML models partnering with product managers, strategic partners, and subject domain experts. In addition to typical advanced analytical models (behavior segmentation, churn prediction, purchase propensity, recommendation engine, etc.), the team specializes in Dynamic networks, DNN time series forecast, and reinforcement learning based sequential decision optimization.

In this position, you will be managing data science initiatives spanning multiple product lines. You will have opportunities for impact across the entire company, for instance, as it comes to user centric cross-product recommendations. At the same time, there is also some room for small-scale incubation. If you have a history of enabling organizations to successfully innovate, then we want to talk to you!

Responsibilities

  • Build/manage stakeholder relationships to deliver complex projects by maintaining data science roadmapvia quarterly sprint planning.
  • Work closely with stakeholders (engineering and marketing leadership) to prioritize, formulate, and action against business opportunities.
  • Provide technical mentorship (present/defend results) to guide data science solutions for a diverse set of business problems enabling learning while driving business impact.
  • Our team philosophy encourages knowledge sharing so you will the opportunity to contribute to the research community through technical papers and presentations published both internally and externally.

Qualifications

  • MS/Ph.D. in a quantitative field
  • 3+ years of experience managing an applied research team.
  • Research experience in, and familiarity with, the literature and the state-of-the-art research/practice in areas related to DNN, time series, reinforcement learning, network analysis, or causal inference.
  • Demonstrated leadership working with cross functional teams to deliver complex projects.
  • Ability to communicate quantitative analysis in a clear, precise, and actionable manner to non-technical audience.
