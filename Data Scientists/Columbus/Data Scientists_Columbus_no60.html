Research Assistant- RI Mathematical Medicine

We are seeking a Research Assistant - This position is in the Battelle Center for Mathematical Medicine in Dr. Christopher Bartlett’s lab. We are seeking a motivated research assistant to join our lab group. Our team is testing a genetic biomarker on aphasia recovery in a clinical trial in conjunction with The Ohio State University. Aphasia is a complication of neurological stroke whereby language functions fail to recover post-stroke, with limited therapeutic options. We test DNA from persons with chronic aphasia to assess variation at specific genetic locations hypothesized to help in language recovery. This project is funded by the National Institutes of Health as a clinical trial of our genetic biomarkers to predict recovery. The project is conducted through collaborations with the extensive expertise present in the Battelle Center for Mathematical Medicine and The Ohio State University’s Wexner Medical Center and Department of Speech Language Pathology. With guidance from Dr. Bartlett, you will be responsible for quick turnaround of sample processing and biomarker testing on an ongoing basis. Responsibilities will include logging incoming samples, DNA extraction, quantify sample quality, performing genotyping assays, data cleaning. You will also be responsible for lab management and safety, ordering, and assisting Dr. Bartlett with protocol preparation and submission. A background in genetics and DNA extraction is preferred, though general molecular biology experience is relevant. Experience with medium throughput genotyping technology is not required, but you must be comfortable with and excited about learning how to use this automation. The selected candidate must be self-motivated, professional, and dedicated to the ideal of helping persons who suffer from the chronic effects of stroke. You will have many opportunities to learn new skill sets, develop professionally, and contribute to clinically validating biomarkers for language recovery as part of a vibrant and diverse team of research and clinical professionals.

Responsibilities:

What Will You Be Doing?
  • Performing laboratory-based work including skills/techniques such as polymerase chain reaction (PCR), multiplex-PCR, gel-based nucleic acid visualization, spectrophotometry and fluorometry, DNA extraction, medium throughput genotyping based on flow cytometry mechanics.
  • Maintain a log of incoming samples and track sample progress through pipeline.
  • Generating data and maintaining a lab notebook.
  • Performing miscellaneous related duties to help the team.
Qualifications:

  • Six months to two years relevant laboratory experience preferred
  • Strong organizational skills
