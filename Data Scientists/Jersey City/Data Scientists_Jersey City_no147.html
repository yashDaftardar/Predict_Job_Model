Director, Data Science and Strategy

Location: Parsippany

As a privately-owned, specialty healthcare company, Ferring focuses on developing life-changing innovations that help people live better lives. Our independence helps us cultivate an entrepreneurial spirit and long-term perspective that enables us to achieve growth and scale while remaining agile and true to our ‘people first’ philosophy. Built on a 70-year commitment to science and research, Ferring is relentless in pursuit of science that drives powerful discoveries and therapies to help people build families, stay healthy, and stand up to the world’s oldest enemy: disease.

Summary:

This role will be responsible for building and maintaining a data driven information engine and leading the analytics and market research teams to execute on ideas by gathering, analyzing and automating the necessary data. They will create, standardize and democratize data science best practices and provide intelligence through trend-based forecasting, data mining, and statistical models using internal and external customer level datasets. In addition, they will drive a culture for informed, value-added, data driven decision making that improves business precision, productivity and performance. This role requires a unique combination of skillsets and strong business acumen along with the technical competence to gain the trust of internal and external stakeholders. This position will combine data science, software engineering, and business acumen to develop new innovative solutions that address critical business needs.

Responsibilities:

Identify trends and patterns that help our businesses make better decisions and build the precision, productivity and value of data and analytics :

  • Derive business value from internal and external data to addresses business critical challenges through data analysis and insights.
  • Apply a deep understanding of our businesses to recommend and develop appropriate analytics solutions and inform decision-making.
  • Communicate complex information and develop standardized requirements to inform the analytics and market research strategy.
  • Lead analytics and market research teams to implement new analytical methods to enhance output.

Own architecture and enterprise data management strategy and culture:

  • Develop data models and ensures appropriate data integration.
  • Synthesize data insights to align projects to organizational objectives.
  • Ensure automated standard reporting and access to data through BI platforms.
  • Integrate Market Research and Competitive Intelligence into an insights driven framework.

Optimize data infrastructure and automation by developing and deploying models across a multitude of algorithms to achieve faster, more targeted, self-service insights:

  • Create, standardize and democratize data science best practices.
  • Accelerate time consuming tasks that require vast amounts of knowledge to boost productivity and focus more on complex business problems and creative solutions.
  • Improve data accuracy, decreases bias, and avoid human based error that occurs with manual data modeling processes.
  • Partner with local and global IT on the optimal and relevant data architecture.

Adopt automation-first technology and promotes the operationalization of machine learning models:

  • Enhance technologies and processes to accelerate and operationalize Machine Learning (ML), Artificial Intelligence (AI) and cloud-based computing.
  • Leverage technology to automate AI and ML to implement better data management techniques into critical business processes to remain competitive.
  • Use Automated ML to boost productivity and automate the tasks associated with developing and deploying models.

Promote data consolidation for workflow efficiencies:

  • Clean and organize data to decrease time required to discover and normalize consistent and manageable data sources and increase opportunities to access new data sources.
  • Minimize data from disparate sources, stored in separate silos and in different formats.
  • Move toward data accuracy, workflow efficiency, single source consolidation, commonality and accessibility.

Unlock data across intelligence team (Market Research, Operations and Commercial Analytics) for greater insights:

  • Identify & use new customer data sources to improve business results.
  • Collect more data and encourage more ideas needed for analysis.
  • Promote joint management by bridging the gap between data science (knowledge) and operations (strength), establish ROE between teams, set measurable and joint KPIs.
  • Identify novel, non-overlapping data sources and innovative tools to address business needs.

Collaborate and partner with peers in other functions on cross-functional analytics projects and solutions:

  • Utilize full operations team to help with critical tasks (monitoring, alerting, upkeep, governance).
  • Partner with internal and external stakeholders and vendors to identify opportunities to use data to inform strategic choices.
  • Analyze and discuss project results and recommended actions with senior and executive leadership teams.
  • Conduct and coordinate large scale projects ensuring quality and meeting critical timelines.

Requirements:

  • Graduate Degree in quantitative disciple (e.g., Mathematics, Engineering, Operations Research or Business) required
  • Advanced degree (masters or doctoral) in statistics, computer science, economics, applied mathematics, or related field preferred
  • 5+ years of relevant experience, including business analysis, advanced statistics or quantitative/qualitative research
  • Experience in the appropriate use of statistical tools including correlation, regression, factorial analyses and modeling
  • Demonstrated competency in data engineering and pipeline technology experience with a common open-source data programming language
  • Strong understanding of healthcare data & technology; cross-industry experience in pharmaceutical, consumer, or technology industry preferred
  • Strong quantitative and diverse analytical skills: exploratory data analyses, computer science, statistics, modeling, mathematics, programming languages and business acumen
  • Querying, algorithms, data engineering, natural language processing, engine recommendation, experimentation, programming, data storytelling and intelligence, predictive modeling, machine learning
