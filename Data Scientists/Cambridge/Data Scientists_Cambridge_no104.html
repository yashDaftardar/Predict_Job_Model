Data Scientist Channing Division of Network Medicine

- (3147959)


The Channing Division of Network Medicine group on Systems Genetics and Genomics (SGG) is a dynamic group of physician-scientists, bioinformaticians, and statisticians targeting diverse diseases with large scale genetic and genomic epidemiological studies, often focusing on diseases of the respiratory system. The successful candidate will assist bioinformaticians, investigators, and data coordinating center staff of the CDNM in pursuing their mission on a number of studies, projects, and clinical trials.

PRINCIPAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:

  • Performs specific duties pertaining to research activities assigned by principal investigators.
  • Works independently and reports weekly or as needed to the Principal Investigators.
  • Prepares figures, tables, and slides for lectures and publications.
  • Coordinates research projects with collaborators.
  • Supports multi-‘omics’ research projects in asthma, COPD, and other major diseases, including genomics, transcriptomics, metabolomics, and epigenetics.
  • Organizes, calculates, and interprets data using general statistical tools.
  • Performs data retrieval, data management, and executes queries of the Partners Healthcare BioBank repository.
  • Performs other duties as required such as liaising with other investigators involved in the project.

M.S. or equivalent in Bioinformatics, Biomedical Informatics, Computer Science or a related biomedical research field required. Experience in Natural Language Processing (NLP), database management, and research data repositories including electronic medical records and biorepositories is preferred. Familiarity with R/Bioconductor, Python, and Linux/Unix command line required. Research experience in an academic or hospital setting preferred. Excellent communication skills, and verbal and written fluency in the English language, are required.

SKILLS/ ABILITIES/ COMPETENCIES REQUIRED:

  • Must have knowledge of bioinformatics and biomedical terminology as well as good general written and verbal skills.
  • High degree of communication skills and ability to present research professionally at meetings and via teleconference.
  • Must have the ability to work independently and in a timely fashion.
  • Meticulous attention to detail in all aspects of the job is necessary to obtain valid research results.
  • Needs ability to relate and work well with individuals from various backgrounds and fields of expertise.
  • Must have data analysis skills, and experience using R/Bioconductor, Python, and high performance computing.
  • Prior experience with biorepositories and/or medical records databases is recommended but not required.
  • Must work collaboratively with others
  • Experience in natural language processing (NLP), database management, and research data repositories including electronic medical records and biorepositories is recommended but not required.
