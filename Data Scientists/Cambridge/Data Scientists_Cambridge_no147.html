Full Job Description

Abbott is a global healthcare leader that helps people live more fully at all stages of life. Our portfolio of life-changing technologies spans the spectrum of healthcare, with leading businesses and products in diagnostics, medical devices, nutritionals, and branded generic medicines. Our 109,000 colleagues serve people in more than 160 countries.

Abbott Vascular provides innovative, minimally invasive and cost-effective products for treatment of vascular disease. Our extensive portfolio includes drug-eluting stents, bare metal stents, guide wires, balloon dilatation catheters, imaging catheters and software, vessel closure devices and peripheral stents.

Our location in Westford, MA, currently has an opportunity for a Staff R&D Engineer, Machine Learning . You will help develop machine learning and advanced analytics features for Abbott’s line of hospital cardiovascular diagnostic systems. Features you develop will help interventional cardiologists optimize their treatment strategies and improve patient’s lives. You will work with cardiology experts to identify opportunities, with research scientists to identify data sources, and with software engineers to develop your solutions into production-ready software. Help us usher in the next step in the evolution of evidence-based medicine, where software learns from past patient cases to predict individualized risks and opportunities for each new patient.

WHAT YOU’LL DO

  • Work with marketing, thought leaders in the cardiology community, and members of the advanced technology team to identify areas where machine learning and other data-driven approaches can help improve physician strategy during a coronary catherization procedure.
  • Develop raw data sources into curated, well-structured, and valuable assets for training machine-learning models.
  • Find, learn, develop, and apply whatever mathematical and computational techniques best address the opportunity and support the users of our cardiovascular diagnostic systems.
  • Develop prototype implementations in Python, present results and approaches to myriad stakeholders, prove the feasibility and viability of an approach.
  • Work with software engineers to integrate your prototype into a well-tested, robust software system.
  • Help to get the system approved by the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) and equivalent regulatory bodies in other countries around the world.
  • While there may be opportunities to publish, your main goal will always be to create working software that solves real problems for patients.
  • Most projects will involve working with structured data in either aggregate form or as individual patient records. In some situations, you might be incorporating insights from unstructured data such as medical imaging or natural language text.
  • Perform other related duties and responsibilities, on occasion, as assigned.

EDUCATION AND EXPERIENCE YOU’LL BRING

Required

  • Masters Degree in Computer Science, Applied Math, Biomedical Engineering or a related mathematics-intensive field. Or an equivalent combination of education and work experience.
  • 10+ years of experience developing machine learning or other data-driven software solutions.
  • Demonstrable programming skills in Python or similar language.
  • Willingness and ability to dive deep into the problem domain, to understand the context of the cath lab and the needs of our users.
  • Ability and desire to learn about new techniques and approaches.
  • Ability to work within a team and as an individual contributor in a fast-paced, changing environment.
  • Self-driven, able to collaborate in teams and interact with customers.
  • Strong verbal and written communications with ability to effectively communicate at multiple levels in the organization.
  • Strong business and technical aptitude with an attitude to solve complex problems.
  • Multitasks, prioritizes and meets deadlines in timely manner.
  • Strong organizational and follow-up skills, as well as attention to detail.
  • Ability to travel approximately 10%, including internationally.
  • Ability to maintain regular and predictable attendance.

Preferred

  • Doctoral Degree in a relevant discipline/concentration, with relevant project experience preferred.
  • Knowledge and experience with medical software systems or with the medical problem domain are a plus.
  • Software development experience and strong software engineering skills are a plus.
  • Knowledge of PyTorch, Sci-Kit Learn, Pandas, and Numpy are all a plus.
  • Familiarity with advanced probability modeling (e.g., Bayesian estimation) is a plus but not required.
