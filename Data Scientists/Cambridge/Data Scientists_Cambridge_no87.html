Analytics is a competitive differentiator for Nike and is fundamentally changing how the company serves athletes and consumers around the world. The Nike Commercial Analytics team builds analytic-based solutions to manage marketplace and optimize the supply chain. Using big data, advanced analytics and state-of-the-art technology, the team strives to ensure that Nike gets the right product to the right place at the right time for the consumers.

Who we are looking for

We are looking for a hardworking Data Scientist Director to join our Advanced Analytics team within Commercial Analytics. This role will be part of a multi-functional agile squad and will provide technical leadership in building predictive and prescriptive analytic solutions.

The candidate needs to be an excellent problem solver and have extensive hands-on analytics experience. You have deep understanding of complex technical and business issues. You simplify and distill complexity into testable hypotheses and scalable solutions. You know how to break down the work, amplify each squad member’s strength and identify/mitigate risks. You help push the boundary of what’s possible and challenge your team to strive for excellence and deliver value!

What you will work on

If this is you, you will be part of our Advanced Analytics, In Season Optimization squad. We deliver scalable solutions to power data driven automated decision making on a variety of problems, such as pricing, product allocation and other related key business decisions. Specifically, you will:

  • Design and Implement sophisticated algorithms and improve existing approaches based on statistical/econometric methods, machine learning techniques and big data solutions to forecast consumer demand and determine optimal level of investment in various stages of the supply chain.
  • Direct members of the squad through assessment of business requirements, data constraints and pros/cons of alternative analytics techniques to determine the best solution approach. Facilitate the planning, scheduling and value measurement of the work to meet timeline targets and success criteria.
  • Design, write, own and maintain production grade analytics code to support forecasting and/or optimization models. Review and ensure code quality of squad members.
  • Mentor and coach other team members; continue to expand knowledge of data science/analytics trends.
  • Be a key contributor to building, scoring, deploying, monitoring, updating and promoting Nike’s central forecasting platform.
  • Support the adoption of analytic products through effective storytelling and collaboration with key partners.
  • Participate in a continuous learning environment within the advanced analytics community through persistent development of new skills and sharing of knowledge through mentorships and contributions to the open source community.
Who you will work with

You will work with the Director of Data Science while partnering daily with fellow data scientists, data analysts, engineers and product owner on your squad. You will collaborate across our broader organization with our business teams in Demand and Supply Management as well as other data, analytics and technology functions at Nike.

What you bring
  • Advanced quantitative degree (Statistics, Mathematics, Economics, Computer Science or related field) and at least 7 years of relevant industry experience or Bachelor’s degree and 10-12 years of relevant experience.
  • Demonstrated understanding of market demand and supply interactions. Familiarity with common data sets related to products, consumers, market performance and supply chain.
  • Extensive expertise with data science methods, machine learning algorithms and production level programming principles.
  • Extensive and/or in-depth knowledge of forecasting techniques, such as surveys, trend analysis, econometric models and machine learning algorithms.
  • Highly proficient in programming languages (particularly Python, Pandas and SQL) and ability to apply them to develop, build and maintain production grade analytics software.
  • Demonstrated ability to apply programming skills to data acquisition, preprocessing, modeling and monitoring as well as familiarity with the wide range of data science/analytics software tools.
  • Experience in leading technical projects at enterprise scale and managing a team through all stages of Data Science/Analytics lifecycle.
  • Knowledge/Proficiency of mathematical optimization modeling and algorithms. Linear and Mixed Integer programming highly desired.
  • Demonstrated experience with common data science/analytics software tools (e.g. Jupyter Notebook, SQL, Spark, Git) and cloud computing platforms (e.g. Amazon Web Services).
