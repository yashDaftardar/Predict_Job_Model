Collibra Engineer, Enterprise Data and Digital Health

- (3147253)


Summary

Mass General Brigham is transforming the way patient care is delivered through investments in data and analytics. In recent years Mass General Brigham has significantly invested in data and analytic infrastructure, including an enterprise wide clinical and administrative system, an enterprise data warehouse, big data analytic capabilities, machine learning (ML) / artificial intelligence (AI) capabilities, and building out a massive bank of genetic data to drive improvements in personalized/precision medicine. To ensure maximized value from the exponentially growing data assets, robust data management and governance must be implemented across the enterprise.

We are looking for an experienced Collibra Engineer who will be responsible for the technical accountability of Mass General Brigham’s data assets. The incumbent will be accountable for creating metadata information for various data assets and build stewardship workflows using data governance tools while ensuring standards and best practices are adopted. Collibra Engineer will work closely with Data SME and business stakeholders to ensure data definitions are implemented and any necessary technical documentations are created.

While this enterprise role will support multiple data domains, we are seeking a Collibra Engineer with an extensive understanding of data management activities, have ability to execute business decisions, implementing business requirements in Data Governance tool, identify and work in resolving any data quality issues, and process stewardship work flow in DG Tools.

Principal Duties & Responsibilities

  • Set up Communities, Domains, Types, Attributes, Status, and Workflow.
  • Ensure appropriate standards and best practices are adopted while creating metadata content within Collibra DGC.
  • Demonstrate complete technical understanding of the asset responsible for within DGC.
  • Ensures appropriate user access policies are implemented as defined by the data owner.
  • Ensures Data SME’s are defined for each corporate data asset.
  • Ensures scheduled re-synchronization of metadata from Tableau, SQL Server, Azure, etc is reflecting within Collibra platform.
  • Ensures data quality/data integrity is maintained during technical processing of data (Example: ETL transformations) and work alongside Data SME’s to resolve any issues.
  • Ensures data consumers receive metadata information associated with the specific dataset
  • Follows change management process for the maintenance and enhancement of datasets.
  • Ensures any changes to the data content and metadata definitions are logged and auditable.
  • Facilitate the documentation and approval of data definitions, data quality rules, data profiling and data lineage for the data domain and create a community across the enterprise.
  • Support stakeholders in utilizing DG tools through sharing of best practices and demonstrations.
  • Hands-on work with Data Owners, Data SME
  • Support others in the daily use of Data Governance tool(s) and ensure adherence to Data Governance and Data Quality Standards at Mass General Brigham.
  • Use the Mass General Brigham values to govern decisions, actions and behaviors. These values guide how we get our work done: Patients, Affordability, Accountability & Service Commitment, Decisiveness, Innovation & Thoughtful Risk; and how we treat each other: Diversity & Inclusion, Integrity & Respect, Learning, Continuous Improvement & Personal Growth, Teamwork & Collaboration
  • Other duties as assigned


Qualifications

  • 5+ years of experience with Data Governance/Management.
  • Excellent understanding of data administration and management functions (collection, organization, analysis, distribution etc.).
  • Demonstrated experience with modern data management tools (e.g. Informatica, Collibra, Trillium, etc.). Preferably Collibra.
  • Experience with healthcare data a plus.
  • Experience understanding technical specification documentation.
  • Solid understanding of data structure, integration and relational database design.
  • Able to quickly adapt and learn in an ever-changing technical, contractual, and regulatory environment.
  • Prior experience developing and implementing data governance policies and procedures.
  • Proficient in MS Office (Excel, Word etc.).
  • Bachelor’s degree or combination of equivalent education and work experience.

Skills, Abilities and Competencies

  • Excellent stakeholder-facing and internal communication skills.
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills.
  • Ability to execute multiple tasks efficiently and effectively.
  • Self-motivated, independent and possesses the ability to learn quickly.
  • Analytical mindset with strong problem-solving skills.
  • Ability to build consensus across teams, with staff and leadership.
  • Ability to successfully negotiate and collaborate with others of different skillsets, backgrounds and levels within the organization.
  • Ability to effectively conduct meetings, both formal and informal.
  • Requires minimal direction from leadership.
