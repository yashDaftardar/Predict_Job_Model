Hybrid Role: Office/Work from Home

Position Summary:

The position will be responsible for managing a statistical programming team, providing support and technical guidance of statistical programming to clinical projects, and managing CROs, implementing quality control process.

Responsibilities and Duties:

  • Provides functional expertise and leadership to support clinical development, regulatory approval and market acceptance
  • Leads design and development of SAS programs/Marcos/efficient tools for ISS, ISE, clinical study reports, for regulatory submission, and for IB updates, DSUR, PSUR etc.
  • Validates/reviews statistical regulatory submission package (CDISC, SDTM, ADaM, define document, review guide etc.)
  • Supervises and manages CROs to ensure quality performance and meeting of timelines
  • Develops complex SAS programs and performs quality control checks for SAS code and outputs produced by CROs
  • Keeps management current on project status and provides ongoing risk assessments
  • Proactively identifies problems and develops global tools that increase the efficiency and capacity
  • Organizes or participates technical meetings including internal and external representatives
  • Responsible for managing statistical programmers, including setting objectives, managing performance, retaining and recruiting talents.

Experience:

  • 12 years of relevant pharmaceutical industry work experience, 1-2 years of people management experience
  • Experience writing SAS programs to create data sets, tables, figures, and listings reporting results of clinical trials
  • Experience with regulatory submission, creating all files, documents, and analyses necessary to support an electronic submissions in eCTD format, including ISS/ISE
  • Experience with SAS language, procedures, and options commonly used in clinical trial reporting including the Macro language, BASE SAS, SAS/STAT and SAS/GRAPH
  • Experience with the CDISC SDTM and AdaM models and transforming raw data into these standards
  • Experience working with all types of safety data and coding dictionaries commonly used by the pharmaceutical industry

Educational Requirements:

  • Bachelor/Master's Degree/in Biostatistics, Mathematics, Statistics, Computer Science, Life Science (or equivalent degree)
