This position in the Method Trending and Monitoring group, part of Analytical Development and Attribute Sciences, provides the opportunity to build and develop new tools and strategies to trend and monitor the GMP release and stability methods across an exciting and diverse biologics portfolio. The candidate will identify and connect data sources across systems, merge and analyze the results using statistical and visualization techniques to generate ongoing reports on method performance. You will be contributing to departmental strategy and future directions for method trending, and will be working with a broad network of matrix teams across our sites and outsourcing partners building a strong network of communication. This is a non-lab facing position that requires knowledge of GMP biologics methods in the areas of either physicochemical separations, process impurities, or potency. Experience in statistical analyses, data sciences, and data visualization for evaluating method performance and assay control trending is desired.

Roles and Responsibilities:

  • Work within a cohesive team focused on data science to build new systems from the ground up to monitor global release/stability methods within the biologics development department. Your skills in advanced Excel, Visualization software, data bases, and coding (Python, R, SAS, etc.) are the key to success for this position.
  • Design and develop monitoring systems for critical key performance indicators of both the methods and the group. This will include identifying metrics, and developing automated dashboards and reporting structures for our key stakeholders across our sites, departments and network.
  • Contribute to the growth of the team and the goal of transitioning from manual data collection systems in Excel used both internally and by our external testing labs and moving towards automated systems using your data science skills and by functioning as a liaison with the IT, Statistics and Data Analytics groups to develop a system that connects to a central data source.
  • Actively engage in investigations related to deviations and method failure to determine the root cause analysis and work with our subject matter experts to correct it.
  • You will contribute to the presentation of our progress at high visibility meetings across the network using your skills in 'telling a story with data.' The writing of standard operating procedures, work instructions and protocols will be required.

This role offers an exciting opportunity to be involved in a new team and build the systems that will assure the robustness of our release/stability analytical methods across a broad biologics clinical pipeline including antibody, fusion protein, bi-/tri-specific protein, and combination therapy, to meet regulatory needs. Most importantly, this work directly contributes to bringing life-saving medicines to patients who are waiting.

Competencies:

  • A PhD (5+), MS (7+), or BS with 9+ years in biochemistry, analytical chemistry, biotechnology, pharmaceutical sciences, or related degree with experience in analytical method development and the trending and monitoring of analytical methods.
  • Knowledge in data sciences, statistics, visualizing data, cleaning data, advanced Excel, working with databases, and visualizing data is highly desired. General knowledge in Python is highly desired. These skills will be used for the monitoring and trending of all GMP release and stability assays.
  • Basic business and/or technical knowledge and understanding of GMP methodologies and control strategies with expertise in one of the following, separation sciences (HPLC, iCIEF, CGE), potency, HCP or other impurity assays.
  • Clear and concise oral, written, and public presenting skills along with meticulous attention to detail are a must.
  • Experience working in process improvement projects that require creative problem solving skills and self-motivation are desired.
