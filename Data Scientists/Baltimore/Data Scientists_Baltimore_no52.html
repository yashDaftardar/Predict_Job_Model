Summary of Major Responsibilities

We are seeking a highly motivated Scientist, Machine Learning to join the Biostatistics and Informatics team. You will leverage electronic medical record (EMR) and electronic health record (EHR) data to lead the development of machine learning methods to enable earlier cancer detection and localization. You will conduct analyses on clinical data to drive the development of a state-of-the-art AI engine with that integrates multi-omics assay data and supports a continuous learning loop. In close collaboration with other machine learning scientists, statisticians, data scientists, clinical operation specialists and technology teams, you will help build high quality, scalable solutions to address a critical unmet medical need.

Essential Duties and Responsibilities

Include but are not limited to the following:

  • Find cancer-relevant signal in EMR and EHR clinical data.
  • Develop machine learning models and AI solutions to improve the accuracy of early cancer detection, including but not limited to feature engineering, statistical analysis, evaluating classifier performance, error analysis, and ensuring fit-for-purpose.
  • Lead the optimization and validation of the clinical data-based model performance.
  • Analyze and understand the potential added value by integrating multi-omics assay data and clinical data as well as understand underlying biological reasons.
  • Partner with other machine learning scientists, data scientists, statisticians, clinical operation specialists and cross-functional teams to support continuous learning loop.
  • Collaborate with the Technology team to integrate validated algorithms into software development.
  • Communicate results in internal and external forums to support scientific, regulatory and business objectives.
  • Uphold company mission and values through accountability, innovation, integrity, quality, and teamwork.
  • Support and comply with the company’s Quality Management System policies and procedures.
  • Regular and reliable attendance.

Qualifications

Minimum Qualifications
  • Master’s degree with 3 years’ experience or PhD degree in Statistics, Computational Biology, Applied Mathematics or related quantitative fields with at least one significant project leveraging machine learning (ML) methods or models.
  • Experience with supervised and unsupervised machine learning algorithms and ensemble methods.
  • In-depth knowledge of statistical analysis methods in clinical data analyses.
  • Strong programming and computing skills (e.g., Python, R).
  • Proficient in managing multiple projects as part of a small team with matrixed resources.
  • Self-motivated, with strong organizational skills, attention to detail and an ability to adapt to increasing opportunities and responsibilities as our team rapidly grows.
  • Demonstrated ability to perform the Essential Duties of the position with or without accommodation.
Preferred Qualifications
  • Master’s degree with 3-5 years’ experience or PhD degree with 1+ years’ experience in industry; experience in cancer diagnostics or biopharma is preferred.
  • Previous experience with EMR/EHR relevant clinical datasets.
  • Expertise in data mining approaches within healthcare settings generating insight from routinely collected healthcare data.
  • Extensive scientific understanding of cancer biology, technical proficiency and creativity, and a strong capacity for innovative thinking.
  • Familiar with machine learning model tracking and deployment platforms (e.g., MLflow, Comet.ml).
