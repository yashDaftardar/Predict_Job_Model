Full Job Description

The Associate Data Scientist applies and integrates cutting-edge statistical, mathematical, predictive modeling and business analysis skills to conduct and manipulate complex high-volume data from a variety of sources. In this role you will interpret findings and make recommendations to business partners.

Functions

  • Design, develop, and evaluate predictive models and advanced algorithms. Develop, implement, and test machine learning models.
  • Leverage structured and unstructured datasets to implement new statistical or other mathematical methodologies as needed for specific models or data analysis (i.e., linear and nonlinear regression, clustering, etc.).
  • Generate and test hypotheses. Analyze and interpret the results of product experiments.
  • Design and coordinate field experiments to identify product enhancement opportunities.
  • In partnership with other data scientists, serve in a consultative role to share and illustrate insights in support of organizational goals.
  • Assist analysts across the organization to identify process automation opportunities and improve analysis methods.
  • Provide ad-hoc analysis which includes the ability to combine several data sources to communicate findings with effective visualizations and reports as needed for senior/executive management.
  • May assist in sourcing new data, building and optimizing data structures for the storage, integration, designing a prototype ETL process and seeing it through to production.
  • Demonstrate compliance with all bank regulations that apply to your position and keep up to date on regulation changes.
  • Maintain working knowledge of our policies and procedures regarding the Bank Secrecy Act, Regulation CC, Regulation E, Bank Security and other regulations that apply to your position.

Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s degree in STEM field, e.g., data science, statistics, economics, financial engineering, operations research, physics, or technology required
  • Advanced degree in STEM field, e.g., data science, statistics, economics, financial engineering, operations research, physics, or technology preferred
  • 4-7 of experience data mining and analysis experience required, with strong understanding of statistical methods and principles
  • Understanding of, and ability to implement, several supervised and unsupervised machine learning algorithms (i.e., k-NN, Naïve Bayes, Logistic and Linear Regression, Random Forests, etc.)
  • Proficiency in common data science programming languages and associated toolkits such as R, SQL, Python with NumPy, Pandas, SciPy, scikit-learn, TensorFlow, etc.
  • Understanding of cloud computing concepts, and big data processing methods such as MapReduce, Spark, and parallel computing in general
  • Excellent data visualization skills using multiple tools, such as Tableau or Power BI, Matplotlib, ggplot, and D3.js
  • Advanced Microsoft Excel skills, ideally including an ability to transition processes from Excel to more sophisticated tools

Physical Requirements and Working Conditions

Work involves:

  • Sitting
  • Occasional walking
  • Lifting up to 10 pounds.
