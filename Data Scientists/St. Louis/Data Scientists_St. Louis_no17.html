Director of Customer Data - Crop Science





PURPOSE:

Responsible for creating new information assets and evolving existing data systems that unlock innovative methods in digital product delivery, data science, and machine learning to enable smart pipeline, connected value chain and customer insights. Leading a highly skilled, agile engineering teams that ship software powered data solutions that will drive digital transformation, creation of new business products and services and shape the future of agriculture. Responsible for developing and implementing Data Quality, Governance and Evangelism programs for data managed within the asset to promote optimum data asset usage while designing standards and policies to ensure compliant usage. This role reports to the Head of Global Data Assets Crop Science.


YOUR TASKS AND RESPONSIBILITIES


The primary responsibilities of this role, Director of Customer Data - Crop Science, are to:

  • Define and manage the strategic vision, direction and execution of all associated information asset engineering products for Bayer Crop Science;
  • Create innovative data product strategies that support Bayer Crop Science objectives and strategies and ensure asset products and roadmaps are aligned to deliver the necessary response;
  • Identify new kinds, types and sources of data to drive business innovation across the company;
  • Deliver a clearly differentiated information asset portfolio that address the needs of Bayer Crop Science in an increasingly fast-moving environment;
  • Lead multiple multi-functional engineering teams concurrently, and manage resources to ensure on-time delivery;
  • Partner with digital product teams to unlock data insights and embed decision science into everything we do;
  • Drive the Agile/Scrum process for the development of our information asset products;
  • Create and deliver presentations and demos, both internally and publicly;
  • Build strong relationships and communities with engineering, stewardship governance, strategy teams to support asset investment and development;
  • Create new thought leadership content around the underlying information asset and data for our blog, white papers, conferences, etc.;
  • Place a high priority on building an effective team and talent pipeline by selecting, attracting, developing and leading an inclusive team Lead Open Data efforts, making data FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable and Reusable) to strengthen Bayer Crop Science's effectiveness in carrying out its mission.

WHO YOU ARE


Your success will be driven by your demonstration of our LIFE values. More specifically related to this position, Bayer seeks an incumbent who possesses the following:


Required Qualifications:

  • Must have one of the following:
    • Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Science or related field with minimum AND 12 years of engineering management or equivalent experience; or
    • Minimum of 16 years of engineering management experience or equivalent;
  • Experience building and leading diverse and highly talented teams at Internet-scale;
  • 8+ years of experience leading IT teams within life sciences, bioinformatics, biochemistry, genetics, biology, or other scientific discipline;
  • 8+ years of experience managing software development teams and shipping software-based products or services;
  • 5+ years of experience in developing and leveraging relationships to influence business outcomes;
  • Systematically strong analytic and quantitative analysis skills;
  • Experience leading agile organizations with a strong understanding of agile development, continuous deployment, DevOps, and decision science – AI, ML, OR, etc.;
  • Excellent organizational, verbal and written communication skills with proven ability to grow relationships with key partners and suppliers;
  • Ability to work effectively in ambiguous situations using critical thinking skills and be decisive while working cooperatively with others;
  • Experience with modern engineering technologies and data principles, for instance: Big Data Cloud Compute, NoSQL, etc.
