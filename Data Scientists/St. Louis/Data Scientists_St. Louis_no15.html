Data Scientist Computer Vision





YOUR TASKS AND RESPONSIBILITIES


The primary responsibilities of this role, Data Scientist Computer Vision, are to:

  • Provide technical contributions in a fast-paced team environment to accelerate our efforts on building cutting-edge workflows and applications for capturing, processing and analyzing large data sets;
  • Create novel and apply state-of-the-art methods and technologies from computer vision, machine learning, and deep learning in pilot projects together with scientists from across Research and Development (R&D);
  • Realize tangible value creation, work towards communicating learnings and successes and establish the application of such approaches within R&D;
  • Provide guidance and recommendation to project teams on requirements for data collection in field, lab or manufacturing settings and corresponding labeling, cataloging and storage needs;
  • Perform independently statistical analysis, computer programming, predictive modeling and experimental design;
  • Build cross-functional relationships to collaboratively partner with the business and effectively network within the Data Science Community;
  • Use advanced mathematical models, machine learning algorithms, operations research techniques, and strong business acumen to deliver insight, recommendations, and solutions;
  • Develop sustainable, consumable, accurate, and impactful reporting on model inputs, model outputs, observed outputs, business impact, and Key Performance Indicators (KPI);
  • Present validated results to all levels of organization, including peers, senior management, and internal customers to drive both strategic and operational changes in business.

WHO YOU ARE


Your success will be driven by your demonstration of our LIFE values. More specifically related to this position, Bayer seeks an incumbent who possesses the following:


Required Qualifications:


  • Bachelor’s degree with at least five years of experience OR Master’s degree with at least two years of experience OR Ph.D. in artificial intelligence, machine learning, deep learning, computer vision, statistics, applied mathematics, computer science or other related quantitative discipline;
  • Experience developing end-to-end computer vision and machine learning pipelines including data preprocessing, model development and evaluation, model deployment, data and model drift detections;
  • Have strong proficiency in predictive modeling, to include comprehension of theory, modeling/identification strategies and limitations and pitfalls;
  • Knowledge of computer vision and deep learning frameworks such as OpenCV, Tensorflow, PyTorch and Keras with hands on experience working in computer vision and machine learning using models such as CNN, RNN, LSTM and GAN;
  • Experience with geospatial image analytics, agricultural image analysis, deployment of computer vision models in mobile apps;
  • Demonstrate intermediate proficiency in computational skills and level of experience building data models using R, Python or other statistical and/or mathematical programming packages;
  • Demonstrate basic understanding of software development best practices (Version Control, Code Documentation and Review, Cloud Based Sequence Analysis, Database Management);
  • Visionary mindset and ability to think and act strategically, combined with execution orientation;
  • Ability to solve problems and to structure and simplify complex tasks;
  • Experience in successful delivery of valuable analysis through application of domain knowledge;
  • Evidence of ability to strong business acumen;
  • Strong communication competencies to include presentations and delivery of complex quantitative analyses in a clear, concise and actionable manner to extended team and small groups of key stakeholders;
  • Fluency in English, both verbal and written.
