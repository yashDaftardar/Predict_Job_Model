GENERAL DESCRIPTION:

The Principle Statistician coordinates statutorily driven data and analysis requirements for the Wyoming Department of Education. With a primary focus on state accountability, career and technical education, and the Hathaway Scholarship, this position gathers customer requirements from multiple sources to develop new, and evolve existing, data collection and analysis processes. This position is expected to provide leadership and direction to the accountability consultant, Hathaway Scholarship and Career Technical Education educational programs, data collection, and technical staff to ensure accurate, high-quality data is collected and responsibly managed. This position is responsible for complex statistical data analysis, including regression and HLM, and the generation of customized data sets and reports to fill the needs of internal and external customers. Advanced R and SQL programming skills required. Additionally, this position assists with report creation using WebFocus reporting software.

Human Resource Contact: Chris Smith (307) 777-6243 ; chris.smith1@wyo.gov

ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS: The listed functions are illustrative only and are not intended to describe every function which may be performed at the job level.

  • Data Modeling and Managemen t:
    • Provide direction in the collaborative evaluation of the WAEA and ESSA accountability systems and prepare analysis for the PJP and TAG to ensure that the committees have accurate information to evolve the state accountability system and improve instruction.
    • Compile, design, and implement conceptual, logical, and physical data modeling.
    • Provide data modeling thought leadership in collaborating with education program and data management staff to ensure data storage and management processes are structured to meet multiple stakeholders' needs with unique reporting and research requirements.
    • Evaluate sources of data and information to identify valid uses, and limitations, for value-added analysis.

  • Data Analysis and Reporting
    • Design research and statistical modeling projects that apply valid techniques and use information obtained from baselines or historical data to structure uncompromised and efficient analyses.
    • Identify relationships and trends in and across various data sets through appropriate analysis and research techniques.
    • Design and perform longitudinal data analysis to meet program research and reporting requirements.
    • Conduct program evaluation analysis.
    • Monitor and evolve ways in which statistical methods are applied to education data and continuously evaluate whether analysis methods and results are valid and reliable.
    • Report research and analysis findings in final reports and/or supporting documentation (charts, graphs, findings). Information is required to be presented appropriately to the intended audience of each specific report.

  • Data Requests
    • Use statistical software and database programming techniques to enable and support ad-hoc and system-generated data and information reporting.
    • Complete requirements documents for data requests for use in automation of such requests and facilitating knowledge management of data needs and requests.
    • Provide data and information to the third-party public and private research organizations.
    • Creates ADA compliant reports using WebFocus software.
