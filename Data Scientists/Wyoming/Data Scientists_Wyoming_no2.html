GENERAL DESCRIPTION:
The position exists to improve the operational efficiency of the Human Resources Division. This position primarily works with Group Insurance plans, providing highly technical solutions to the section's needs, managing varied projects within the Human Resources Division, and assisting the program managers with activities such as vendor management, reporting, contracting, and the execution of projects. This individual designs & develops Employees' Group Insurance's (EGI) internal reporting infrastructure, implementing automated business intelligence solutions to drive key leadership decisions. This position is also responsible for the ongoing management of information security policies, procedures, and technical systems to maintain the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of all organization health care physical and information systems. The position requires a blend of technical acumen with keen analytic insights to aid strategic planning initiatives across all business lines.

Human Resource Contact: Nicki Reitmeyer; nicki.reitmeyer@wyo.gov

ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS: The listed functions are illustrative only and are not intended to describe every function which may be performed in the job level.

  • Provides coordination and leadership for records/data management for HRD.
  • Establishes methodologies for the collection and abstraction of data from multiple sources.
  • Analyze data files, data layouts, and data dictionaries from providers of health care administrative data (eligibility, account/plan, medical claims, pharmacy claims) to understand data structure and operating rules for transforming to common data format.
  • Conduct in-depth interviews with data providers (member or payer staff) to ensure a comprehensive understanding of data operating logic and data provided.
  • Reviews complex financial, insurance trade, or policy statements.
  • Analyzes all areas to provide the agency with concrete tools to monitor the program.
  • Author data mapping and transformation requirements based on an understanding of operating rules.
  • Exercises judgment in the application of health care analysis.
  • Conducts surveys.
  • Provides analysis of pertinent financial, economic, demographic, health, or care issues.
  • Serve as subject matter expert in primary data domain relative to EGl's data and transformation rules.
  • Execute test cases to validate source data have been accurately mapped, transformed, and loaded to a common format.
  • Define standard data quality checks at appropriate stages to ensure all data meets requirements for analytics and reporting.
  • Interface with internal business stakeholders to understand data needs and uses around data analytics and reporting.
  • Designs and implements auditing and reporting processes and works with payroll analysts to ensure the payroll system's data accuracy.
  • Create and refine processes to improve our section's ability to help the State attract and retain the best employees. Identify, suggest, and implement ongoing process improvements with a focus on eliminating inefficient and/or manual processes.
  • Responsible for implementing, managing, and enforcing information security directives as mandated by HIPAA.
  • Perform ongoing information risk assessments and audits to ensure that information systems are adequately protected and meet HIPAA requirements.
  • The position is responsible for independently planning and carrying out all stages of assigned projects, including the approach to analysis and resolution of data conflicts. Final work products are typically reviewed in terms of whether program objectives have been satisfactorily met.
