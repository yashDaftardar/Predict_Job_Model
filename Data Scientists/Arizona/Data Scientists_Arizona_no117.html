Full Job Description

Job Purpose Summary: The Quantitative Medicine Scientist (QMS) is a member of C-Path's Quantitative Medicine Program. This individual develops solutions within their area of responsibility by identifying the most appropriate quantitative methods guided by scientific and clinical considerations and knowledge of what the data will support. This role specifically will require the QMS to develop predictive analytics in collaboration with cross-functional teams, and key stakeholders to help extract insights from Electronic medical records (EMR) and other related clinical information systems intended to support decision making in drug development. To be successful, the QMS must have the ability to communicate effectively with external stakeholders from industry, regulatory agencies, non-profit organizations, and academic institutions. Strong writing skills are essential for the role and will be relied upon to contribute to the scientific content of regulatory submissions, manuscripts, posters, grants, and other externally facing documents.

Qualifications: To perform this job successfully, the individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily. The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skills, and abilities required. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.

Essential Job Duties and Responsibilities:

  • Extract, Interpret and analyze clinical data from a variety of sources.

  • Use quantitative methods to integrate knowledge of real-world data/evidence (RWD/RWE) and predictive analytics to inform drug development strategy.

  • Assemble and analyze large diverse datasets, extracting insights and driving additional research opportunities.

  • Execute modeling and simulation analysis plans.

  • Works with multifunctional internal and external teams to deliver and report the assigned analytics tasks.

  • Design and prototype novel data visualization methods, analysis tools, and algorithms.

  • Implement modeling and simulation plans based on agreed upon best practices (i.e. model-based drug development).

  • Summarize and interpret results of data analyses with respect to their utility and impact in supporting development of quantitative drug development tools.

  • Assist in preparation of scientific summaries and reports for regulatory submissions and publications.

  • Other relevant duties and responsibilities may be assigned.

Education and Training:

  • PharmD, PhD, MD or equivalent training or experience in health informatics, clinical informatics, statistics, computer science, artificial intelligence, engineering, or related discipline. 1-2 years industry/postdoc experience in RWD/RWE and predictive analytics preferred

  • Regulatory and/or EMR certifications is a plus.

  • Proficiency or experience working in team settings.

Computer Skills:

  • Proficient in one or more programming languages such as R, Python, and SAS

  • Knowledge of relational database design and management including Structured Query Language is highly preferred

  • Experienced in reading and interpreting freehand and electronic medical terminology (e.g. abbreviations found in SIG of Rx, common abbreviations, acronyms, and codes found in SOAP notes, ICD9 codes, and medical and pharmacy claim data) is highly preferred

  • Extensive experience in machine learning with a proven track record of developing and applying advanced computational techniques to solve complex disease problems

  • Knowledge of advanced machine learning methodologies such as deep learning, sequence modeling, and natural language processing

  • Experience with AWS or other cloud architecture is a plus

  • Experience working with high-performance computing clusters, especially ones designed for AI/machine learning applications, such as AWS SageMaker, is a plus

  • Proficient use of Microsoft Office Suite: Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook.

Knowledge/Skill/Abilities:

  • Technical: Demonstrates thorough understanding of the following: 1) Strategies for achieving effective data acquisition, management, quality, storage, use, and application to address key drug development needs. 2) Public health reporting and information system standards. 3) Public health reporting and information system standards 4) Health information vocabularies and data standards (e.g. HL7, ICD, LOINC and SNOMED codes) Information system design, development, and management) EHR/EMR, Pharmacy and Laboratory information systems

  • Clinical Pharmacology : Demonstrates thorough understanding or aptitude for the following: 1) principles and practices of clinical care, epidemiology and public health research and practice. 2) knowledge of phase I-III studies including design and interpretation. Demonstrates basic knowledge of the following 1) neonates and infectious diseases. 2) hematology, immunology, molecular biology, receptor biology, and common transport systems 3) physiology and the biochemical process of organs pertaining to absorption, distribution, metabolism, and elimination. 4) ability to recognize the indications of for pharmacotherapeutics used to treat common disease states 5) routine clinical laboratory assessments and associated reference ranges 6) Ability to interpret clinical laboratory findings and identify signs of organ dysfunction, and infectious and noninfectious diseases. 7) limitations associated with clinical laboratory findings and circumstances when standard findings are unreliable.

  • Communication : demonstrates ability to effectively present development/analysis plans, results, and strategies to various audiences in both verbal and written form; demonstrates ability to write clinical pharmacology results, interpretations (including impact) and conclusions for reports and regulatory documents that are clear and concise.

  • Scientific Excellence: demonstrates understanding of the complexities and recent developments in healthcare analytics and the implications for drug development.

  • Innovation: constantly looking for new approaches and able to devise/apply new techniques in quantitative medicine, clinical pharmacology and modeling and simulation.

  • Courage with Decisiveness to Act: bias towards action to achieve goals; excitement, enthusiasm, and a sense of urgency with regard to the development of drug development tools.

  • Practice highest level of integrity and core value system consistent with C-Path's code of conduct.

  • Ability to meet target deadlines and manage time effectively, balanced across multiple projects.

Language Ability: Excellent oral, written and virtual communication skills.
