With more than 130 dealerships, 3 operations centers and 15 reconditioning centers across the nation, our 4,000+ employees are focused on getting the right customer in the right car, at the right terms. With over 25 years of industry experience, and our dedication to streamlining the purchase process, we're redefining what it means to buy and finance a used car.

Responsibilities:
That’s Nice, But What’s the Job?

In short, as a Data Scientist you will build, implement, maintain, and report on predictive models and other analytic tools in support of DriveTime business initiatives.

In long, the Data Scientist is responsible for:

  • Work with business partners to develop new statistical models and/or analytic data solutions
  • Partner with business customers to manage data for modeling and analytics
  • Work with IT to implement models and or analytic data solutions
  • Assist colleagues in retraining models as needed
  • Present results of analysis to leaders and colleagues
Qualifications:
So What Kind of Folks Are We Looking for?
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills. The ability to talk and write with confidence, charisma and competence for a wide variety of audiences including management.
  • Organization and time management skills in spades. You’ll be handling multiple projects and deadlines that will require you to prioritize then re-prioritize… then… re-prioritize again.
  • Intellectual curiosity. Why? What? How? Do you find yourself always wanting to learn more and broaden your knowledge base? If so, this could be the role for you.
  • Agile in a fast-paced environment. We move, and we move quickly. Thriving in an environment that never stops, is a must.
  • Rebel with a cause. You are always looking beyond the obvious for continuous improvement.

The Requirements.

  • Bachelor’s Degree in Math, Statistics, Physics, Computer Science, or related discipline
  • 0-2 Years of Experience Working in a Data Scientist or Statistician Role
  • Knowledge in Python, R, and other statistical tools
  • Knowledge in MS SQL, Snowflake, and other database tools
  • Ability to understand and interpret results from multiple regression types, gradient boosting, decision trees, and other machine learning techniques
  • Excel skills a plus
  • Ability to access, handle, and manipulate multiple data formats

Nice to Haves.

  • Master’s Degree in Math, Statistics, Physics, Computer Science, or related discipline a plus
