Job Description:
Overview

We seek an enthusiastic, collaborative data/machine learning scientist to help develop groundbreaking computational technologies that will enable rapid development and improvement of next generation cellular therapy products. The successful candidate is expected to play a key role partnering with several internal research teams to develop and implement robust statistical / machine learning / deep learning models from images of cells to predict cellular phenotypes.

The successful candidate will work alongside colleagues in Predictive Sciences and Knowledge Sciences as well as data stakeholders in Cell Therapy Development and Operations (CTDO) and Cell Therapy Process Science and Development to enable high-throughput, automated characterization of CAR T cells for product development and production.

Keen interest and hands-on expertise in the inter-disciplinary application of advanced analytical methods to cellular imaging datasets are imperative. We are deeply passionate about what we do here at BMS, because we know that our work helps deliver truly innovative and life-changing therapies for complex diseases of unmet medical need—if this inspires you as well, we’re excited to have you join us!

Responsibilities

Working in collaboration with computational, biological, and clinical scientists across the Bristol Myers Squibb IPS and CTDO organizations, responsibilities include but are not limited to:
  • Develop and apply robust, state-of-the-art algorithmic and data-driven approaches to model and predict cellular phenotypes or states from cellular images.

  • Help develop an understanding of relevant cellular phenotypes and their associated characteristics.

  • Assist in automating the management, handling, processing and cleaning of large amounts of instrumental data including cellular images and high-dimensional cellular metadata.

  • Engage with wet-lab colleagues to understand and participate in the planning of experimental strategies necessary to maximize predictive capabilities, and to understand the resulting data generated.

  • Present strategies, approaches, results, and conclusions to a multidisciplinary team of computational and biological scientists.

  • Automate data processing and develop data visualization dashboards using R|Shiny.


    • Background experience & complementary knowledge

  • Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in computer science, data science, statistics, computer vision, or a related data science-oriented field.

  • Demonstrated ability to develop, implement, and test robust statistical multivariate models of imaging and/or high-dimensional data.

  • Expertise in programming and data manipulation, using e.g. Unix shell, R, and Python, contemporary, open-source machine learning and/or deep learning tools and libraries, and collaborative development skills using version control systems (e.g. Git).

  • Proven problem-solving skills, collaborative nature and flexibility across multiple research domains.

  • Some background knowledge in computational biology, immunology, and wet-lab experimental procedures and data analysis are a plus.
