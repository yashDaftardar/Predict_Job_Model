Job Description

The Pacific Northwest National Laboratory (PNNL) and the Environmental Molecular Sciences Laboratory (EMSL) seek a data scientist focused on data management and data integration. The EMSL is a U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) Office of Science user facility that provides innovative and breakthrough experimental and computational science that addresses DOE Office of Biological and Environmental Research (BER) programs by providing access to more than 75 state-of-the-art instrumental and high-performance computing capabilities. The candidate should have experience with or interest in scientific software development and management of scientific data in accordance with the FAIR principles. The candidate will contribute to scientific data management at EMSL, spanning the full data life-cycle including sample and metadata submission, instrument operation, data synthesis and analysis, data archival and curation in the EMSL data repository, and data release and publication, as well as data Search and Retrieval services for the broad scientific community. The candidate will also contribute to innovative methods to integrate the diverse data generated in EMSL. The candidate must be adept at collaborating with interdisciplinary technical teams through strong communication and interpersonal skills.

  • Designs, develops and implements methods, processes and systems to manage and integrate diverse data.

  • Applies knowledge of software development and data modeling to integrate and clean structured and/or unstructured data.

  • Collaborates with domain scientists in order to generate data to define requirements and prioritize development of software for data analysis, visual analytics, or other computational techniques.

  • Contributes to a research program in data science applied to the biological or environmental sciences, developing data analysis and integration approaches for omics or imaging data.

Discipline, principal job duties/expectations, and qualitative and quantitative measures of performance that exceed the Functional Descriptor:

  • Selects and develops technical approaches on assignments with occasional oversight on complex problems.

Level 2 : Receives guidance on new assignments, making preliminary selections on technical alternatives; independently completes recurring assignments. Defines and leads project work at a small task/project level, reporting results on time and on budget. Contributes to proposals. Embraces expectations for quality, safety, and security. Communicates the importance within the work team.

Level 3 : Selects and develops technical approaches on assignments with occasional oversight on complex problems. Principal investigator or co-PI on projects or tasks, while integrating capabilities of work team members. Supports scoping, scheduling, and budgeting at a project or major task level. Generates new ideas for proposals and business development opportunities while leading development of technical section of small to medium proposals. Demonstrates ability to acquire funding for self with programmatic impact at the sector and division level. Serves as a role model for quality, safety, and security.

Minimum Qualifications

BS/BA with 2 years of experience, MS/MA with 0-2 years of experience, or PhD with 0 year of experience

Preferred Qualifications

  • BS/BA with 5 years of experience, MS/MA with 3 years of experience, or PhD with 1 year of experience

  • Degree in Data Science, Computer Science or related field

  • Experience in data management and scientific application processing, including data warehousing, workflow development, and automation.

  • Proficiency with software version control systems (such as Git), REST API interfacing, and database platforms

  • Experience with software containerization

  • Experience with data curation processes, minting Data DOI’s, and applying Data Repository services

  • Experience with metadata definition standards, as well as metadata transfer and application processes including Dublin Core and OAI-PMH.
