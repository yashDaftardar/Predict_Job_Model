Job Description

The Quality Assurance Model Developer role will be part of the Quality Assurance (“QA”) team that support and reviews bank-wide models to forecast pre-provision net revenue (“PPNR”), estimate interest rates risk, or calculate Credit Valuation Adjustment. These models are used for a variety of use cases including Comprehensive Capital Analysis and Review (“CCAR”) stress testing. This role will work closely with Model Developers and senior management to ensure that modeling approaches are appropriate, robust, and well supported, and that model documentation, as well as other documentation, is adequate and accurate.

Responsibilities

  • Provide holistic review of model technical documentation for a variety of PPNR and treasury models (including time series models, derivatives modeling, state-transition models, etc.) by assessing the adequacy and thoroughness of the model documentation
  • Support for quantitative and qualitative modeling approaches by ensuring model developers adhere to Model Development Standards and
  • Implement peer-review process by providing guidance to developers utilizing internal model development standards, company policies, industry best practices, and supervisory guidance so that developers may improve their modeling efforts
  • Support integration efforts to help ensure consistency of approaches/assumptions for modeling efforts across development teams for Treasury, Mortgage, PPNR, and Investment Portfolio CCAR models
  • Perform benchmarking or attempt alternative approaches for select models
  • Provide guidance for developers on emerging best practices and become a thought leader within the Treasury Model Development team
  • Opportunity to transition model developer after having a wide perspective of the models covered and the development teams

Basic Qualifications
  • Bachelor's degree in a quantitative field, and five to eight years of experience in statistical modeling OR
  • Master's or PhD degree in a quantitative field, and less than five years of experience in statistical modeling

Preferred Skills/Experience
  • Prefer Master’s degree or PhD in a quantitative field (Economics, Statistics, Mathematics, Quantitative finance, Applied Statistics, MFE, or a similar field)
  • Proficiency with R and other statistical modeling software (SAS, Matlab, Python, etc.)
  • Experience with time series and/or forecasting models
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills (both the ability to write at the level of an academic journal and the ability to explain complex ideas in non-technical language)
  • Strong knowledge of financial and economic concepts
  • Considerable knowledge of financial analysis techniques and econometrics
  • Knowledge of best practices and stress testing requirements for CCAR and/or IRR and/or Derivatives modeling
