Job Description

We are seeking an exceptional hands-on technical leader with a proven track record to join the U.S. Bank Enterprise Analytics and Artificial Intelligence (EAA) group as a distinguished AI engineer. This team is responsible for advancing the applied use of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning at U.S. Bank and showcasing the potential for AI through early-stage solutions (e.g. “art of the possible”) and application of these solutions to U.S.Bank use cases.

Distinguished Engineers are recognized as experts in one or more domains within U.S. Bank and across the industry. They represent the senior technical experts within the organization and have a strong track record of growing and influencing others.

As a Distinguished Engineer in the AI research group, you will be responsible for researching and evaluating new and emerging developments in the fields of computer vision, graph neural networks, transformers and natural language processing. You will be responsible for modifying these advances to tailor to the needs of the business and integrating those methods to U.S.Bank use cases. You will be working with senior leaders, business translators, business units, and partners to influence the adoption in the business and maintaining technology roadmaps. In addition, you will mentor junior engineers and grow domain experts within the organization.

This is an executive-level individual contributor role representing U.S. Bank’s AI/ML capabilities in internal and external forums requiring technical acumen as well as the ability to simplify concepts for cross functional teams. The ideal candidate will have previously served in a technical leadership capacity in the use of AI/ML and have experience with advanced Neural Network modelling techniques, which would be leveraged for applications comprising time series data, graph data, computer vision and natural language processing.

Take on some of technology’s greatest challenges and make a significant impact to millions of users by leveraging the bank’s unique data assets to drive groundbreaking work in AI/ML that can be applied at massive scale across U.S. Bank lines of business.

Required Qualifications

  • Graduate degree (PhD strongly preferred) in a quantitative discipline, e.g. Engineering, Computer Science, Mathematics, Operations Research, Data Science, and 8+ years of experience in a highly quantitative position
  • 5+ years of experience with track record of success and proven outcomes in developing and operationalizing advanced machine learning and artificial intelligence in an applied industrial or corporate research setting
  • Ability to extrapolate emerging AI techniques and their applications to construct roadmaps for future applications. Ability to understand, explore, slice and dice structured and unstructured data sources and quickly identify the AI/ML techniques appropriate for analysis

Comprehensive knowledge of modern and cutting-edge AI and ML techniques, tools, and best practices

  • Ability to develop and debug in at least one of Python, Java, C, MATLAB. Proficient in git version control.

  • Experience in computer vision techniques that involve object classification, object detection and other techniques with different backbones such as Resnet, or mobile Nets OR

  • Experience in natural language processing techniques that use sequence to sequence, Transformers ( e.g. BERT, RoBERTa, GPT2 etc.) applied to NLP techniques such as entity recognition, classification, question answering, summarization

  • Ability to design, tune and evaluate key metrics of your model’s performance which are aligned with business goals
  • Proven track record in creating and managing proof-of-concept (POC) projects and successful knowledge transfer of POC concepts to production level implementations
  • Strong verbal/written communication and presentation skills, including an ability to effectively communicate with both business and technical teams

Preferred Qualifications

  • Experience with time series data, sequential data or graph data analysis for anomaly detection, classification and forecasting
  • Strong background in Mathematics and Statistics
  • Published research in areas of Machine Learning, Deep Learning or Reinforcement Learning at a major conference or journal or open-source contribution
  • Extensive experience with AI and ML software and computational packages (e.g., PyTorch, TensorFlow, mxnet) and python packages (scikit-learn, numpy, pandas, OpenCV)
  • Experience with reinforcement learning, convex optimization and graph neural networks is a plus
  • High enthusiasm, integrity, ingenuity, results-orientation, self-motivation, and resourcefulness in a fast-paced environment
