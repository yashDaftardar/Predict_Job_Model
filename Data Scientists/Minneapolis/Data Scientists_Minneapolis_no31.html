The Senior Data Analyst, Global Internal Audit will be responsible for planning, organizing and executing analytics related to internal audits throughout the Company (including IT, financial, operational, compliance, consulting and fraud analytics). In addition, this role will support finance BI function analytics related to planning, development and reporting.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Provide key analytics to GIA management and audit team to help inform and develop field strategies.
  • Consult with other BSC divisions to help provide better business models.
  • Partner with IT to develop meaningful business models and identify opportunities to help automate processes.
  • Develop solutions to a variety of complex problems of diverse scope and complexity whereby analysis of data requires evaluation of identifiable factors. Exercise judgment within generally defined practices and policies in selecting methods and techniques for obtaining solutions. Have a broad knowledge of a variety of alternatives and their impact on the various business units.
  • Work cross-functionally with Finance and IT, as well as the business divisions, to ensure effective workflow throughout the account activation process (for analytic tools like Tableau).
  • Support GIA management team in developing planning inputs based on key business trends and risk ratings related to Teammate data.
  • Advise the audit teams by providing risk based analysis, including modeling and management of all related audit data.
  • Help strategically align GIA’s requirements in the development, coordination and on-going maintenance of the audit related activities.
  • Design and create data visualizations (reports and dashboards) as required to support user stories.
  • Provide an understanding of business and data requirements, and iteratively design visualizations for initial concepts and prototypes that can be presented to customers for feedback.
  • Develop Tableau-based ad-hoc user interface(s) for end users.
  • Perform detailed data analysis (i.e., determine the structure, content, and quality of the data through examination of source systems and data samples).
  • Define, execute and interpret complex/nested SQL/Alteryx queries.
  • Establish standards and best practices for dashboards and related technologies for agreed-upon projects.
  • Manage a repository of re-usable data visualization templates and views.
  • Support activities to document, evolve, and communicate artifacts developed using Tableau.
  • Work with vendor(s) to maintain and implement a long term strategy for Tableau.
  • Administer Tableau Server in multiple environments, as needed.

An average work week relating to this position will include the following:

  • Audit Analytics standard – 10 Hours per week
  • Audit Analytics advanced – 5 Hours per week
  • Audit Analytics Improvement – 3 Hours per week
  • Tableau Teammate Dashboard – 2 Hours per week
  • Projects – 20 Hours per week (consulting, planning, development, and reporting)

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Mathematics, Economics, Information Systems and/or Accounting, computer science, or related degree.
  • Tableau Developer Certified
  • Alteryx Certified
  • SAP experience
  • Advanced skills related to Tableau, HANA, and Alteryx
  • Work on budget savings projects
  • Data Warehouse experience (HANA specific)
  • Experience with Tableau Server
  • Experience developing reports using Tableau
  • Experience developing dashboards using Tableau
  • Experience developing Alteryx workflows
  • Experience with HANA Studio
  • Experience with Alteryx Server

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience with health care data
  • Informatica experience
  • Experience with Tableau administration
