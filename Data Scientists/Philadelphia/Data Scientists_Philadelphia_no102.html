Assistant Vice President & Actuary (Job Number: 335432)

Description


Description:

Chubb is the world’s largest publicly traded property and casualty insurer. With operations in 54 countries, Chubb provides commercial and personal property and casualty insurance, personal accident and supplemental health insurance, reinsurance and life insurance to a diverse group of clients. The company is distinguished by its extensive product and service offerings, broad distribution capabilities, exceptional financial strength, underwriting excellence, superior claims handling expertise and local operations globally.

The Assistant Vice President & Actuary will work within the Major Casualty Actuarial group as part of the Chubb North America Actuarial team. The position will serve as Lead Actuary overseeing the portfolio management support for the Global Casualty Domestic (GCD) unit and will report to the Lead Actuary for Global Casualty. It involves managing one direct report. This position will have responsibility for GCD profit studies, pricing factor reviews, reporting and planning.

Qualified candidates should be credentialed actuaries with seven or more years of experience.

Duties & Responsibilities:

  • Actuarial support for the Annual GCD Profitability Studies.
  • Actuarial support for the review and implementation of pricing factor/model updates to GCD’s pricing systems.
  • Actuarial pricing support for the GCD Reserve reviews including XS of Agg monitor.
  • Actuarial support for GCD monthly, quarterly, and annual monitoring and reporting of results, including current accident year indications, AvE, and aggregate monitoring.
  • Actuarial support for GCD planning, including detailed trend and portfolio analysis.
  • Work on other projects as requested or identified.

Requirements:

  • BS degree or higher in Mathematics, Actuarial Science, Statistics, Finance, or a related field
  • ACAS; FCAS preferred
  • Proficiency in Excel; familiarity with SQL, Access, other MS Office applications a plus
  • Generally 7+ years’ experience with a property and casualty insurer or in a consulting role; prior pricing experience a plus
  • Ability to communicate results to underwriting/senior management
  • Ability to work effectively with/through a small staff of actuarial analysts and develop such
  • Ability to work independently with minimal supervision
