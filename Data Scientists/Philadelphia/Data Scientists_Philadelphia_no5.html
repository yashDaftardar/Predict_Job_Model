Sr. Data Analyst:

Job Summary:
The Penn Medicine Center for Health Care Innovation facilitates the rapid, disciplined development, testing and implementation of new strategies to reimagine health care delivery, improve patient outcomes, patient and clinician experience, and reduce the cost of care. We believe the best way to make big improvements to patient health and health care delivery is to experiment quickly at low cost, only scaling once we find high impact solutions. The Center provides an exciting and fast-paced opportunity for motivated, entrepreneurial individuals to effect systemic change at Penn Medicine and across the healthcare industry.

The Senior Data Analyst at the Center for Health Care Innovation's Acceleration Lab will be responsible for taking a leading role in providing high-level analysis of business or clinical data to assess clinical quality, service, and performance and produce decision support information. The Senior Data Analyst will provide guidance in data analysis and interpretation and exercise considerable discretion and independent judgment. This individual will also be responsible for aiding and mentoring other team members in technical and health care aspects of their positions and advancing efforts to improve data-related knowledge, skills, and resources for our team. The position requires a high degree of engagement and resourcefulness as well as outstanding communication and organizational skills.

Accountabilities:
  • Initiates and coordinates complex data analysis projects for the Center for Health Care Innovation. Determines project parameters, guidelines, and structure. Collaborates frequently with requestors to elicit data needs and collaborate on analytic design. Generates documents, manuscripts, and/or presentations summarizing and providing in-depth analyses of these projects.
  • Monitors, analyzes, and evaluates complex organizational data with statistical tools to identify trends, variances, and problem areas. Such analyses are used to support decisions that will guide Innovation Center projects by offering insights that can suggest ways to improve performance such as improved quality of care, improved patient safety/satisfaction, cost savings, increased revenue, etc.
  • Interacts and consults with the appropriate internal and external parties to ensure an understanding of factors that affect the data and possible sources of variation.
  • Ensures the integrity and accuracy of the data. Identifies any discrepancies in the data and in the event that data is not being captured correctly, works with the appropriate members of IT to resolve the issue and ensure that the data integrity is maintained.
  • Performs advanced analyses, including regression analyses, predictive analytics, and cost analyses.
  • Utilizes data visualization techniques to guide exploratory and complex analyses and develops data tools.
  • Manages and analyzes medical and pharmacy claims data with an understanding of the applicable data standards.
  • Develops computer programs, using either SAS, Stata, Python, or R, to merge data from multiple sources and perform advanced manipulation.
  • Demonstrates leadership through training other staff on data analysis tools and promoting a better understanding of health care data.
  • Works closely with the teams in order to guide data exploration and evaluation.
  • Collaborates with colleagues across Penn to share expertise and identify data and analytic resources available to the team.
  • Ensures compliance with internal and external data policies and procedures and maintains adequate documentation.
  • Reviews current demands and proposes and leads projects which will meet current and future needs of the team.
  • Manages competing requests and guides data prioritization activities in a challenging and dynamic environment.
  • Performs duties in accordance with Penn Medicine and entity values, policies, and procedures
  • Other duties as assigned to support the unit, department, entity, and health system organization
