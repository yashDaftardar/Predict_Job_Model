Overview:
JBT Corporation’s Automated Systems business is a leading global supplier of automated, self-guided vehicle systems to the automotive, food & beverage, hospital and consumer product manufacturing industries. With offices in the United States and Europe, we are one of the world’s leading producers of automated vehicle systems.

We have an immediate opportunity for an Applied Mathematician or Applied Physicist to join our R&D team in the Greater Philadelphia area.

We develop sophisticated algorithms to control Automated Guided Vehicles (AGVs), both at the vehicle level and at the fleet coordination level. We are continuing to expand the type of applications these vehicles service. As a result, we are continuing to improve and expand our navigation and routing algorithms. The successful candidate will be responsible for researching, analyzing, and developing new algorithms to improve the performance and extend the capabilities of AGVs and the fleet management system.

Responsibilities:

Responsibilities
  • Solve challenging Automated Guided Vehicle control and optimization problems
  • Solve challenging Automated Guided Vehicle navigation and routing problems
  • Solve challenging Automated Guided Vehicle modeling and system analysis problems
  • Be the primary resource to help others within the organization solve any higher level mathematical problem
  • Utilize specialized knowledge and skills to supplement and expedite other ongoing projects as neededQualification
Qualifications:

  • Minimum MS in Applied Mathematics or Applied Physics
  • 2-3 years of experience in commercial/industrial environment 5+ preferred.
  • Fluency in MATLAB, with experience in building, iterating, and validating algorithms and models from scratch
  • Knowledge of relational databases, such as SQL
  • Software development skills
  • Ability to convey higher level mathematical concepts to non-experts
  • Ability to develop problem definitions, models, and constraints from informal requirements and deal with ambiguity and competing objectives
  • Ability to travel up to 15% to debug algorithms in the field under real world conditions
