Job Description

Responsibilities:

  • Lead the statistical programming activities for multiple and/or large/complex Late Stage Drug and/or vaccine development projects for our Company's Biostatistics and Research Decision Sciences (BARDS) department

  • Develop and execute statistical analysis and report deliverables (e.g. safety and efficacy analysis database, tables, listings, and figures), the span of which range from individual clinical trials to world-wide regulatory application submissions and post marketing support.

  • Design and maintain statistical databases that supports multiple stakeholder groups which include clinical development, outcomes research, data management, clinical, regulatory and safety evaluation.

  • Act as a key collaborator with applying statistics and other project stakeholders in ensuring project plans are executed efficiently with timely and high quality deliverables and serve as the statistical programming point of contact and provide knowledge throughout the entire project lifecycle.

  • Assure deliverable quality and process compliance and deliverable development utilizing global and TA standards that optimize analysis and reporting.

  • Maintain and manage a project plan including resource forecasting, timelines, deliverables, safety data, monitoring committee reporting, plus 1-2 Interim Analysis, Clinical Study reports.

  • Serve as a member on departmental strategic initiative project teams such as new statistical computing platform evaluation and development

Education Requirement:

  • Must have a Master’s Degree in Statistics, Biostatistics, Clinical Research, or a closely related field and three (3) years experience in statistical programming in a clinical trial environment.

Required Experience:

  • Must have experience in SAS background/tools such as: SAS (SAS/Base, SAS/STAT, SAS/SQL, SAS/MACRO, SAS/GRAPH), R, operating system: UNIX and world-wide regulatory application submission deliverables such as ISS, ISE, Define and eCRT.

  • Thorough knowledge of SDTM/ADaM data models and CDISC standard, programming techniques (SAS), analytical ability, and sound professional judgment required.

Other Requirements:

  • Willing to work 40 hours per week – M-F: 9:00am - 5:00pm.

  • Must have proof of legal authority to work in the United States.
