Full Job Description

The University of California, Irvineâ€™s Program of Research in Translational Technology Enabling High Quality Care (ITEQC) is currently accepting applications for a research assistant to assist with a large-scale, statewide formative evaluation. Under the direction of the UCI Evaluation Principal Investigators (Dara Sorkin and Dana Mukamel), the applicant will support the design and implementation of a formative program evaluation for the Help@Hand Project. Help@Hand is a statewide collaborative innovation project to bring technology-based mental health solutions to the public mental health system in order to improve efficiency, impact, and reach.

The incumbent supports the efforts to develop and implement the UCI Formative Evaluation Plan for the CalMHSA Innovative Technology Suite. Key responsibilities include duties related to completing all program-related needs, including coordinating, scheduling, and traveling to meetings across the state of California; assisting in performing data collection, data entry, and data management activities; and assisting with report generating, report writing, and presentation and manuscript preparation. Additional duties include effectively interfacing with county leads, clinic sites, and other CalMHSA partners, maintaining evaluation-related documentation and databases, and providing assistance with any institutional, county, or state regulations. Position requires travel by air to distant areas of California and in personal car throughout various counties within Southern California.
