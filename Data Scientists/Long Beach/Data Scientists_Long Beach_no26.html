The Danaher Diagnostics Sepsis initiative was formed in 2017 with the aim to move the needle on sepsis and reduce mortality globally by bringing innovative technology solutions to market. The team is composed of individuals from the Danaher Diagnostic platform, Beckman Coulter, Cepheid, and Radiometer operating companies (OpCos) who work together on sepsis solutions.

Building from the success of the Sepsis initiative thus far, Danaher Diagnostics is creating a data analytics and science team to generate novel insights and real world evidence (RWE) from real world data (RWD) that contain lab results generated by our instruments linked to patient medical records.

This role is responsible for statistical analysis related to our RWD/RWE, drive technical execution, and help us create a best-in-class data science capability. You will work with various key individuals both inside and outside the organization to execute on our research initiatives.

If you are independent, self-motivated, and thrive in a goal-oriented and complex work environment - read on. This position can be based at one of our locations or remotely.

This role has the opportunity to
Collaborate with clinicians, researchers, biostatisticians, product managers, and engineers to define and clarify scientific research questions, develop statistical analysis plans, and help ensure data quality standards for observational research studies
Develop innovative solutions and approaches to turn raw data from various sources (EHR, DHR instruments) into novel insights.
Implement cutting-edge approaches for analyses and interpretation of large-scale proprietary and publicly available medical data.
Engage with and cultivate support from broad community of stakeholders, ranging from detailed technical reports to high-level executive summaries.
Develop visual analytics to enable self-service applications
Provide clear technical input, options, and directions to strategic decisions for research data strategy and best practices for real-world data utilization
Provide mentorship and training across the research organization toward novel approaches for statistical modeling, data mining, and/or machine learning

The essential requirements of the job include:
Bachelor’s degree or significant industry experience in a relevant field, such as Computer Science, Computational Biology, Statistics, etc. and 9 years of demonstrable research record as a data scientist analyzing large (1TB+) clinical healthcare data, including patient records from the EMR and lab results OR Master’s degree and 7+ years or Doctoral degree and 3+ years of same
Expertise in data science tools and languages such as Python and/or R, Linux, and version control
Knowledgeable of clinical data standards and data models most commonly used by the healthcare industry
10-20% travel at some point in the future when business travel is allowed

It would be a plus if you also possess experience with:
Setting up clinical protocols to collect RWD and generating RWE from RWD
Tools (e.g., Hadoop, Spark) and platforms (e.g., AWS, Azure) for large scale analytics (1TB+). Machine learning tools and packages (e.g., TensorFlow, Keras). Bioinformatics research using DNAnexus platform, including app and workflow development. Controlled-access data from public repositories, such as MIMIC-III, TCGA and the UK BioBank.
…and have a Graduate-level degree or Doctorate
