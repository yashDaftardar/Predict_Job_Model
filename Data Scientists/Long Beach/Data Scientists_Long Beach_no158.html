Full Job Description

This is an outstanding and unique opportunity for a Staff Statistical Programmer to join an early-stage product development group, Transcatheter Mitral & Tricuspid Therapies (TMTT)! TMTT is passionate about developing solutions for patients suffering from structural heart disease. This is a fast-paced dynamic, and diverse team and the individual in this position will play a pivotal role in the development of new therapies and devices, from concept through to human use.

You will develop, test, validate, document, maintain and execute programs in SAS, and serve as lead programmer. He/she will take primary role in review and validation of SAS programs created by more junior-level programmers. In addition, will ensure that all appropriate documentation is appropriately filled out and approved.

Job Functions:

  • Provide programming expertise on one or more clinical trials in programming, documentation, validation and review of summary tables, data listings, and graphs for inclusion in clinical reports or presentations in collaboration with project statistician
  • Lead in programming analysis datasets
  • Deliver expertise on programming ad hoc data requests with cross-collaboration with team members
  • Develop program specifications and design documents in partnership with project team
  • Ensure analysis data and programming code meet regulatory and company standards and are consistently structured to permit efficient programming, reporting, and review
  • Mentor, coach, and provide guidance to junior-level programmers
  • Collaborate with CDM to review draft CRFs (Case Report Form), databases and perform edit checks
  • Partner with clinical data management team to ensure that the databases is defined consistency across clinical trials
  • Participate and develop procedures (e.g., SOP development and standardization of output)
  • Assist in compiling technical documents for internal and external audits; Other duties assigned by Leadership

Basic Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's Degree in in Statistics Mathematics, Computer Science or related field and a minimum of 6 years experience in statistical analysis is required (Master’s degree requires 5 years experience; PhD requires 2 years experience)
  • Expertise in SAS and Windows operating systems required
  • Extensive understanding and knowledge of regulatory guidelines (e.g., GCP, ICH, FDA, ISO) relevant to in Pharmaceutical/Medical Device research setting

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Strong expertise in MS Office Suite including Word, PowerPoint, Access, and Excel
  • Excellent written and verbal communication skills and interpersonal relationship skills including negotiating and relationship management skills
  • Keeps a strong pulse on new developments in statistics and regulatory guidance
  • Experience using other software packages (e.g., R, S-Plus)
  • Excellent problem-solving, interpersonal, analytical and critical thinking skills
  • Strong leadership skills and ability to inspire change
  • Ability to provide training and mentorship to lower-level employees
  • Experience in facilitating change, including collaboration with management and executive stakeholders
  • Strict attention to detail
  • Ability to lead opposing priorities in a fast-paced environment
  • Ability to work in a team environment, including serving as consultant to management
  • Experience in interacting with suppliers, vendors and/or customers
