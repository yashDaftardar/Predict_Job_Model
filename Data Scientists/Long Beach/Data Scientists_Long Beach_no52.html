Full Job Description

The Southern California Association of Governments (SCAG) is seeking a skilled, energetic, and team-oriented professional for the position of Assistant Regional Planner, focusing on data science. This position seeks someone with an understanding of analytical and programming skills in the context of supporting complex regional planning issues.

The Modeling and Forecasting department is responsible for model software development and enhancement, model input setting and quality control, model run operation and monitoring, model output process and analysis, and model result summary. One of the tools developed and utilized in the Modeling and Forecasting department is the SCAG Scenario Planning Model (SPM). The SPM is a comprehensive web-based land use sketch planning tool used for scenario development, modeling, and data organization developed to support a collaborative and seamless local and regional plan development process. The primary purpose of SPM is to enhance the Region's ability to address complex issues and evolving challenges by providing better information about alternative future scenarios and by building an improved linkage between local and regional planning. For more information on SPM, please visit https://scag.ca.gov/scag-scenario-planning-model.

This position will work closely with senior department staff to support various tasks in the enhancement and operations of SCAG's Scenario Planning Model (SPM), including but not limited to: model input setting and quality control, model run operation and monitoring, model output process and analysis, and model result summary. This position will also support transportation and land use data collection and analysis, local outreach for technical assistance, and perform other duties assigned. This position provides a great opportunity for entry-level planners to develop and expand professional capacities through involvement in many innovative projects at SCAG.

WHO WE ARE:
Founded in 1965, the Southern California Association of Governments (SCAG) is a Joint Powers Authority under California state law, established as an association of local governments and agencies that voluntarily convene as a forum to address regional issues. Under federal law, The SCAG region encompasses six counties (Imperial, Los Angeles, Orange, Riverside, San Bernardino and Ventura) and 191 cities in an area covering more than 38,000 square miles. The agency develops long-range regional transportation plans including sustainable communities' strategy and growth forecast components, regional transportation improvement programs, regional housing needs allocations and a portion of the South Coast Air Quality management plans. Currently, the Regional Council consists of 86 members.
