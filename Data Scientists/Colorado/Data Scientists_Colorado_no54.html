Full Job Description

ERM is seeking a Data Scientist to join its Geospatial and Climate Services team, based in Washington-DC. The Data Scientist will work alongside specialists in geospatial analysis, remote sensing and climate science to help develop efficient workflows and tools for our quickly growing portfolio of work. Current workflows include identification of risk to infrastructure and assets from climate change, using satellites to monitor worksites, helping clients measure the carbon sink and stock potential of their landholdings, routing linear infrastructure through large sections of Africa and Latin America, assistance in siting of renewable energy assets, among others. The Data Scientist will join a team that works with most sectors, services and geographies to help our clients achieve their sustainable development goals through data-driven decision making. We leverage several “frontier technologies” in our workflows with a strong emphasis on providing integrated products of unique value. Several of these products are now being scaled for both internal and external use, and the Data Scientist will provide key skill sets in helping ERM to scale its products.

RESPONSIBILITIES:

  • Collecting data through means such as analyzing business results or by setting up and managing new studies
  • Transferring data into a new format to make it more appropriate for analysis
  • Creating new, experimental frameworks to collect data
  • Building tools to automate data collection
  • Searching through large data sets for usable information
  • Manipulate and analyze multi-dimensional and time series data
  • Creating reports and presentations for business uses
  • Correlating similar data to find actionable results
  • Automate remote sensing, geospatial, or climate science workflows
  • Develop cloud based tools to assist in workflow standardization and automation
  • Assist with the preparation of methodology sections for reports and proposals.
  • Work within, and guide others in working within quality/budget/schedule expectations and scope-specific assignments.
  • Complete work assignments in accordance with ERM's management system, format and quality standards, and guide others in doing likewise.

REQUIREMENTS:

  • 1-3 years of professional experience.
  • A bachelors or masters in computer science, math, software engineering, statistics, data science or related technical field.
  • Knowledge of programming languages like SQL, Python, R, and Scala. Experience with additional languages such as HTML/CSS, JavaScript, and/or C++ would be preferred
  • Experience using cloud-based solutions such as MS Azure and/or ESRI Image Server
  • Experience optimizing scripts and APIs for efficiency and performance
  • Background using multi-dimensional or time series data
  • Experience in working with gridded data (e.g. NetCDF)
  • Familiarity with business intelligence tools (e.g., Tableau, PowerBI, etc.)
  • Strong mathematics skills (e.g., statistics, algebra)
  • Advanced ability to perform exploratory data analysis
  • Experience with common data science toolkits
  • Working knowledge of statistics
  • Analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Experience with machine learning and AI
  • Familiarity with data management tools
  • Exceptional technical writing skills
  • Ability to communicate complex data in a simple, actionable way
  • Ability to visualize data in the most effective way possible for a given project or study
  • Ability to work independently and with team members from different backgrounds
  • Excellent attention to detail.
  • Ability to multi-task, maintain flexibility, travel internationally, and work independently with minimal supervision.
  • Demonstrated attributes to become a strong senior consultant – team player, eagerness to learn and grow, self-starter who takes initiative, versatile, and service-oriented mentality.
