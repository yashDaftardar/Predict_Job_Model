Full Job Description

Under limited supervision, reporting to the Senior Director of Data Analytics Services, the Senior Data Analyst serves as a team lead that supports the success of
major gift development efforts at the University of South Carolina (UofSC) by performing advanced analysis of annual, major and planned gift donors, prospects
and alumni. Serves as the primary point of contact for larger fundraising units to effectively translate reporting objectives and business requirements into technical
specifications, report design and code and utilize Tableau, QlikView, and/or Microsoft Power BI and various other applications to document, design, develop, test
and implement complex analysis, dashboards, extracts and other solutions in accordance with project plans, business requirements and specifications. Partners
closely with data analytic team members to help them understand the needs of the customer and desired requests. Assists the Senior Director with policies and
procedures for the team with emphasis on data strategies and methodologies. Works closely with the Senior Director to executes plans for continuous data quality
improvements that allows for the implementation of sophisticated BI and performance management solutions that supports the fundraising efforts individually, at Campus, Colleges, Unit level and within the University system. Proactively lead efforts to investigate, identify, and fulfill internal and external reporting needs by communicating, collaborating, consulting and advising a large variety of stakeholders and end-users across the UofSC system

Bachelor’s degree in Information Technology Systems or related field and 7 years experience in computer systems development and modifications; or equivalency.

Ability to work evenings and weekends when needed and travel overnight for professional development conferences. Must have a valid driver’s license.

Advanced technical and analytical skills with the ability to process, interpret and analyze constituent data using their broad knowledge base of various database data types. The ability to identify and predict trends;
communicate data results; provide in-depth and meaningful insight; and make appropriate recommendations based on results. The ability to quickly develop and maintain collegial working relationships and successfully work both independently and collaboratively as part of a team. Excellent attention to detail and outstanding verbal, written and interpersonal communication skills. Resourceful and creative with the ability to utilize strong reasoning and critical thinking skills to solve complex problems. Excellent time management and organizational skills with the ability to manage multiple ongoing tasks despite interruption. The flexibility to manage a large
volume of work by organizing and prioritizing tasks to meet shifting priorities and deadlines. A proven ability to exercise professionalism and discretion in working with confidential information. An adherence to industry guidelines, methodologies and code of ethics statements as defined by the Association for Professional Researchers in Advancement (APRA), the Association of Fundraising Professionals (AFP), the Council for Advancement and Support of Education (CASE), and other professional organizations and associations.
