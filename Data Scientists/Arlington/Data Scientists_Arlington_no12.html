Full Job Description

Position Purpose The candidate will be responsible for designing and executing analytical projects in accordance with the needs of city departments. Additional core responsibilities include assessment of data inventories, analyses of various data streams, and utilization of large data sets to determine unique insights to further business needs. The candidate will find innovative ways to combine fields of data that don’t naturally mesh together as well as conduct exploratory data analysis in order to navigate a dataset and come out with conclusions based on certain assumptions. The ideal candidate will support their department in transforming data as a key input in solving challenging real-world problems. By using available data and information, the Data Science Analyst will help the City become smarter, more efficient and effective in delivering services and programs to constituents. Applications must include a link to the candidate’s GitHub repository, if available. Note: multiple operating departments may be recruiting for this requisition. Please review the Supplementary Questionnaire for specific departmental requirements, such as program or field knowledge.

Essential Functions

1. Work as a departmental advisor on the entire data lifecycle - from data acquisition, quality control, processing, analysis, to visualization.
2. Contribute to all stages of data science and decision modeling projects, including problem formulation to solution development, that answer business questions, solve business needs, add business value and aid city staff to make informed strategic decisions.
3. Identify opportunities for leveraging data to drive program, service and department solutions.
4. Develop mechanisms to ingest, analyze, validate, normalize, and clean data.
5. Use unstructured data and data mining techniques to extract valuable information.
6. Retrieve and combine data from databases, websites, department systems, etc.
7. Develop tools to monitor and analyze model performance and data accuracy.
8. Collaborate with partners to produce and communicate insights to various levels of leadership.
9. Create data visualizations that clearly communicate trends and are accessible for non-technical audiences.
10. Provide mentorship to Data Analyst I position(s), if needed.
11. Contribute to the city’s Innovation Data Academy, Data Collaborative, and other citywide data initiatives, as needed.
12. Manage Community of Practice for departmental staff and support/lead data analytics trainings, as needed.

Minimum Qualifications

EDUCATION:


  • Bachelor's degree in a math, statistics, physics, computer science, engineering, or related field.
EXPERIENCE:
  • Five (5) years of experience in database management, operations, statistical analysis (which must have included one or more of the following machine learning frameworks):
    • Keras
    • Tensorflow
    • PyTorch
    • SQL Server
EQUIVALENCY(IES):
  • High School diploma plus nine (9) years of the required experience will meet the education and experience requirements.
  • An associate degree plus seven (7) years of the required experience will meet the education and experience requirements.
  • A bachelor’s degree or higher in a non-specified field plus seven (7) years of the required experience will meet the education and experience requirements.
  • A master’s degree or higher in a specified field plus three (3) years of the required experience will meet the education and experience requirements.
  • A current (PMP) certification plus three (3) years of the required experience will meet the education and experience requirements.
OTHER REQUIREMENT(S):
  • No felony or class A misdemeanor convictions.
  • No class B misdemeanor conviction within the last ten (10) years.
  • No family violence convictions.
  • Cannot currently be on deferred adjudication for any felony, class A misdemeanor or class B misdemeanor charge.
PREFERENCE(S):
  • Knowledge of Power BI, DAX, and Report Builder (SSRS)
  • Knowledge with programming, visualization, and statistical tools such as R, JMP, SAS, Tableau, Python, Perl, Java/ C++
  • Knowledge in SQL is required
  • Knowledge of other querying languages is a plus
  • Strong interpersonal skills and ease in building effective relationships with all levels of city staff and officials, as well as public stakeholders and citizens
  • Ability to use data science tools to approach a problem and ability to humanize data sets and communicate insights through that lens
  • Ability to learn new programming and data analysis techniques
