About This Position


**In Office or Remote**
The Manager of Statistical Programming is responsible for leadership and management of statistical programming personnel as well as overall direction and guidance to the statistical services activities of the company. Activities must meet schedules and budgets, adhere to relevant SOPs, and meet the statistical requirements of the company as well as the company’s clients. The Manager of Statistical Programming also may be responsible for being the primary point of contact for the company to clients, to the scientific community, and to regulatory agencies; the Manager of Statistical Programming may represent the company at client or scientific meetings.

RESPONSIBILITIES
1. Provide oversight and direction of statistical programing activities, including quality and timeliness of product deliverables, process improvement, resource allocation, and coordinating training and development of personnel. Ensuring the efficiency, quality and integrity of data reporting and project programming activities are executed properly.
a. Assist in developing and/or implementing strategies and policies for statistical services aligned with maximizing quality and reliability of deliverables at an optimized cost.
b. Assist in implementation of improved processes and management methods to ensure attainment of the company’s business plan for growth and profit as well as maximizing client satisfaction.
c. Manage Statistical Programming activities by directing and coordinating activities consistent with established goals, objectives, and policies of the company and manage expectations of clients.
d. Create or direct the production of SOPs related to statistical programming activities.
e. Assist in the preparation and execution of budgets and timelines.
f. Identify project priorities and project timeline goals and communicate priority related issues and timelines to programming teams.
g. Communicate accurate and timely status updates to other managers and other department team members.
h. Evaluate statistical programming processes, activities, and deliverables, including hardware and software necessary to accomplish activities and documentation of processes adequate to meet external auditor requirements.
i. Provide mentoring and guidance to direct reports and other employees, including coordinating training.
j. Provide assigned staff with performance goals, feedback, and improvement opportunities.
k. Conduct timely performance evaluations for all direct reports.
2. Perform Statistical Service activities as needed.
a. Review/draft protocol sections, including sample size calculations.
b. Prepare/QC or review statistical analysis plans.
c. Perform/QC or review statistical analysis.
d. Produce/QC or review tables, figures, listings (TLFs).
e. Create/QC or review creation of SAS datasets and associated documentation for internal use and for regulatory submission (e.g., CDISC).
f. Convert/QC or review conversion of data from external sources to datasets to be used internally.
3. Serve as primary contact/data services project lead and/or statistician for projects as needed.
a. In coordination with the associate director of statistics and/or project manager, manage client expectations, including the scope and timeline of statistical work for the project.
b. Participate in project meetings for projects where statistical services are contracted.
c. Prepare and present documents/reports/presentations for senior management, clients, regulatory authorities, and scientific community, as requested.
d. Participate in scientific and regulatory meetings as requested.
4. Serve as member of management teams.
5. Perform any other duties as assigned.

SKILLS
Master’s degree (M.S., M.A.), preferably in Statistics, or equivalent, At least 5 years relevant experience; At least 1 year in a management position
Master’s degree (M.S., M.A.), preferably in Statistics, or equivalent, At least 7 years relevant experience
  • Proficient in SAS.
  • Intermediate competency with standard office applications (Word, Excel, Acrobat).
  • Intermediate understanding of applied statistics.
  • Working knowledge of Good Clinical Practice (GCP) and International Council on Harmonization (ICH) guidelines.
  • In-depth knowledge of Clinical Data Interchange Standards Consortium (CDISC) regulatory requirements/related guidance documents.
Prior experience demonstrating strategic thinking, management, team leadership skills, and knowledge of pharmaceutical development, regulatory requirements/related guidance documents, Good Clinical Practice (GCP) and International Council for Harmonization (ICH) guidelines.

Licenses, Certificates, Registrations Needed
Membership in relevant professional associations and technical groups.
