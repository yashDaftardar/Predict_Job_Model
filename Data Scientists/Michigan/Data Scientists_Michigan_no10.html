DETROIT DEMOLITION DEPARTMENT

DATA SCIENTIST


The Data Scientist will support the City of Detroit Demolition Department through data analysis, manipulation, management/handling, quality control, mapping, and other tasks as needed. The Demolition Department’s Data Team utilizes various data management systems and techniques to track, analyze, and report on all activities related to the City of Detroit’s blight remediation program.

The individual performing this job must be extremely detail-oriented, with very good organizational and computer skills.


  • Reports to the Assistant Director – Field Operations

  • Direct report to a Field Superintendent


~ THIS IS A TEMPORARY ASSIGNMENT ~

Examples of Duties



  • Assist with data quality control by cleaning, joining, updating, and refining data sets
  • Work with various forms of geospatial data to create maps, geographically cluster demolition targets, and develop effective and efficient demolition strategies
  • Work in conjunction with other City departments and partner agencies to improve data-sharing techniques, processes, and transparency
  • Support procurement and project planning operations by generating documentation for bid solicitations, creating presentations, and performing other miscellaneous tasks as needed
  • Perform various data entry tasks, especially into the primary data management system Salesforce
  • Monitor all program data, both spatial and non, for inaccuracies or discrepancies
  • Use various statistical, spatial, or other methods to track and analyze demolition data from Salesforce
  • Generate reports and dashboards using demolition data
  • Share data with other City departments and partner agencies as directed
  • Generate standard operating procedures for data-related processes
  • Perform additional duties as directed

Minimum Qualifications

  • High School Diploma or GED
  • Bachelor’s Degree or Associate’s Degree with professional experience preferred
  • 1-3 years experience implementing data solutions including the extraction, processing, and transferring of data between various management systems with the use of critical thinking.


EQUIVALENCY

  • Equivalent combinations of education and experience that provide the required knowledge, skills, and abilities will be evaluated on an individual basis.


LICENSE, CERTIFICATION AND OTHER SPECIAL REQUIREMENTS:

  • Valid State of Michigan Operator License required
  • Employees in this class will be required to provide and operate a motor vehicle.

Supplemental Information



KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • Machine learning, model deployment, and cloud computing techniques to administer data solutions
  • Statistics, including regression analysis and parametric / non-parametric testing
  • Utilization of geospatial / non-geospatial technologies for data visualization and automation
  • GIS principles, proficiency in ArcGIS Software, experience using ArcGIS Developer tools and application development
  • Microsoft Suite, particularly Excel and Access
  • Government, planning and/or policy and a willingness to learn the complicated details of the demolition program
  • JavaScript, SQL, Python, Tableau, FME, RPA, and Salesforce preferred.


ABILITY TO:

  • Able to understand and fulfill oral and written instructions
  • Ability to work well in a fast-paced and demanding environment
  • Excellent time management skills and ability to multitask

  • Updated Resume
