PHARMACOGENOMICS DATA CURATOR AND DOCUMENTATION SPECIALIST

Phenomics Health Inc.’s Software Development Team seeks full-time Pharmacogenomics Data Curator and Documentation Specialist. Successful candidate must possess referenced knowledge, skills, and experience.

GENERAL DESCRIPTION

  • Full-time position
  • Phenomics Health Inc. (PHI) is a Plymouth/Ann Arbor Michigan based precision medicine company creating clinical treatment decision support products and services to provide medication recommendations, anticipate adverse drug reactions, drug-gene and drug-drug interactions, and improve patient outcomes.
  • The Pharmacogenomics Data Curator will work with the PHI software development and scientific teams to create the next generation of the Pharmacogenomics Informatics Platform (PIP), a University of Michigan discovered and PHI-developed software platform that is part of a larger clinical decision support system we call the Precision

Phenome Platform (P3).

  • The PIP will include a set of bioinformatics methods and a suite of pharmacogenomics-informed artificial intelligence/machine learning techniques used to analyze patient data from electronic health records, “multi- omics” biomedical data, pharmacogenomics, and pharmacology data.
  • The Pharmacogenomics Data Curator will create and maintain a PHI Data Lake and a PHI Data Warehouse composed of data and meta-data from a variety of bioinformatics, biomedical, clinical, pharmacology, and “multi- omics” sources. The Data Lake and Data Warehouses will include both structured, semi-structured, and unstructured data. Segments of the data lake and data warehouses will reside both “in the cloud” and in a VPN network accessible local repository for querying and processing.

RESPONSIBILITIES

OPERATIONS - GENERAL:

  • Acquire data from primary sources via the web, direct data download, use of source APIs or other mechanisms.
  • Store data in evolving PHI data lake and data warehouses for querying and processing.
  • Data clean-up, curation, and wrangling.
  • Merging and aggregating data from multiple sources.
  • Interpreting, converting, and formatting data. Data formatting is an important component of machine learning systems and is key to the success of the platforms.
  • Ensure data is backed-up and archived securely.
  • Data sources include government and academic research facilities as well as commercial entities. Commercial and open-source data licenses must align with PHI’s intended commercial use. The data curator will work with PHI personnel to ensure the licenses are compatible.
  • Create documentation describing the technical aspects of the pipeline and the data lake and data warehouses.
  • Storing, organizing, and maintaining data resulting from a report generating pipeline across local and cloud-based storage systems.

REGULATORY & COMPLIANCE:

  • It is important to recognize that the data will include protected patient information and therefore, data storage, transfer, and handling must comply with all federal and state HIPAA regulations and guidelines. Must insure privacy and security of patient-specific data.
  • The FDA has proposed a regulatory framework for artificial intelligence/machine learning-based “software as a medical device” (SaMD) systems. All use of AI/ML should comply with this evolving regulatory framework.

CLINICAL PILOTS:

  • As PHI launches its pharmacometabolomic clinical tools, this position will support design of clinical pilots and retrospective analyses with the Management team.

COMMUNICATION:

  • Provide frequent updates of progress in achieving milestones per project, depositing code, data, and documentation as work progresses.
  • Communicate regularly with software development, scientific teams and PHI management on a scheduled and ad hoc basis using email and Slack.

KNOWLEDGE / SKILLS / EXPERIENCE

  • Experience with API-based data retrieval including RESTful and SOAP-based APIs.
  • Experience with relational and non-relational databases and query languages such as SQL.
  • Experience in creating and building data lakes and data warehouses.
  • A working knowledge of cloud-based systems such as Amazon Web Services and the AWS suite of services used to run data lakes and data warehouses.
  • Minimum Bachelor’s degree in Information Science, or a related field or equivalent experience.
  • Preferred Masters of Information Science (MIS), MS in Bioinformatics, Biostatistics, or Computer Science; PharmD/Ph.D. in Pharmacy or Pharmacology.

PHYSICAL REQUIREMENTS
While performing the duties of this job, the employee is frequently required to do the following:

  • Operate equipment, read technical information, and/or use a keyboard.
  • Utilize hand and finger dexterity.
  • Coordinate multiple tasks simultaneously.
  • Collect, interpret, and/or analyze complex data and information.
  • Occasionally lift greater than 30 pounds.

WORK ENVIRONMENT
Work environment will be a laboratory/office setting. Offices are adjoined to the laboratory.
