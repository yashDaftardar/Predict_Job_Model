Full Job Description

NVIDIA’s invention of the GPU in 1999 sparked the growth of the PC gaming market, redefined modern computer graphics, and revolutionized parallel computing. More recently, GPU deep learning ignited modern AI — the next era of computing — with the GPU acting as the brain of computers, robots, and self-driving cars that can perceive and understand the world. Today, we are increasingly known as “the AI computing company.” We're looking to grow our company, and build our teams with the smartest people in the world. Join us at the forefront of technological advancement!

We are now looking for a Data Visualization Intern to join our RAPIDS team.

NVIDIA is hiring interns and engineers to scale up its RAPIDS accelerated analytics team. At NVIDIA, we are passionate about learning new things and using our new-found knowledge to change the world. If you have strong programming skills, a deep understanding of data visualization and graphics technologies, experience working with cloud and on-premise big data architectures, and love to be part of a dynamic team working on open source projects , we want you.

You and other engineers in this team will help advance NVIDIA's capacity to build and deploy industry defining solutions for a wide range of applications in, cyber security, web analytics, and healthcare.

What you’ll be doing:

  • Working with new and existing technologies to build data visualization and enterprise grade applications that showcase end to end GPU accelerated data science.

  • Developing frameworks for interactive data visualizations that utilize the capabilities of high performance GPU architectures.

  • Collaborating with distributed teams and a broad range of partners to advance the art of data analytics and data visualization.

What we need to see:

  • Pursuing BS, MS, or PhD, or equivalent experience in Computer Science or related field.

  • Experience with modern frameworks, stacks, and best practices, ideally in JavaScript and/or Python and/or C++ (yes we are working across all 3, but expertise in all is not required).

  • Strong skills in scripting tasks (Python, Bash, etc.) as well as comfort in using Linux and typical development tools (Git, Jupyter, etc.).

  • Ability to develop, document, and deploy web services and APIs (Node.js etc.).

  • Solid understanding of data structures, algorithms, and hardware memory.

  • Highly motivated team player who can work successfully with multi-functional teams, and coordinate effectively across organizational boundaries and geographies.

  • Strong communication and interpersonal skills are required along with the ability to work in a dynamic, product oriented team.

Ways to stand out from the crowd:

  • Experience using data analytics libraries (Pandas, NumPy, etc.).

  • Experience with GPU libraries (OpenGL, CUDA, WebGL, OpenCL etc.).

  • Experience with Node.js and server side web technology.

GPU computing is the most productive and pervasive platform for deep learning and AI. It begins with the most advanced GPUs and the systems and software we build on top of them. We integrate and optimize every deep learning framework. We work with the major technologies companies and support a broad range of Fortune 500 companies in their machine and deep learning needs.

We have some of the most brilliant and talented people in the world working with us, and our engineering teams are growing fast in some of the hottest state of the art fields: Deep Learning, Artificial Intelligence, and Autonomous Vehicles. Come help us build the state of the art.
