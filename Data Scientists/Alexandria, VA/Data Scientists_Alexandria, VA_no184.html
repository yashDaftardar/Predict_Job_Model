Job Details

Mary's Center is committed to promoting diversity, equity and inclusion (DEI) within our organization and the communities we serve. We embrace diversity of experiences, ideas, and individuals, and seek to bring a diverse group of candidates to the table.

The Senior Data Analyst work with other Data Management and Health Analytics (DMHA) team members to run and prepare reports for Mary’s Center initiatives including departmental requests, grant applications and standard reporting and ad hoc reporting. The Senior Data Analyst takes lead on complex data analytic projects and advises Data Analyst I on their queries, reports, and projects. The Senior Data Analyst is responsible for running routine quality and outcomes reports, responding to requests for data as well as grant reporting. This position requires advanced command of SQL coding and advanced Microsoft Excel skills, including creating and using complex formulas and manipulating large datasets.

Reportability

This position will report directly to the Director of Quality and Outcomes.

Essential Duties and Responsibilities

To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily.

  • Builds complex queries in SQL Server

  • Builds Tableau Dashboards

  • Analyzes large datasets and able to strategize methods of data collection

  • Trains Data Analyst I on SQL, Tableau and other data analytic tools

  • Supports and advises Data Analyst I on data management, data organization, data reporting and analysis

  • Supports and provide guidance, as necessary, to Data Analyst I on their SQL queries to ensure accurate data extraction and reporting

  • Present at select meetings on relevant data analyses, dashboards and data projects per Director of Quality and Outcomes’ direction

  • Co-leads and co-facilitates Data Management and Health Analytics (DMHA) team meetings

  • Leads and facilitates data support meetings with stakeholders

  • Assists other team members with their duties as necessary

  • Runs routine quality and outcomes reports by scheduled due dates

  • Collaborates with stakeholders to validate and analyze data to ensure accurate reporting

  • Prepares patient panels for chronic disease management and group care

  • Co-manages health maintenance reminder messages

  • As assigned and when necessary, coordinates with departments on ongoing data needs

  • Leads assigned grant related data management/maintenance

Qualifications

The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skills, and/or ability required. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.

Education and/or Experience – Undergraduate degree in social science research and evaluation field and four years’ experience working in a healthcare, public health or research/evaluation setting as a data analyst. Master’s degree in a related discipline preferred. Extensive experience with Microsoft Excel, including creating and using complex formulas and large datasets. Intermediate to advanced knowledge of Microsoft Office applications (Word and PowerPoint) is essential. Intermediate to advanced practical knowledge of SQL is a must. Has knowledge of and experience with integrating SQL queries with Tableau. Thorough understanding and working knowledge of patient/client confidentiality issues and HIPAA compliance/regulations. Individual must be detail-oriented and can coordinate multiple tasks at once.

Physical Demands – Regularly required to sit; frequently required to reach with hands and arms, walk, stoop, kneel, crouch, talk or hear; must be able to lift objects up to twenty-five (25) pounds.

Work Environment – Mostly in a typical office and/or home setting with quiet to moderate noise level. May be exposed to extreme cold, heat, and humidity due to outside weather conditions

In order to be considered for this position, you must submit a resume and cover letter, in addition to answering all questions included within the application.
