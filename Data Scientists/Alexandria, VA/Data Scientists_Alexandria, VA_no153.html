Firebird AST is seeking ORSAs/Data Scientists supporting the Defense Threat Reduction Agency (DTRA) Research and Development (DTRA-RD). Applicant will provide Technical Subject Matter Expertise (SME) support to the DTRA RD on all aspects of Counter Threat Network (CTN) technologies for current and anticipated programs and conduct outreach for new and innovative technologies.

Job Responsibilities

  • Support analysis of technological investment opportunities in CTN capability development
  • Conduct quantitative and statistical analysis to identify meaningful trends and answer requirements analysis
  • Use computer languages and development platforms to write scripts and develop innovative processes and tools to support evolving DTRA RD CTN mission requirements
  • Develop solutions to reduce known and previously unrecognized capability gaps to meet requirements
  • Develop solutions to reconnoiter, retrieve, clean, manipulate, and disseminate findings from large sets of structured and unstructured data
  • Develop task automation solutions to increase time savings, promote data standardization, promote data interoperability, and reduce human error
  • Map workflow processes and propose solutions for automation
  • Provide technical advice, feasibility analyses, and products necessary for implementation
    • Include conclusions and/or recommendations that are supported by data, analysis, and operational relevancy
  • Provide Operations Research Systems Analyst (ORSA) expertise to plan, coordinate and conduct analysis related to Line of Effort (LOE) modeling and assessment of mission activities.
  • Lead efforts in identifying, developing, and assessing alternative data sources for integration into the data collection efforts
  • Provide subject matter expertise in developing Measures of Performance (MOPs), Measures of Effectiveness (MOEs), and other analytical metrics to assess achievement of desired future force effects
  • Contribute in the operational environment by managing large quantities of data, developing strategies, developing metrics, and providing tactical, operational, and strategic recommendations for courses of action (COA) to leadership and decision-makers
  • Provide recommendations that are tactically, operationally, and strategically relevant
  • Improve overall staff abilities/effectiveness/efficiencies by sharing analytical and technical skills
  • Provide assessment frameworks to support quantitative and qualitative analysis to measure and support accomplishment capability exploration events
  • Support the team in generating and delivering reports and briefings on the data collected and the final analysis of the information with recommendations to leadership
  • Present organized analytical assessments to DTRA-RD program integrators and leaders
    • Identify potential initiatives to improve current efforts, take advantage of technological opportunities, improve the understanding and/or evaluation of complex issues
    • Recommend improvements to policy development, decision-making, management, or administration of the DTRA-RD contribution to the counter threat network mission
  • Assist in planning, coordinating, and directing CTN technical activities
    • Provide technical expertise in all CTN investments and maintain awareness of current scientific developments within the national and international technical communities in the following areas:
      • Computer Science
      • Machine Learning from sensor data
      • Artificial Intelligence
      • Robotics including Unmanned Ground, Aerial, and Water Borne Systems
      • Multi-sensor data exploitation
      • Multi-entity data correlation, aggregation, and dis-ambiguation
      • Traditional and modern signals SIGINT

EDUCATION

Research-based bachelor’s degree in in a technical field (Operations Research, Industrial/Systems Engineering, Systems Analysis, Computer Science, Systems Science & Mathematics, or related field AND five (5) years or more relevant experience.

REQUIRED

  • Demonstrated experience in Computer Science and Quantitative Methods background
  • Mastery in use of personal computers with extensive experience using Microsoft Office Suite and web-based applications
  • Expertise in:
  • Programming languages (e.g. Python, SQL)
  • Statistical Analysis languages (E.g. R)
  • Development environments (e.g. Atom, Anaconda Spyder, Jupyter Notebook, GitHub, R Studio)
    • ArcGIS
    • Palantir
    • Analyst Notebook (ANB)
    • Google Earth
  • Tableau or comparable tool
  • Microsoft Excel
  • Microsoft Word, PowerPoint, and Outlook

PREFERRED

  • Demonstrated experience in Defense Acquisition and Program Management
  • Academic and-or professional experience is desired in two or more of the following:
    • Computer Science
    • Machine learning from sensor data
    • Artificial intelligence
    • Robotics including Unmanned Aerial, Ground, and Water Borne Systems
    • Multi-sensor data exploitation
    • Sensor fusion
    • Traditional and modern signals SIGINT
  • Experience integrating human factors engineering assessments with capability development
  • Detailed knowledge of military and intelligence community (IC) organizations, operations, and decision-making processes
  • D. or D.Sc. from an ABET-accredited college or university in a technical field (Computer, Electrical, Materials, Mechanical, Aerospace, Systems Engineering, Computer Science, and Systems Science and Mathematics

Knowledge, Skills, And Attributes

  • Demonstrated knowledge with various techniques associated with data ingestion, data conditioning, identifying data anomalies and data aggregation
  • Demonstrated ability to evaluate the structure/type of data ingested into a model to determine the optimal statistical method to use to verify models and determine that algorithms are performing correctly.
  • Incorporate risk analysis into risk mitigation strategies that help identify less desirable outcomes of capability explorations, their probabilities, and the appropriate action for risk mitigation.
  • Demonstrated experience with leveraging existing business intelligence capabilities to take data/information and aggregate that data/information for senior leaders for use and decision making.
  • Ability to read, analyze, and interpret technical journals, reports, legal documents, etc.
  • Ability to prepare reports and presentations to company management and customer(s)
  • Demonstrated leadership ability, strategic and innovative thinker, and passion for the mission
  • Detail-oriented, self-starter with excellent communication and interpersonal skills
  • Ability to delegate implementation plans to subordinate members of group
  • Knowledge of traditional and non-traditional chemical warfare agents and improvised threats
  • Ability to quickly learn to use new IT systems and software

Other Requirements

  • Active / current DoD TS/SCI clearance required
