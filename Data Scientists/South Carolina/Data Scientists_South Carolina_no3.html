GENERAL RESPONSIBILITIES

In this challenging role, you will be supporting the IE - Instit Effectiveness department at Greenville Technical College. Duties may include any or all of the following.

Support institutional research department qualitative and quantitative data analysis, ad hoc reporting, development of statistical studies, and data presentation. Coordinate, administer, analyze, and report institutional surveys and other research activities.

Responsible for data extracts from GTC databases and systems for the purpose of analysis, compilation, and completing ad hoc reporting requests. Interpret data to monitor existing trends. Uses applicable computer software to manage statistical studies and projects. Creates charts and graphs for institutional reports and day-to-day presentation of college data. Analyze and interpret statistical data to provide results to institutional constituents.

Support the Business Analyst with extraction and analysis of data including report preparation for state and federal compliance, institutional initiatives, and other research requests.

Facilitate the development, administration, data entry, analysis and reporting of departmental and ad hoc survey requests. Develop Standard operating procedures for managing the administration and schedule for the college. Analyze and interpret the results of these surveys; prepare reports communicating the results of the data analysis.

Coordinate and administer institutional surveys including but not limited to CCSSE, SSI, SENSE. Analyze and interpret the results of these surveys; prepare reports communicating the results of the data analysis.

Facilitate the development, administration, analysis, and reporting of instructor and course analysis.

Perform special projects assigned by the Director of IR or the Vice President of Institutional Effectiveness.

MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS
Bachelor's degree plus two (2) years professional experience involving the collection, analysis, interpretation, and presentation of quantified data.

PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS
he employee must have exemplary interpersonal skills and the ability to interpret client needs by listening and understanding. In addition, employee must possess demonstrated ability to analyze and interpret data using inferential statistical techniques. Must have the ability to work well under strict deadlines with excellent organizational skills, manage multiple projects with limited supervision. Technical skills required include research design, data collection, data manipulation, qualitative and quantitative data analysis, survey development, and presentation of information. Knowledge of applicable computer software products, including Microsoft Office Suites, advanced MS Excel, Stata, SPSS, or R. Experience with qualitative software such as MAXQDA, NVivo or Qualtrics. Ability to analyze and interpret data using various statistical and mathematical techniques. Ability to predict trends and future action based on the analysis of collected information. Ability to prepare and present reports communicating the results of data analysis.
