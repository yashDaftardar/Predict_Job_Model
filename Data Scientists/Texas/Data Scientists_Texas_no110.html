Our team creates and advances a modular platform for automation, control, data acquisition, and reporting for use in automotive-related R&D test cells, mobile systems, and in-vehicle deployments.

Objectives of this Role:

  • Work with a team of data acquisition, instrumentation, and controls experts to perform software development and deployment to support data collection and test system control for a wide variety of test systems across a 300+ person laboratory
  • Demonstrate working knowledge of the following phases of system/software integration: Requirements, Gathering and Analysis, System Design, Development, Integration and Testing, Acceptance, System Installation, Deployment, Maintenance, Troubleshooting, and Data Security
  • Assist lead software architect in development of software tools to integrate general purpose data-collection and controls system with specific laboratory test needs

Daily and Monthly Responsibilities:

  • Manage projects, work in a team or alone to develop/support applications that are required to control test articles in an Engine Testing Laboratory; The working environment will include office as well as laboratory/field assignments
  • Capture user requirements and analyze user needs and software requirements to determine feasibility of design within time and cost constraints; ensure user and customer stakeholder satisfaction with the end product software application
  • Respond to suggestions for software modifications or enhancements, and work with customers and other technical staff in resolving software, hardware, operating system, or networking issues
  • Responsibilities may also include operating system and hardware configuration; provide technical assessments and recommend a best course of action, with justification, to other members of the development team/technical staff
  • Primary job function is located at SwRI main campus in San Antonio, Texas; travel required on an as-needed basis to conduct project work

Requirements:

  • Require a Bachelors with a 3.00 GPA in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, or Electrical Engineering
  • 2 years: LabVIEW development experience and expertise
  • NI Certified LabVIEW Developer (CLD) at a minimum and if none, must be willing and able to obtain CLD certification within 2 years of start of employment
  • A valid/clear driver's license is required
