Statistician

T3i, Inc.
San Antonio, TX
Job Description:
The T3i Statistician supports Air Force (AF) Special Warfare (SW) training and development at Lackland AFB, San Antonio, TX. SW operators are the only AF airmen specifically trained and equipped to primarily operate as surface combatants, where they conduct: Special Operations, Personnel Recovery, Joint Fires Integration, Tactical Airlift Operations, and Special Reconnaissance Operations.

The statistician is assigned to the SW Research Flight and provides professional knowledge and skills through consulting, evaluating, and analyzing all aspects of statistical application for proposed research projects. The statistician evaluates and analyzes the statistical validity of proposed research projects as directed by the Research Flight. Additionally, the statistician communicates and interprets statistical results to researchers to ensure that data is accurately reported and formally assess all areas of statistical considerations such as study design, statistical methodology, end point measures, reliability and validity of the instruments, stopping rule for safety monitoring, and data collection and data analysis.

Responsibilities:
  • Apply professional knowledge of statistics to include but not limited to, data management, collecting and interpreting data, data analysis, forecasting, trends, and predictions
  • Apply statistical theories, techniques, and methods to health data as well as military training data, such as course results, and provide recommendations and/or corrective actions to ensure adequate and appropriate statistical procedures are used in the protocols
  • Gather and interpret quantified information to include, but not limited to fitness events, medical encounters, academic performance during a course
  • Apply data management skills toward organizing, prepping and cleaning data for analysis
  • Apply statistical theory, principles, and techniques to a broad range of biomedical disciplines and contributes to planning/preparation of clinical and human performance (HP) research protocols
  • Communicate with immediate government leadership through point papers or power point presentation about any changes, strengths, or weaknesses as a result of any statistical findings
  • Conduct sample size estimation and statistical power analysis to determine the adequate number of subjects required for any given study
  • Perform statistical analysis for the data collected from approved research by use of SAS, Stata, R-suite, Python or Tableau
  • Develop and implement statistical methodology and procedures to support HP research programs
  • Maintain knowledge of study design and data analysis methods for a variety of study types including, descriptive studies, randomized controlled trials, meta-analyses and systematic reviews, large retrospective and prospective cohort studies and predictive validity investigations
  • Maintain professional knowledge of and skill in applying broad areas of classical and modern advanced actuarial science, operations, research, level knowledge of theory and techniques of applied statistical methods
  • Monitor trends in attrition and performance and submit to immediate government leadership
  • Regularly participate in staff, training, scheduling, and other meetings to support the program
  • Maintain strong communication (written and oral) with government and company leadership

Required Skills, Experience and Education:
  • Masters ’s degree from an accredited institution in statistics or biostatistics
  • Minimum of 2 years’ experience performing statistical / computational work
  • Pass the National Agency Check with Inquiries (NACI) background check
  • Possess excellent oral and written communication skill
  • Proficient with computers, e-mail, voicemail, texts, and mobile messaging, as well as MS Office applications including, Word, Excel, and PowerPoint
  • Experienced and proficient with statistical software, such as SAS, Python, R-suite, Tableau

Required Background Check: National Agency Check with Inquiries (NACI)
