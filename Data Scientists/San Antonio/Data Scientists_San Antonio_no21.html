Full Job Description

Apogee Solutions, a Woman Owned Small Business, is seeking a Modeling and Simulation Analyst to support the Capability Development Integration Directorate (CDID) located at the Medical Center of Excellence at Fort Sam Houston, TX. Qualified candidates must have an active DOD Secret clearance.

The Modeling and Simulation Analyst will provide operations research and system analysis support to enable qualitative and quantitative analysis and modeling of Army Health System concepts and capabilities. The Army conducts conceptual experimentation planning, coordination, and execution using a SECRET/FVEY enclave on the Defense Research and Engineering Network (DREN) called the Battle Lab Simulation Collaboration Environment (BLCSE). The DREN/BLCSE is a high capacity low latency network capability designed to support federated simulation events replicating Corps, Division, and Brigade Combat Team operations. The DREN/BLCSE integrates/connects all Army warfighting functions CDIDs and enables distributed event planning and coordination, and participation. The Medical Battle Lab participates in Joint, Army, and interagency events and experiments to analyze medical concepts, operational and organizational concepts, and identify materiel, equipment, training, and personnel requirements.


The Modeling and Simulation Analyst will:

  • Provide simulations technical, database, and software subject matter expertise to support participation in federated constructive simulation-based experiments to replicate medical operations and formations at echelon.
  • Support collaborative and planning efforts, distributed experimentation integration activities, and execution of distributed simulation events.
  • Participation in Initial Planning Conference (IPC), Mid-Planning Conference (MPC), TS Enclave, and Battlefield Synch/Set the Conditions Seminar, Mission Rehearsals, and Final Planning Conference (FPC) for all simulation-supported experimentation.
  • Support workgroups conducted to support the annual Futures and Concepts Center (FCC) for Army Futures Commandâ€™s Campaign of Learning and Experimentation-related events (Technical and Scenario Work Group, Orders Writing Conferences, etc.).
  • Build the associated medical database representing the appropriate task organization, capability, and environment for integration into federated events.
  • Provide OneSAF operator training to military role players in support of disturbed federated events.
  • Coordinate and synchronize constructive simulation support through the Sustainment CDID as directed or required by the Medical Battle Lab or FCC Force Integration Division.

Required Experience

  • Active DOD Secret clearance
  • Bachelor of Science in Science, Technology, Engineering, Math, or other related STEM field
  • Master of Science in Operations Research, Systems Engineering, or Applied Mathematics preferred
  • Certificate in Operations Research and Systems Analysis, Military Application Course (ORSA-MAC) or similar discipline degree
  • Technically proficient to support constructive computer-generated force simulations such as One Semi-Automated Force (OneSAF) software.
  • Strong understanding of calculus, data analysis, statistics, and probability and extensive experience with cost analysis, mathematical modeling, linear statistical modeling, simulation, and decision analysis
