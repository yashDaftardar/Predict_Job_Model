Summary

The Health Research Statistician for the Orlando VA Healthcare System Research and Development Service works in a staff capacity reporting directly to the Supervisory Research Health Science Specialist. The Health Research Statistician will be an integral member of the Service's professional staff, contributing to improvement of health care quality, clinical research and outcomes in the OVAHS. The incumbent actively collaborates with a diverse faculty and staff.


Responsibilities

The incumbent will have a wide range of responsibilities in providing data management and statistical expertise to all components of the OVAHS Research Service. The incumbent will be expected to work independently on a wide variety of projects currently on-going and to be developed. Incumbent communicates with the Principal Investigator (PI) and other research health scientists as well as analysts and other project's and administrative staff. The incumbent provides consultation to investigators and other research staff about VA data sources, available variables and data access. The incumbent identifies operational improvement issues and shortcomings and recommends remedial action to mitigate impact on the Research Service Investigators.

Duties may include but are not limited to;

  • Provides consultation to investigators and other research staff about VA data sources, available variables and data access.
  • Performs statistical analyses using a variety of software, such as SAS, SPSS, BASE, MACRO, SQL, PROCS, STAT, ODS), STATA and R and data management using PERL or Python among others.
  • Provides a high level of quantitative, mathematical, statistical and programing support for the OVAHS research studies, using statistical programs for complicated statistical modeling.
  • Performs database management using a variety of software, such as SQL, SPSS, BASE, and MACRO, among others.
  • Creates programming for and implements quality assurance checks on data, cleaning up and preparing data as necessary for analysis.
  • Conducts data analysis including, but not limited to, data cleaning and descriptive analysis, identification of limitations and threats to internal and external validity, data entry, verification of item content across a samples and surveys, analysis according to data analysis plan, and sample size calculations.
  • Defines statistical programming problems, plans statistical analysis, designs the statistical part of a research project, and chooses statistical analytical methods to be applied in solving the problems for VA or other federally or privately funded health services research projects.
  • Assures that OVAHCS Investigators are kept apprised of key changes in databases that might influence the analysis plans or outcomes of ongoing or future research.
  • Maintains a comprehensive, accessible and timely monitoring system of all databases in-house, with appropriate back-up copies/ systems, to assure ready access and retrieval for approved analyses.
  • Oversees the preparation of surveys and other data collection instruments and tools integral to OVAHCS research projects, including application of coding conventions, programming for skip patterns and subgroup analyses, assignment of scale scores and weights for both previously validated scales as well as new derived measures.
  • Provides OVAHCS Investigators with details of data collection methods used to populate VA databases.
  • Provides expert statistical consultation on issues of data entry, preliminary quality scoring and review and statistical analyses to ascertain missing data patterns.
  • Maintains and updates documentation of the techniques applied, retaining the raw data alongside the multiple imputation datasets for ongoing analyses.
  • Conducts standard and complex statistical analysis of single and multi-level OVAHS research studies, incorporating appropriate and state-of-the-art statistical methods.
  • Oversee the structure and function of statistical consultation and services for the researchers, make administrative decisions about the appropriate allocation of programing and analysis time to specific projects and develop strategic plans for future projects that meet the need of the OVAHS Research Service and its research and administrative staff.
  • Presents findings that may include text, graphs, charts and other narrative and pictorial representations and syntheses of the results of statistical analysis for OVAHCS research projects based on original or derived data.
