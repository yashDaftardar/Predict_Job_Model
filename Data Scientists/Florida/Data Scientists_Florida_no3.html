RSA Fraud Data Scientist (FDS)

About RSA:

RSA oﬀers mission-driven security solutions that provide organizations with a uniﬁed approach to managing digital risk that hinges on integrated visibility, automated insights and coordinated actions. RSA solutions are designed to eﬀectively detect and respond to advanced attacks; manage user access control; and reduce business risk, fraud and cybercrime. RSA protects millions of users around the world and helps more than 90 percent of the Fortune 500 companies, and every branch of the U.S. federal government, thrive and continuously adapt to transformational change. For more information, go to rsa.com.

The FDS responsibilities within an account may include analyzing fraud patterns, and emerging fraud threats around Online Banking, Mobile, eCommerce, and other channels, as well as consult customers around best practices to mitigate their fraud vulnerabilities, optimize their RSA Fraud Detection systems, and manage customer’s expectations throughout the implementation process of his / her recommendations.

This person primarily engages with the Fraud and Risk teams, as well as business and technical staff of the customer. He / She would work closely with RSA's Data Science and Product team, on research and best practices.

Primary responsibilities will include, but are not limited to:

  • Study the customer's specific fraud patterns, gather client requirements, understand their needs and manage clients' expectations.
  • Perform detailed analysis of the customer’s risk and fraud environment, including analysis of extensive production data to identify vulnerabilities and risk patterns.
  • Suggesting custom risk model developments to allow optimization of fraud detection, usability, operational constraints and risk policies based on the customer’s needs.
  • Function as a focal point for all fraud and risk management inquiries and contribute to the customer's understanding and knowledge of the various RSA fraud and risk management systems.
  • Consult and work with internal organizations to offer and define steps and processes in the implementation of change requests if required.
  • Travel – The position will include a potential to travel to RSA Customers, predominantly in the Americas.
  • Some outside-normal-hours calls / visits as necessary.
  • Proactive following-up on all issues and visits.
  • Monthly/Quarterly Reporting and/or graphical presentations as needed.

Minimum requirements for consideration:

  • BA / B.S.c –Statistics/ Industrial and Management Engeneering /Economy/ Computer science
  • Experienced in statistical and business analysis – 3 years
  • Excellent analytical skills
  • Excellent presentation skills
  • Service oriented, experience in a service providing position
  • Responsible, well organized, self-learning, can-and-will-do, team player attitude with a focus on solutions
  • Proficient in Excel – Must
  • High English proficiency, both written and oral – Must
  • Experience with SQL queries – Must
  • The following will be regarded as an advantage:
    • Project Management experience
    • Work experience with financial institutions’ fraud departments, specifically internet banking / finance systems
    • Fraud and Risk Management experience
    • Risk Management consulting
    • CISSP
    • Python (Data Science focused), Tableau or BI
    • Spanish oral and written proficiency
