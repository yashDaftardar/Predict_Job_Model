About The Energy Authority
The Energy Authority is a public power-owned, nonprofit corporation with offices in Jacksonville, Florida and Bellevue (Seattle), Washington. TEA provides public power utilities with access to advanced resources and technology systems so they can respond competitively in the changing energy markets. Through partnership with TEA, utilities benefit from an experienced organization that is singularly focused on deriving the maximum value of their assets from the market.


SUMMARY
The Energy Authority’s Connected Analytics group is looking for motivated interns who are interested to learn how data analytics is applied in the utility sector and who enjoy learning in a collaborative environment. The Connected Analytics team works with public power utility clients in more effectively operating their electricity networks and engaging with customers through data-driven insights from utilities’ smart metering systems. The Connected Analytics intern will provide support to the Data Science team within the Connected Analytics group. This position will provide the intern with opportunities to enhance technical, presentation and interpersonal skills. This position is for an initial 1-year term with an option to extend as skills match need.

ESSENTIAL DUTIES & RESPONSIBILITIES include the following. Other duties may be assigned.

  • Assist in the development of clean data-sets for analysis, including Advanced Metering Infrastructure (AMI) meta-data, buildings data, and other category or time-series data.
  • Apply Statistics, Mathematical and Software skills to support statistical and machine learning model development.
  • Produce spreadsheets and visualizations to evaluate and interpretmodel results.
  • Collaborate with Data Science team on documenting models, including data and software requirements, methodology, and process workflow.
  • Develop interactive PowerBI reports to understand, visualize, and transform data to support the Data Science team reporting to clients.
  • Develop PowerPoint presentations to support the Connected Analytics team presentations to clients.
  • Learn relevant skills using state-of-the-art tools including R, Databricks and Azure applied to distributed computing.
  • Attend necessary training including PowerBI, Data Analysis fundamentals, TEA onboarding.
  • As time permits, become familiar with the Public Power Sector and the fundamental nature of AMI operations.
  • As time permits, become familiar with TEA’s organization – front, middle, and back office - and functions.

EDUCATION and/or EXPERIENCE
A student actively enrolled at an accredited university, a 3.0 cumulative GPA (or better) in major field of study, and demonstrated proficiency in analytical studies. Preferred areas of study: Statistics, Mathematics, Computer Science, Engineering, or Economics.

MATHEMATICAL SKILLS
This position requires strong comprehension of applied mathematics, including concepts from calculus, linear algebra, probability and statistical theory, stochastic processes, quantitative finance, and optimization theory. It also requires the ability to identify and apply the appropriate mathematical concepts to practical problems.
