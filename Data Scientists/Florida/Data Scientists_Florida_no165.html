The brand was founded in 2006 and has continued to serve as a pioneer and thought leader in the vaping category. NJOY holds a contract with the National Institute of Drug Abuse (NIDA) to supply a standard research electronic cigarette for use in independent, government-funded clinical studies â€“ a project with enormous potential in the United States and across the world.

Overview:

The Public Health Statistician will be the quantitative in-house expert and practitioner in conducting and/or review of quantitative analysis of public health-related study data from clinical & population studies conducted by NJOY and/or 3rd party providers as well as data from other disciplines from time to time. The candidate will report to the Director of Health Impact and be a member of the Regulatory Affairs team.

Primary Responsibilities:

Manage research datasets, analyze relationships and data trends, assess descriptive and epidemiologic statistics, and contribute to developing analysis methodology for existing datasets as well as future public health assessments. Support the regulatory team in discovery statistical explorations on a weekly basis. Compare internal survey findings to national datasets. Generate and contribute to study reports at a standard suitable for submission to FDA and others as appropriate. Partner with the regulatory team in iterative statistical explorations to further quantify the impact of NJOY products.

Minimum Qualifications:

  • Expertise and experience in SPSS and SAS
  • Masters degree in statistics, public health biostatistics, or data analytics.
  • Demonstrated quantitative analysis capabilities.
  • Demonstrated familiarity with epidemiologic methods, study designs, & common measures of assessing impact
  • Ability to translate findings in clear data visualization schemes.
  • Ability to work positively in a team environment on shared goals.
  • Ease with working in a fast-paced environment
  • Ability to quickly apply statistical abilities to macro-datasets for statistical discovery inquiries
  • Attention to detail and timeline delivery.
  • Clear, concise written communication skills.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience analyzing US national population datasets such as, but not limited to, National Youth Tobacco Survey (NYTS), Population Assessment of Tobacco and Health (PATH) or other national population health survey data.
  • Experience with Gapminder or other data visualization schemes.
