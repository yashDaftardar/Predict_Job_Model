SUMMARY

The Viral and Rickettsial Disease Laboratory (VRDL) at the California Department of Public Health (CDPH) performs diagnosis for and research on viral pathogens to promote and protect the publicâ€™s health within communities throughout California. CDPH seeks motivated and experienced bioinformaticians and data scientists to join the team. Under the direction of the Research Scientist Manager, the Bioinformatics Data Scientist will provide analysis of whole genome sequencing data for SARS-CoV-2 and other pathogens of public health significance. As a member of our team, the data scientist will play a key role in the development and management of data and procedures working closely with laboratory staff.

ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS

  • Critically analyze genomic data to support public health surveillance and infectious disease outbreak investigations using conventional and innovative analysis tools
  • Assist in the development, management, and maintenance of large-scale genomic databases used to manage workflows of SARS-CoV-2 and other pathogens
  • Write code to modify genomic and phylogenetic analysis pipelines, and assess the efficiency and quality of these pipelines including developing quality control metrics and procedures
  • Ensure ongoing adherence to state and federal regulations pertaining to databases under their purview
  • Work with internal and external collaborators, including local public health laboratories and collaborating sequencing laboratories

REQUIRED JOB QUALIFICATIONS KNOWLEDGE/SKILLS/ABILITIES:

  • Proficiency in database technologies, especially SQL, including troubleshooting and design modifications
  • Experience with Linux environment and Unix/Linux utilities
  • Experience with NGS technologies and sequence analysis tools
  • Ability to script in shell, Python, or a similar language
  • Experience with analyzing and managing large datasets
  • Highly self-motivated and enjoy working as part of a multi-disciplinary team
  • Strong analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Able to present complex results, both verbally and in writing, to computational and non-computational audiences
  • Incumbent must be able to effectively communicate orally and in writing with public health and healthcare professionals, and other CDPH employees

Education/Experience

  • B.S. or higher degree in Data Science or a related field, such as bioinformatics, statistics, or computer science.
  • 2+ years working experience in disease-related bioinformatics and/or database management
  • Experience in Bioinformatics and Next Generation Sequence (NGS) analysis
  • Ability to build custom tools when needed

Certificates/Licenses/Clearances

  • None

Other Skills, Knowledge, and Abilities

  • Experience with cloud computing and maintenance of cloud-based infrastructure is preferred
  • Knowledge and/or experience in the fields of microbiology, virology, and molecular biology is preferred
  • Familiar with public genomic databases and resources (NCBI, GISAID, Ensembl, etc.)
  • Knowledge and understanding of elements of data governance
  • Knowledge of and skilled in the use of Microsoft Office products including Word, Excel, Access, Outlook, and PowerPoint
