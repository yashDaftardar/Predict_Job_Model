Who we are:
Chesterfield County is a thriving, affluent, suburban community located in the Richmond, Virginia Metropolitan Area. Centrally located within the state, Chesterfield is a vibrant community for business, education, recreation, and tourism. Chesterfield’s mission supports these core areas; and by joining the Chesterfield team, you can contribute to making our community the best place to live, work, learn, and play.

Chesterfield County government is comprised of over 50 different departments, as well as a separate school division and public utility division overseeing water and wastewater operations, employing over 12,000 staff and serving more than 350,000 citizens.

What you do and how you do it:
As a data science and analytics specialist, you’re naturally curious about what’s happening around you and willing to dig into the data, ask questions and seek out answers. You will be a key resource to support county administration and finance. Serving in this dynamic role, you will work collaboratively with team members to provide critical data support, with a focus on:


  • Strong analytical skills with the ability to collect, organize, analyze, and disseminate significant amounts of information with attention to detail and accuracy
  • Design, develop and implement systems and databases for geospatial data
  • Perform geospatial modeling and spatial analysis tools
  • Create maps and reports for internal and external customers
  • Interpret data and analyze results using statistical techniques
  • Develop and implement data analyses, data collection systems and other strategies that optimize statistical efficiency and quality
  • Acquire data from primary or secondary data sources and maintaining datasets
  • Perform other work as required

Here’s What You Need:
Bachelor’s degree in Statistics, Mathematics, Computer Science, Economics, Information Systems, or related field required; three years of experience in business intelligence (BI), GIS software and data management, statistical operations and analysis, data analysis and data system design; or an equivalent combination of training and experience.

The successful candidate will share a passion for local government, possess strong analytical, mathematical and critical thinking skills with attention to detail and organization; some knowledge of data administration including the ability to establish and maintain appropriate version control over work products; possess and maintain a foundational understanding of statistical analysis and reporting; possess the ability to understand and utilize geospatial data; possess strong interpersonal skills, work well with a dynamic team and thrive in a fast-paced environment.

What's in it for You:
If you are passionate about making a difference in the community, join our team. We offer a competitive compensation and benefit package, including the Virginia Retirement System defined benefit plan. We work hard to achieve our mission, but we also value work-life balance through paid-time off and flexible work schedules. We support professional development through career development plans, tuition reimbursement program, our award-winning learning and development programs, and opportunities for advancement within the organization.

This position is a part of an approved Career Development Plan (CDP) and offers career progression opportunities and salary incentives based on performance, qualifications, and experience.
