Full Job Description

Join the growing UW Health Applied Data Science team – who was recently featured in NEJM Catalyst for implementing predictive analytics within the healthcare system!

What You’ll Do:

  • Design and build software that uses machine learning solutions to improve clinical care, with a focus on deploying actionable solutions to doctors and nurses.
  • Write production-level code consistent with software engineering principles, methodologies, and best practices; includes version controls, code reviews, software design, evaluation, and code debugging and troubleshooting.
  • Work with data scientists and other roles on a cross-functional team to design and deploy solutions in production software and systems using agile principles and agile scrum methodologies.
  • Work closely with IT and analytics shared services teams to advance MLOps, machine learning capabilities, and data architecture.

Who You Are:

You are an applied data scientist or machine learning engineer who wants to take an active role at shaping the future of healthcare data science and the use of machine learning to improve everyday clinical care. You are excited about facing the challenges of using technology in new and improved ways to benefit healthcare.

  • Ability to write robust code in Python, R, Java, Scala, C++.
  • Skilled at version control technologies and practices including GitHub, Azure DevOps.
  • Knowledge of MLOps including machine learning best practices, design patterns, metrics, and machine learning technical debt.
  • Strong knowledge of software engineering principles, methodologies, and frameworks.
  • Understanding of public cloud technologies, services, and providers.
  • Skilled at writing Infrastructure as Code (IaC), such as Terraform, and building CI/CD Pipelines.
  • Strong understanding of machine learning frameworks (like Tensorflow, Keras, or PyTorch), libraries (like scikit-learn), neural networks, artificial intelligence or deep learning, reinforcement learning.

Nice to Have:

  • Skilled at working with high throughput data including streaming data like IoT or machine data.
  • Strong understanding of Microsoft Azure technologies, services, and providers.

As an organization, we are considered one of the most progressive and prominent medical centers in the country. As a city, Madison is consistently ranked as one of the Best Places to Live .

Education:
Minimum – Bachelor’s degree in Healthcare, Information Systems, Engineering, Business, Data Science, or related field (four years relevant work experience may be considered in lieu of educational requirement).
Preferred – Master’s degree or Doctorate in Healthcare, Information Systems, Engineering, Business, Data Science, or related field.


Work Experience:
Preferred –

  • Ten (10) years of relevant data analysis or analytics experience in healthcare (provider or payor).
  • Three (3) years of experience using Epic Chronicles, Epic Registries, Epic Clarity, and/or Epic Caboodle Data Warehouse.
  • Extensive project management experience, including agile methods.

Licenses and Certifications :

Preferred –

  • Epic certifications in any subject area
  • CPS, CFA

Required Skills, Knowledge, and Abilities

Delivering Analytic Solutions

  • Multiple successful large analytics initiatives where you had a significant role in conceiving, designing, developing, and implementing the initiative, including the appropriate organizational change management.
  • Consistent demonstrated success in data-driven change management including analyzing information, determining the “story” that the data tells, developing insights, and implementing change.
  • Advanced or expert knowledge and demonstrated successful delivery in multiple, preferably three (3) or more, analytics competencies. Domains include, but are not limited to: business intelligence, predictive analytics, text analytics, statistics, machine learning, analytic tools and platforms, data management, data architecture, data governance, analytics education/learning, ontology, metadata, business planning and development, cost accounting, financial modeling/analysis, quality measurement, clinical care, health outcomes, social determinants of health, and/or claims.
  • Very good analytical thinking, critical thinking, project management, and SQL competencies.
  • Very good subject matter expertise in one or more areas such as hospital operations, ambulatory operations, population health management, performance measure development, healthcare administration, patient satisfaction, strategic planning, market analysis, labor and productivity analytics, financial modeling, cost accounting, revenue cycle management, and/or survey design/development.
  • Demonstrates professional standards related to data access, data security, data sensitivity, and data confidentiality.

Communication and Leadership

  • Advance communication and leadership skills.
  • Demonstrated success where earned the trust of stakeholders, IS colleagues and IS leaders.

Teaching and Mentoring

  • Demonstrated experience coaching, teaching and mentoring colleagues on analytics skills in area of expertise.
  • Demonstrated experiencing developing and delivering training relevant to area of expertise.
