How will you make an impact? (role and responsibilities)

As a Data Science Business Analyst, you will be responsible for aligning technology solutions with business strategies, consulting with business/project owners to define business processes, develop technology concepts, and implement to user satisfaction. You will also:

  • Facilitate delivery of new reporting and analytic initiatives. Responsible for working with business owners and assessing requests for requests, potential new solutions for their needs, and/or opportunities based on requirements.

  • Act as liaison with business representatives, developers and analysts. Work with other co-workers in various units to ensure successful completion and rollout of change.

  • Communicate using both formal and informal methods across cross functional team throughout the development life cycle. Responsible for aligning communication deliverables and methods to each iteration.

  • Elicitation and creation of documentation to describe the requirements of the initiative; facilitating end user and developer communication and understanding.

  • Facilitate and coordinate tracking of all aspects of MiLo Labs initiatives. Ensure transparency and clarity of backlog, priorities, timelines, status, etc.

  • Facilitate preparedness for transition of initiative between the Data Science and Data Science Analyst teams.

Do you have what it takes? (requirements)

Education: Completion of a Bachelor’s Degree. Additional years of related work experience (2) may be substituted for education beyond an Associate Degree.

Experience: More than 3 years of recent and related experience performing complex work in area of specialization

Required Knowledge & Skills:

  • Knowledge of MGIC business units and the interrelationships thereof

  • Knowledge of MGIC’s business, products, technologies, business practices and direction

  • Understanding of concept and phases of SDLC; able to complete assigned project tasks per each corresponding SDLC phase

  • Understanding of project management principles and lifecycle

  • Understanding of how IT affects MGIC’s business processes

  • Understanding of IT infrastructure

  • Technical writing

  • Ability to express technical concepts in terms that are business understandable

  • Project management skills, experience in organizing, planning, and executing projects from the envisioning stage through to implementation

  • Ability to analyze project needs and determine resources needed

  • Agility in elicitation and communication methods
