General Overview

This role provides leadership within the Advanced Analytics group for coordinating our data science infrastructure and standards (ML Ops), and bringing on novel data science pipelines which involve new techniques, business areas, and/or data types and maturing them to become part of our Advanced Analytics portfolio.

ESSENTIAL RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Perform management responsibilities to include, but are not limited to: involved in hiring and termination decisions, coaching and development, rewards and recognition, performance management and staff productivity. Plan, organize, staff, direct and control the day-to-day operations of the department; develop and implement policies and programs as necessary; may have budgetary responsibility and authority.

  • Identify and evaluate scientific research opportunities within the enterprise and in partnership with external organizations. Lead Data Science support of those projects, including research design planning, analysis and publication/presentation. Develop Highmark Health capabilities related to the governance, administration and external communication of research findings.

  • Lead advanced Data Science efforts for Enterprise Analytics. This includes prioritization and oversight of projects conducted by Data Scientists, connect with executive customers to maintain relationships regarding current and upcoming work efforts. Responsible for coordinating data governance efforts for data science and scientific research. Management of data scientists is similar to management of managers so coordinating a group of individuals independently conducting their work is key.

  • Consult on future advanced analytics (e.g. machine learning, AI) and other innovative technical and scientific opportunities across business areas. Participate in internal think tanks and strategic initiatives to advance future capabilities across the enterprise.

  • Assist in coordination and act as liaison across analytic teams throughout Highmark Health and all of its affiliated organizations. This will include participation in data governance meetings and also involve pulling together work-groups or councils as appropriate to share analytics across all entities.

  • Other duties as assigned or requested.

Minimum Qualifications:

  • Doctorate Degree in Life Science field (Biology, Psychology, Neuroscience, etc.) or Other Field with Human Subjects Research Experience ( Substitution - Masters Degree and an additional six (6) years relevant and progressive experience in lieu of Doctorate)

  • 7 years of the following:

  • Scientific research

  • Peer-reviewed publications

  • Human subjects research

  • Advanced analytics (machine learning, AI, segmentation, image analysis, NLP, etc.)

  • IRB process (preferably involving multiple institutions)

Or

  • 7 years Healthcare Industry (including both payer and provider experience)

  • 5 years Management of Research Projects (first author or PI) or Management / Leadership

  • NIH Human Subjects Research or

  • Other equivalent Human Subjects Research certification

Preferred Qualifications :

  • 3 years Peer-review for professional journals (as reviewer)

  • Experience with production level data science systems (3 years)

  • Business consulting and innovation experience (3 years)

  • Change management (3 years)

Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities:

  • Analytical and Logical Reasoning/Thinking

  • EMR

  • Health insurance claims

  • Grant Writing (research grants, e.g. through NIH)

  • Management of scientists (PhD level)

  • Collaboration involving multiple institutions

  • Communication including both scientific and corporate settings

  • Peer-review

  • Training/teaching in technical areas

  • Legal & regulatory understanding (both scientific & clinical)
