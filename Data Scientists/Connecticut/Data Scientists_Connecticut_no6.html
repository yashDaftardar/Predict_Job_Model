Unilever is a leading multi-national “Fast Moving Consumer Goods” company, with world leading brands such as Lipton, Dove, Axe, Omo, Knorr, and Hellmans. Unilever has extensive R&D facilities around the globe with a mission to build brands through world-class innovation.

Skin Cleansing is a key category for Unilever that has a very ambitious agenda in several benefit territories such as hygiene and sustainability. Delivering Superior Brand Experiences is at the heart of the category, and R&D has a pivotal role to play in developing impactful global innovations and renovations that delight consumers and provide us with a source of competitive advantage. CTI (Consumer Technical Insights)-Claims is an integral part of R&D, sitting at the interface between product design and marketing. Our purpose is to guide our brand and category design partners to create products that deliver to the consumer needs, with product experiences that delight consumers and cue desired benefits.

Purpose of the Job

We are looking for a Data Scientist who will support our Consumer Technical Insight team in Skin Cleansing R&D with insights gained from analyzing data. The ideal candidate is adept at using large data sets to find opportunities for product optimization and using models to test the effectiveness of different courses of action. They must have strong background in statistical modelling, experience using a variety of data mining/data analysis methods, using a variety of data tools, building and implementing models, using/creating algorithms and creating/running simulations. They must be comfortable working with a wide range of stakeholders and functional teams. The right candidate will have a passion for discovering solutions hidden in large data sets and working with stakeholders to improve business outcomes.

Who You Are & What You’ll Do

  • Engagement and collaboration with globally deployed, multi-disciplinary teams to design and implement efficient, well-focused, statistically fit-for-purpose studies, analyse, visualize and interpret data, build and validate predictive models and simulations

  • Work in inter- disciplinary teams that will include product formulators, process/packaging engineers, clinicians, measurement scientists, bio-informaticians, claims experts, and other data scientists, both internal to Unilever and external partners.

  • Provision of data science/statistics expertise across a range of R&D disciplines to define and execute analytics and modelling approaches (e.g. trained panel and consumer studies, investigating key attributes and product preferences)

  • Develop custom data models and algorithms to apply to relevant data sets.

  • Responsibility for the quality and defensibility of statistical analyses, models and data interpretation.

  • Coordinate with different functional teams to implement models and monitor outcomes

What You’ll Need To Succeed

  • Master’s degree or equivalent experience in Statistics, Mathematics or Data Science/Computer Science.

  • 1+ years of relevant experience

  • Experience in FMCG/Retail/Pharmaceutical industries is a plus.

Technical Skills:

  • Technical depth, operational breadth and demonstrated fluency in applying statistics/data analytics in scientific research.

  • Hands-on technical expertise in data management and visualization

  • Strong technical background in multivariate statistical methods e.g. PCA, PLS and machine learning methods and designing simulations (knowledge of Bayesian Methods a plus).

  • Good understanding of the underlying principles/best practices to build and validate predictive models.

  • Experience working with and creating data architectures.

  • Knowledge of a variety of machine learning techniques (clustering, decision tree learning, artificial neural networks, etc.) and their real-world advantages/drawbacks.

  • Strong expertise in Design and Analysis of Experiments (DOE)

  • Hands-on experience with professional statistical software such as SAS, R and JMP. Familiarity with Azure Platform and computer programming skills will be considered a plus.

  • Experience using statistical computer languages (R, Python, SLQ, etc.) to manipulate data and draw insights from large data sets.

Soft Skills:

  • Strong problem-solving skills with focus on business delivery

  • Strong interpersonal skills; strong oral and written communication skills.

  • Ability to collaborate and consult effectively in global teams across time-zones and cultures

  • A drive to learn and master new technologies and techniques

  • Ability to work with uncertainty and to create and manage change
