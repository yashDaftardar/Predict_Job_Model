About the Connecticut Housing Finance Authority:

Connecticut Housing Finance Authority is a self-funded, quasi-public organization. Its mission is to alleviate the shortage of housing for low-to moderate-income families and persons in this state and, when appropriate, to promote or maintain the economic development of this state through employer-assisted housing efforts.

About the Position:

This position is responsible to support the marketing and research functions by creating data-driven research that informs business and policy development as well as program development and evaluation through research, data collection analysis and recommendation. Develop and conduct formal research methodologies and procedures; undertake research activities associated with housing issues and trends; collect, analyze, evaluate and report information, conclusions, policy and/or programmatic implications; assist on comprehensive and complex research assignments; develop and/or assist in the development of databases for access by internal and external users; provide quality assurance of imported data; support initiatives for data integrity and normalization; perform complex statistical analysis; prepare comprehensive narrative and statistical reports; design and implement surveys; may prepare special reports for agency officials in response to inquiries from legislature or the public; perform related duties as required.

Job Duties and Responsibilities:

Participate in Authority inter-departmental teams in program evaluation and improvement including written analysis, summaries and recommendations associated with administration, policy and regulation, as applicable. Use data sources to identify programmatic needs and assist in the development of programmatic performance measures and tools. Assist with program assessments ensuring programmatic goals are well documented. Prepare, collect, analyze and prepare data, compile and write ad-hoc reports for CEO-Executive Director, internal departments and the Authority’s Board of Directors.

Use CHFA’s housing database to develop standard reports. Provide data analysis for studies and maintain assigned projects. Identify external data to supplement reporting. Leverage data insight to inform marketing and outreach strategies. Support the development and implementation of agency plans.

Participate in strategic planning with regards to program development. Research internal and external data associated with affordable housing and commercial financing; prepare comparative review of national models in affordable housing program development specifically in Housing Finance Agencies. Analyze and report results conclusions, policy and/or programmatic implications.

Prepare and analyze data associated with the planning of new funding, funding strategies and initiatives; provide supporting data for the implementation of agency plans; housing needs study(s) as well as new and existing agency policy for CHFA.

Minimum Qualifications Required:

Requires a BS/BA in public policy/administration, statistics, economics, or research-based field; requires a minimum of seven years’ related experience in the public, private or academic sectors in the areas of public policy, research, banking, mortgage lending and housing development or an equivalent combination of education and experience.

Critical thinking and problem solving; considerable knowledge of research methods and techniques; considerable knowledge of principles, concepts and methods of data collection and management as well as statistical analysis; knowledge of agency related policies and procedures related to research; considerable oral and written communication skills; strong interpersonal and facilitation skills; considerable ability to analyze data; ability to develop programs and use prepackaged computer programs to perform data analysis.

Must be able to work with a wide variety of people and departments and communicate clearly with them. Must be able to take initiative in planning and scheduling work and have the ability to work independently. Must be able to multi-task on a wide range of projects. Proficient in Microsoft Office™ products i.e., Word™, Excel™, PowerPoint™, Outlook™ and Publisher™.

Salary: Depending on experience

CHFA is currently operating in a remote teleworking environment due to the pandemic. Future options for teleworking in this position may be available.
