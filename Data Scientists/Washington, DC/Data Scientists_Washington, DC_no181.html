A little bit about our team:

The Research + Analysis department works to influence WMG’s strategy and optimize day-to-day operations through data and insights. This position sits within R+A’s USA team, working with local affiliates/labels, international teams, and Business Development to understand the trends and dynamics driving major streaming services from the perspective of the fans and listeners who enjoy WMG music . Our goal for this role is to create expert level insight into audience behavior, by helping to establish best practices for data which captures music listening preferences, to gauge the impact of marketing initiatives and campaigns on our listeners, and to comprehensively understand and predict listener behavior.

Why this could be your next big break:

Data is the lifeblood of today’s record business, and in this role you’ll get a chance to apply your creativity and problem-solving skills to some massive amounts of data to help our labels and artists connect with streaming fans. Because the R+A team works closely with labels, you will have the opportunity to see the inner workings and decision-making of an international record label - and the chance to influence those decisions with analysis. We’re a collaborative team that loves learning new things, sharing ideas, thinking about the future of the music industry – and helping our company get there.

Here you’ll get to:

  • Develop methodology for sound/music information.
  • Help create methodological suite for understanding musical content and the audiences who enjoy them: aural features, audience context and propensity
  • Develop new forecasting and audience targeting procedures.
  • Communicate findings and insights to key stakeholders, help establish best practices, and to guide analysis into action and results.
  • Transform data into stories via tailor-made presentations.
  • Handle various ad hoc requests and projects.
  • Interact with data engineering teams to identify the data needs for the mathematical models.

Rockstar candidates will possess:

  • PhD in Engineering, Statistics/Physics, Electrical Engineering, Music Information, Mathematics or similar
  • Masters Degree with 2-5 years of work experience or commensurate in Engineering, Statistics/Physics, Electrical Engineering, Music Information, Mathematics or similar
  • Proficiency in R, Python, SQL, MATLAB
  • Fluency in Spanish
  • A results-oriented mindset with strong analytical skills and problem-solving ability
  • Strong interpersonal skills and ability to communicate clearly and effectively (orally and in writing)
  • Strong organizational skills with ability to work on multiple projects

It would be music to our ears if you also had:

  • Hands-on experience with aural data; hands-on experience creating musical features from aural information
