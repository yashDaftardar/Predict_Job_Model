Full Job Description

Overview:
LMI is a government consulting firm, dedicated to advancing the management of the government. Federal government departments and agencies sponsor work for this position including the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) and the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) within HHS. Work will be primarily conducted at LMI headquarters in Tysons, VA and at the client site in Maryland. Travel will vary, dependent on contract requirements.

The successful applicant will serve as the point of contact to the client, should be comfortable leading and managing teams of varying sizes, communicate with various internal and external stakeholders, understanding client requirements, oversee and monitor the program budget, creating project schedules, developing a project management plan, and maintaining a risk register. All work will be performed in a collaborative environment, and leadership and communication skills are vital.
Responsibilities:
Key Responsibilities:
  • Must be proficient in information systems, statistics, and the use of statistical software—such as SAS or STATA. This SME will lead the MPMG data definition and issuer performance reporting task.
  • Possess knowledge of various database management systems in order to input, extract or manipulate information, specific samples include: Operational Report (Tableau), QHP Issuer Performance Quality Improvement report and QHP issuer compliance and risk monitoring report.
  • Build data visualization solution strategy partnering with clients and other stakeholders
  • Use a variety of data visualization tools and techniques to develop interactive dashboard report presenting summary of findings and conclusions
  • Provide data and insights through dashboard, reporting building and ad hoc analysis
  • Use multiple data sources to build compliance risk modeling to identify at-risk issuers
  • Quantify QHP issuers’ performance through use of advanced analytical approach and algorithms.
  • Support survey vendor data submission and validation, quality control and data management.
  • Developing agendas and facilitating internal and external meetings and conference calls involving clients, experts and stakeholders

Further responsibilities may include:
  • Additional familiarity and experience with R and Python is considered a plus.
  • Establish and build working relationships with internal and external management and team members;
  • Help lead junior staff during program reviews of data and provide feedback on work performed to management.
  • Must show initiative to execute routine and ad hoc tasks with minimal supervision or monitoring.
Qualifications:
Qualifications:

An ideal candidate will have the following: strong data analytics, visualization and data management skills, knowledge and expertise of federal health regulations; strong analytical skills, strong communication skills, both verbal and written; and strong problem-solving and logical reasoning skills.
  • Experience: At least 5 years of experience related to the two SME areas noted above, or a combination of education and equivalent work experience. SMEs have a higher level of subject matter expertise and require less supervision than Analysts.
  • Education: A Master’s degree in public policy, information management, statistics, business, or related fields.
