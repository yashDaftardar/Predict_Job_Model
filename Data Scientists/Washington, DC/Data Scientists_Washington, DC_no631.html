The office leads an effort to bring its enterprise services into compliance with Federal (OPM and IC) data standards and formats. TechFlow is seeking Data Scientist for analytical support to review, compare and make recommendations for action (data cleansing, transformation) as well as support data migration planning and coordination efforts. The Data Scientist also seeks to build a skills inventory, which will eventually integrate with other customer systems, capturing both position skill requirements and competency levels as well as personnel skill and competencies.

A. Work Requirements:

  • Perform a Gap Analysis of the current data set and planned subtasks.
  • Analyze the current state, create a baseline and identify the gaps in what is currently known and what needs to be known.
  • Develop an action plan to mitigate the gaps.
  • Work closely with system engineers, database administrators, and project managers.
  • Review Sponsor analysis of data sets.
  • Compare data sets against information contained in Sponsor systems.
  • Review mapping of data fields to OPM data standard fields and any indicated structural changes to any fields.
  • Review data cleansing status.
  • Preview mock cleansing to show previous and desired end states.
  • Establish a repeatable process to periodically “scrape” and collect skills from a collection of Sponsor performance reviews.
  • Follow guidance and policies regarding the collection of personally identifiable information (PII).
  • Create a controlled access collection of performance reviews (PII removed).
  • Create repositories to temporarily store collected skills and competencies.
  • Use approved technologies when creating the repository.
  • Consider account ease of migration into a more permanent system when creating the repository.
  • Create a data migration plan to ingest collected skills and competencies into systems.
  • Scrape, gather, and perform analysis of skill sets from open source, commercially available, and existing Sponsor databases and applications for addition to the Skills repository.
  • Scrape or gather and perform analysis of onboarding data for addition to the Skill and Competencies Repository.
  • Review and analysis of all service competency data for addition to the Skill and Competencies Repository.
  • Review and analyze competency data from open source, commercially available, and existing databases and applications for addition to the Skill and Competencies Repository.
  • Evaluate current Chatbot & Machine Learning (M/L), Artificial Intelligence offerings, and integration methods and provide a report on opportunities to integrate with systems.
  • Evaluate and assess planned system restructuring and its data integration impacts with a new external service provider.
  • Work with customer team supporting the system restructuring to understand data standards required and effect on OPM standardization efforts.

  • General equivalency guidelines for education, certifications, and experience are provided below; however, these guidelines do not immediately qualify someone at a particular level. Skill levels for an individual are based upon a combination of education, training, and experience, e.g., someone may have 20 years of professional experience; however, they may only be qualified as Full Performance based upon the expertise needed to perform the work within that category. General professional years of experience may be substituted for specialized years of experience at a ratio of three (3) years of general experience within an occupation for one (1) year of specialized experience. Relevant certifications will be considered equivalent to three (3) months of specialized experience. An Associate’s degree in Computer Science, Information Systems, Engineering, Business, or a scientific or technical discipline related to the specific skill will be considered equivalent to eighteen (18) months of specialized experience. A Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, Information Systems, Engineering, Business, or a scientific or technical discipline related to the specific skill will be considered equivalent to three (3) years specialized experience (total equivalency is not cumulative when combined with an Associate’s degree). A Master’s degree in Computer Science, Information Systems, Engineering, Business, or a scientific or technical discipline related to the specific skill will be considered equivalent to two (2) years specialized experience (up to a total of five (5) years when combined with a Bachelor’s). A Doctorate in Computer Science, Information Systems, Engineering, Business, or a scientific or technical discipline related to the specific skill will be considered equivalent to two (2) years specialized experience (up to a total of seven (7) years when combined with a Master’s and a Bachelor’s).
