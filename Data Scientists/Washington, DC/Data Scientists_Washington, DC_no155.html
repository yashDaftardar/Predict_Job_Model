Postdoc Position in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning (AI/ML) for Medical Data Analysis

The Division of Imaging, Diagnostics & Software Reliability (DIDSR) is one of four divisions within the Office of Science and Engineering Laboratories (OSEL). Our mission is to accelerate patient access to innovative, safe and effective medical devices through best-in-the-world regulatory science. DIDSR’s team of world-renowned scientists undertake the highest quality science to provide FDA and medical device manufacturers with the best methods, tools and expertise to ensure readiness for emerging and innovative medical technologies. Furthermore, we develop appropriate evaluation strategies and testing standards, create accessible and understandable public health information, and deliver timely and accurate decisions for products across their life cycle.

DIDSR’s research is mainly targeted at medical imaging devices, and spans across a wide range of multi-disciplinary topics: from evaluation methods for medical displays and development of statistical methods for design of clinical trials to augmented/virtual reality and Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning (AI/ML) algorithms in medical imaging. The technical backgrounds of our staff include physics, electrical and biomedical engineering, mathematics and statistics, computer science, and radiology.

We currently have a postdoc position with competitive compensation available immediately for a motivated candidate in the areas of AI/ML. The candidates will join our multi-disciplinary team of scientists and train in a highly collaborative environment by conducting cutting edge research on AI/ML algorithms.

Examples of Previous Projects

  • Development of 3D network architectures for a computer-aided detection system (CADe) for lung nodules in chest CT
  • Generative adversarial networks (GANs) for creation of artificial training data for different modalities (time-series and imaging)
  • Test data re-use, differential privacy, AI assessment with an imperfect reference standard, quantification of information gain from multiple access to a test data set.
  • Development of quantitative imaging biomarkers
  • Evaluation of deep-learning based image denoising algorithms
  • Assessment methodologies and metrics for deep-learning based magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) reconstruction and analysis of effect on diagnostic or quantitative imaging algorithms
  • Weakly supervised and self-supervised training for medical imaging

Major Responsibilities

  • Develop deep learning algorithms for imaging / time series data analysis that are robust to variations in image quality / characteristics.
  • Develop metrics and assessment methods for characterizing robustness of AI/ML technologies.
  • Write clean reproducible code that runs on our multi-GPU clusters.
  • Lead / contribute to submissions to major conferences / journals and progress reports.

Basic Qualifications:

  • PhD degree in electrical, computer, biomedical engineering, computer science, or another relevant field of study with experience applying AI/ML methods to medical data.
  • Previous experience, coursework, and solid foundation in one or more areas of machine learning, deep learning (CNNs, RNNs, LSTMs, GANs,…), or computer vision.
  • Excellent coding skills in Python and prior experience with machine learning code libraries (Tensorflow and/or PyTorch).
  • Good oral and writing communication skills.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Previous experience with applying AI/ML, image and/or signal processing methods to medical data, and familiarity with publicly available large medical datasets
  • Proven track record of research and peer-reviewed publications in engineering, computer science, or another relevant field of study.

Residency Requirements:

  • US Citizenship NOT required.
  • All applicants must have resided in the U.S. for at least three (3) of the past five (5) years for the Office of Personnel Management to be able to complete a background check
