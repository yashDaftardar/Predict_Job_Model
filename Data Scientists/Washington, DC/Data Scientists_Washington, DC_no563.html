Overview:
Blackwatch International Corporation (Blackwatch), a small business founded in 2010, provides world-wide mission critical services to the Department of State, Department of Defense, Department of Energy, Intelligence and Law Enforcement communities. We’re headquartered in McLean, VA with satellite offices located in Carmichael, CA and O’Fallon, IL and additional work locations throughout the US, Europe and the Middle East.

Position Title: Senior Data Scientist
Position location(s): Springfield, VA and Fort Belvoir, VA
Position Type: Full-time
Years of Experience: 10+ years of experience
Minimum Security Clearance: Top Secret/SCI (Springfield) or Top Secret/SCI with CI Poly (Fort Belvoir)

Blackwatch International is seeking multiple Senior Data Scientists to support our customer onsite in Springfield and Fort Belvoir, VA. The Senior Data Scientist will use their understanding of geographic information science and technology, spatial thinking, remote sensing, intelligence issues, spatial relationships and visualization techniques and the social and physical sciences to create information, characterize events, and discover relationships and trends. A successful candidate will be able to provide leadership and bring expert knowledge of acquiring and analyzing forensic data and evidence, data preservation and chain of custody and be able to produce descriptive and predictive analyses and communicate findings as written, visual, and/or oral geospatial intelligence.
Responsibilities:
  • Use analytic tools and techniques such as ArcGIS, JEMA-IC/U, MIST, LMSS, data visualization, modeling, systems analysis, and comparative analysis to create structured data environments to address complex military and intelligence problems.
  • Provide database management using programming languages (i.e. Perl, SQL, HTML, etc.)
  • Define, Execute, and Lead complex or technical studies involving the assimilation and integration of multiple datasets, preferably applying data science techniques and methodologies.
  • Apply knowledge of DoD and IC missions to guide the search, discovery, research, extraction, and manipulation of large volumes of data from multiple intelligence disciplines.
  • Develop and refine existing mission Cross-INT/Cross-Domain data acquisition and assimilation processes and documenting those processes in standard operating procedures (SOP).
  • Apply change management practices in support of customers, mission partners, and authorities to introduce, develop, and implement comprehensive methodologies that support repeatable and scalable Cross-INT/Cross-Domain data acquisition and assimilation operations.
  • Pull, compare, contrast, merge, extract, research, de-conflict, extrapolate, and portray data geospatially from multiple NGA and IC databases using JEMA, JAVA, Python, ArcGIS, and other software to manage automated workflows for rapid data acquisition and assimilation.
  • Develop and sustain inter-organizational mission partnerships or relationships across the NGA and/or with the NRO, the IC, and the DoD.
  • Develop and apply quantitative and qualitative analytic methods to identify, collect, process, and analyze large data sets for specified purposes.
  • Seek out spatial data from classified and unclassified sources and utilize existing tools or develop methods to tie spatial information to datasets where necessary.
  • Conduct data mining and retrieval and apply statistical and mathematical analysis to identify trends, solve analytic problems, optimize performance, and gather intelligence.
  • Develop scripts and algorithms e.g. Python, create explanatory and predictive models and conduct comparative analyses to address complex military and intelligence problems.
  • Provide excellent customer support and interact with analytic units in a professional manner to help understand their challenges, incorporate their feedback, and provide quality solutions.
Qualifications:
  • A minimum High School Diploma/GED equivalent. Bachelor’s degree and Master’s degree in Applied Mathematics, Computer Science, Data Science, Geographic Information Science, Informatics and Computing, Information Science, Operations Research, Statistics, or a related discipline preferred
  • Current DoD TS/SCI or DoD TS/SCI with CI Poly security clearance
  • US Citizenship required
  • At least 10 years’ experience in working with large or complex data sets, including performing data mining, running complex queries, or performing tasks in related areas of work.
  • Ability to identify, retrieve, manipulate, relate and/or exploit multiple structured and unstructured data sets from various sources including building or generating new data sets as appropriate.
  • Ability to interpret and evaluate the results of data science community’s methods model and/or algorithms, understanding the meaning, limitations and scope of the results and translate them into insightful output for data science and/or mission and identifying other applications for use.
  • Ability to initiate the efficient implementation of methods, tools, and algorithms using a comprehensive range of technologies.
  • Ability to lead work unit proactively addressing and responding to the most difficult data science-related challenges in a timely manner while effective balancing competing priorities
  • Ability to identify problems to which data science can be applied and initiate appropriate solutions.
  • Experience performing system optimization or trend analysis on an operational system, preferably in the intelligence field.
  • Demonstrated proficiency in applied programming and/or manipulation of data with a programing language such as Python, R, SQL or Java, other languages will be considered.
  • Ability to communicate technically complex methods and solutions to non-technical audiences verbally or in writing.
  • Knowledge of IC and DoD missions.
