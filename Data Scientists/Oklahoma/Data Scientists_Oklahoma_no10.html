    Senior Statistical Programmer

    Position Summary:

    The Planning, Research, and Evaluation department supports data-informed decision making for both OKCPS schools and central office departments. This position plays a key role in district and school improvements, understanding the needs of leaders, serving on process improvement teams, supporting the Student Information System, and fulfilling both routine and emergent data analysis and reporting needs. Duties include, but are not limited to, the creation and maintenance of current and archived databases, creation, testing and documentation of computer syntax for various reports and data analysis, and communicating results to and extrapolating needs from customers.

    Essential Duties:

    • Consults with project staff and district administrators to maintain time lines and meet required deadlines.
    • Reviews requests, resources, and long-range plans for data analysis and database services.
    • Coordinates the gathering of information requested by Federal, State, and other agencies.
    • Analyzes and interprets data to insure consistently accurate and valid information.
    • Provides technical assistance in data collection and analysis to other departments and outside evaluation sources.
    • Exercises general data governance to preclude any violations of the rights of students, teachers, or staff members.
    • Develops and implements SQL processes.
    • Gather and interpret requirements and translate them into deliverables which meet the needs of organization.
    • Explores and implement analytical packages
    • Assists in planning, implementing, organizing, and reporting of testing information or materials.
    • Performs other planning and research functions, tasks, or duties as assigned by the Executive Director of Planning, Research, and Evaluation.
    • Promotes the overall effectiveness of the organization by performing tasks and sharing responsibilities of other members of the Department during peak periods or when there is an overload of duties.
    • Performs other duties as assigned.

    Job Specifications: To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily. The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skill and/or ability required.

    Minimum Qualifications (Knowledge, Skills and/or Abilities Required):

    • Master’s degree from an accredited college or university with major course work in statistical analysis, research or program evaluation design and methods, psychometrics, experimental psychology, education, business or public administration, sociology, economics, or computer science.
    • Excellent oral, written, and interpersonal communication skills.
    • Competence in database creation, project management, and strategic planning.
    • Ability to develop and maintain successful working relationships.
    • Analytical and conceptual thinking skills.
    • Adaptability and flexibility.
    • Proficiency with SQL programming and analysis
    • SQL development ability to write and troubleshoot SQL code and design (stored procedures, functions, tables, views, triggers, indexes, constraints).
    • Create complex stored procedures to meet business demands, triggers, functions and performance tuning of SQL queries.
    • Proficiency with SPSS or SAS or R or Python programming and analysis, including experience with large-scale education databases.
    • Proficiency with Visual Studio, SQL Server Reporting Services, SQL Server Integration Services, and SQL Server Management Studio.
    • Proficiency with a Microsoft Office software (including Excel).

    Preferred Qualifications (Knowledge, Skills and/or Abilities Preferred):

    • Experience in PK-12 Public School Systems or in PK-12 research.
    • Significant experience in designing, maintaining, and manipulating large-scale databases.
    • Significant experience using SQL for storing, manipulating and retrieving data.
    • Significant experience with large scale education information systems.

    Working Conditions and Physical Requirements:

    The working conditions and physical requirements described here are representative of those that must be met by an employee to successfully perform the essential functions of this job. Reasonable accommodation may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform essential functions.

    • Able to sit and/or stand for extended periods of time,
    • Exhibit manual dexterity to enter data into a computer,
    • Ability to see and read a computer screen and printed material with or without vision aids,
    • Hear and understand speech at normal classroom levels, outdoors and on the telephone,
    • Speak in audible tones so that others may understand clearly.
    • Physical agility to bend, stoop, climb stairs, walk, reach and lift up to 25 pounds.
    • Duties are normally performed in an office environment.
    • The noise level in the work environment is usually moderate.
