Company:

SIOS Technology Corp. makes software products that provide the IT resilience companies need to protect business-critical applications in today’s complex data centers and the cloud. Over its 20 year history, the Company has delivered its award winning high availability software products that ensure uptime for critical applications operating across physical, virtual, and cloud environments to a large established customer base.

As a wholly owned subsidiary of a publicly traded Japanese parent company, SIOS Technology Corp. (STC) offers the stability and cash flow of a larger software provider and the excitement, dynamics and value creation opportunities of a software start-up.


Position Overview:


SIOS Technology Corp. is seeking a talented and enthusiastic Software Engineer for the Customer Experience to join our fast-paced, agile, and fun engineering team at the R&D office located on the University of South Carolina campus (Columbia, SC).


Software Engineer for the Customer Experience team works with Customer Support and Engineering to troubleshoot, provide problem resolution, and software solutions for complex problems and products to delight customers and partners. In this role, you are responsible for assisting pre-sales, post-sales, customer support, quality testing, and software development teams in the areas of design, coding, documentation, and/or testing of new or modified software with only general supervision. This position requires the ability to work remotely as needed, and participate in an After-Hours Pager rotation.


Under the direction of your supervisor, you will troubleshoot and analyze customer related problems, obtain and clarify requirements, contribute to design, generate code and solutions, and testing of a moderately complex feature, feature set, hotfix, or component of a product. You will work in close collaboration with the Customer Support Team to review customer logs, participate in live customer troubleshooting sessions, and in sessions providing problem resolution. You will also work in collaboration with other team members with some oversight to complete their projects.


Successful candidates have efficient and creative solutions to complex problems, a good understanding of system and application administration, product development, and current technologies. The successful candidate is mature, capable of independent work and research, and able to contribute effectively to designs, code and code reviews, planning and execution of moderately complex projects, special tasks in parallel with regular work. This is an entry-level position.


Key Duties and Responsibilities:


  • Plan, isolate, troubleshoot, and correct complex problems in product software, with limited guidance and supervision.
  • Analyze software code written in various languages (such as C, C++, C#, Perl, Powershell, Python, Shell) to find and remediate errors
  • Provide exceptional day-to-day support to customers to ensure that customer expectations are exceeded
  • Clearly Identify, troubleshoot, and resolve issues reported by customers (internal and external) in a timely manner
  • Track issues using the provided support tools
  • Own and manage issues from inception through resolution, working with various teams (Engineering, Sales, Quality Testing, and Product Management) as necessary
  • Participate in the customer troubleshooting sessions via meeting software (Zoom, WebEx, Team, etc)
  • Participate in the SIOS After-Hours support rotation
  • Create enhancement requests and product change requests when reported by customers
  • Prioritize time and casework with supervision Create new and maintain existing Knowledge Base Articles / Technical Notes / Solutions and Customer facing documentation using the provided support tools.
  • Share experiences of project(s) with peers to assure breadth of perspective, to increase learning, and to identify potential applications in other areas.
  • Plan, specify, and take responsibility for their contributions to complex software problems.
  • Interact with other technical groups to achieve their project goals
  • Interact with other functional organizations to identify and resolve issues to achieve overall project goals.
  • Communicate progress and problems to management, estimating work schedule impacts and anticipating and avoiding problems.
