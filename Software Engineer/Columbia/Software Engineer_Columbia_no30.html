Full Job Description

Plans, develops, and maintains agency's public, intranet and extranet websites. Ensures positive user experiences through logical navigation, efficient functionality, design aesthetics, branding experience, messaging, site features, information architecture and landing page support. Responsible for collecting and/or writing, organizing, updating, and supervising the placement of content on LLR public and internal websites. Works closely with LLR leadership and staff to maximize Web-based opportunities. Edits and places text for all Agency Divisions and Offices (Labor, Fire and Life Safety, and Professional and Occupational Licensing). Adheres to brand and graphic standards across all sites. Responsible for the design and functionality of existing and newly created agency websites. Evaluates and makes recommendations to improve visual human interface utilizing user-centered design principles. Monitors search engine and Google Analytics performance and recommends/develops content and link-based strategies to improve performance according to key strategic organizational goals. Ensures compliance with Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and other regulatory requirements. Designs traditional paper publications such as letterhead, posters, newsletters, reports as needed. Designs and develops new Web pages and sub-sites to meet user needs. Meets with internal departments to determine whether new pages or sub-sites are required. Helps build pages to conform to Web browsers on both Macintosh and Windows platforms to meet graphic and prevailing Web standards. Where appropriate, works with DoTS OIS programming team to help design pages. Performs testing and quality assurance as part of new web technology rollouts. Coordinates activities of the agency-wide Internet liaisons and creates, maintains and updates policies related to the liaisons responsibilities & role. Works with DoTS OIS programming team as a liaison when web applications are required to interact with external and internal websites. This will include the testing and integration of applications with website(s). Performs other duties as assigned.

A bachelor's degree in communications, computer science, or a related field and experience in
information technology. Related experience may be substituted for the bachelor's degree on a
year-for-year basis.

This position works in an office environment with long periods of sitting. May require additional
work, as needed, outside of normal Agency business hours and/or on the weekend.
