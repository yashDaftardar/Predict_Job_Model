Who We Are
The South Carolina Department of Revenue (SCDOR) helps move South Carolina forward by collecting 95% of the state's General Fund. Committed to making compliance easy for taxpayers, and valuing excellence, collaboration, and professionalism, we work to be an innovative and trustworthy partner for taxpayers, businesses, and tax professionals. With excellent benefits and opportunities for training and advancement, we are dedicated to developing, retaining, and valuing a competent, productive, and diverse workforce.

Job Purpose
Develop and configure applications within the MyDORWAY and DORWAY systems. This integrated tax portal modernizes and secures processes for taxpayers and employees.

Job Functions
  • Development
    • Program, test, debug, and configure new or modified systems from approved design documents.
    • The primary development environment will be the Microsoft .Net framework and VB programming language with SQL Server database, but other development languages and tools may be used as needed.
  • Problem Solving
    • Assist in resolving system, program, and user problems as needed in a production environment.
    • Identify source of problems, perform necessary modifications, and test to ensure that the problems are resolved according to established service level agreements.
  • Operational Knowledge
    • Demonstrate a working knowledge of business operational processes, associated supporting technology, and system interfaces.
  • Technical Support
    • Assist in providing consultation to customers related to information technology.
    • Interview customers to determine business requirements. Identify how technology may enable those requirements and participate in the development of feasibility studies.
    • Assist the user in training and in operation of the new or existing systems.
  • Process and Standards Management
    • Prepare appropriate documentation throughout the development life cycle, ensuring compliance with SCDOR policies, standards, and operations.
    • Assist in training new employees and serve as a technical consultant for them.
    • Keep project leaders and supervisor informed of work progress.

Requires one of the following:
  • Bachelor's degree in computer science or a related area
  • Associate's degree in computer science or a related area and two years of work experience in computer system development, modification, and/or maintenance.
  • High school diploma and four years of experience in computer system development, modification, and/or maintenance.
  • Academic degrees must be from an accredited institution of higher learning.

Background Check: SCDOR employees are required to comply with all SCDOR tax requirements and are subject to a National Criminal Background Check to include fingerprinting.
