Full Job Description

The Center for Applied Systems & Software (CASS) is seeking a Systems Development Engineer. This is a full-time (1.0 FTE), 12-month, professional faculty position. Reappointment is at the discretion of the Director.

The Systems Development Engineer works with the Center for Applied Systems and Software (CASS)’s Software Development Group (SoftDev) as a part of the School of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science’s efforts to provide experiential learning opportunities to students. This position is highly technical and is involved in designing and building solutions to varied problems as well as providing mentorship and training for students. This position hires and trains undergraduate students in developing the necessary skills to provide quality software solutions to solve real world problems for the center’s professional customers.

Position Duties

50% – Student Mentorship and Supervision:

  • Mentor and supervise teams of CASS SoftDev student workers, including pair programming and providing feedback; helping with problem resolution; planning and scheduling work; setting priorities; monitoring work product quality; and recruiting, hiring, and training new students.
  • Provide student workers’ formal reviews, rewards, raises and discipline as appropriate; evaluating and responding to student worker concerns; and otherwise supporting student learning and professional development.

30% – Systems Analysis and Development:

  • Responsible for the delivery of enterprise solutions to CASS customers within their project scope, schedule, and budget. These solutions typically involve using state-of-the-art development tools and technologies as well as assisting in business process analysis and improvement.
  • Perform technical writing when necessary, including documenting architecture, business process models, requirements, solution options, and mockups with the support of SoftDev staff and student analysts.

10% – Project and Client Management:

  • Responsible for managing day-to-day project work, including leading standups, managing tasks using agile planning tools, forecasting student resource levels, providing project estimates, communicating with customers about project status and escalating issues as needed.
  • Assist in maintaining customer relationships.

5% – Operations & Problem Resolution:

  • Assist in the deployment, maintenance and troubleshooting of production applications developed by CASS that are managed directly within the cloud or within the OSU IT infrastructure.
  • Troubleshoot and resolve issues in the SoftDev development environment.

5% – Professional Development:

  • Stay up to date on current technologies and best practices for software development. Keep abreast of modern tools, technologies, and practices to stay proficient within the software engineering discipline.
  • Some funds are available to support professional development activities.
Minimum/Required Qualifications

Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Science or related discipline, or an equivalent combination of education and experience.

Demonstrable work experience building real-world software solutions using current software development tools and technologies.

Demonstrable experience and commitment to working with a diverse group of people within a team environment to successfully complete a project.

Demonstrable experience in successfully mentoring or leading others.

Demonstrable commitment to promoting and enhancing diversity, equity, and inclusion.

This position is designated as a critical or security-sensitive position; therefore, the incumbent must successfully complete a criminal history check and be determined to be position qualified as per OSU Standard 576-055-0000 et seq. Incumbents are required to self-report convictions.

Preferred (Special) Qualifications

Experience in successfully mentoring students or interns in a professional capacity within the fields of IS/IT.

Effective communication skills including preparing and delivering presentations, participating in group discussions, and writing technical documents.

Experience with modern front-end web development technologies and frameworks (e.g., HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and SPA Frameworks like Angular, React, Vue.js).

Experience with the Microsoft .NET development stack and tools (e.g., C#, ASP.NET, WinForms, WPF, MS SQL Server, Visual Studio, VS Code).
