The Oregon Health Authority has a fantastic opportunity for an experienced Senior Software Engineer to join an excellent team and work to advance their IT operations.

What you will do!

As the Senior Software Engineer , you will work with customers to define and translate business requirements into technical requirements in the creation of application systems. You will analyze, design, develop, test and implement application systems, as well as maintaining existing systems. You will also direct and coordinate all levels of the data and process modeling, work process, user interface design, and technological infrastructure design.

This position requires excellent leadership and communication skills, including good listening skills: effective communications to senior or executive management levels in specific customer or public situations; facilitation skills for customer groups at the middle to senior management level.

What's in it for you?

We offer a workplace that balances productivity with enjoyment; promote an atmosphere of mutual respect, dedication, and enthusiasm. You will collaborate in an open office with a team of bright individuals to work with and learn from. We offer full medical, vision and dental with paid sick leave, vacation, personal leave and ten paid holidays per year plus pension and retirement plans . If you're driven by the passion to do something meaningful that changes lives, the Oregon Health Authority is the place for you.

This is a full-time, permanent position that is classified and represented by a union.

This position falls under the classification of Information Systems Specialist 8.

What we are looking for:

  • (a) Seven (7) years of information systems experience in system development ; OR (b) An Associate's degree or higher in Computer Science, Information Technology, or related field, OR completion of a two (2) year accredited vocational training program in information technology or related field AND 5 years of information systems experience in system development ; OR (c) A Bachelor's degree in Information Technology, Computer Science, or related field AND three (3) years of information systems experience in system development ; OR (d) Master's degree in Information Technology, Computer Science, or related field AND one (1) year of information systems experience in system development.
  • Knowledge of concepts, methodologies, techniques and tools of system development.
  • Excellent oral and written communications skills.
  • Ability to work with and facilitate diverse groups and individuals.
  • Ability to prioritize workloads.
  • Ability to analyze complex procedures, processes, and policies.
  • Project management experience.
  • Expertise in application support or development using COBOL, CICS, SQL, and DB2 in support of complex customer requirements.
  • Knowledge of specific DHS/SPD applications, these include, but are not limited to: payment system (SJV), Accounting Interface (AI), and Check Reconciliation (CR).
  • This position requires experience in the System development Life Cycle methodology and experience estimating resources and schedules for complex system developmental efforts.
  • Experience in advancing health equity, addressing systemic health disparities and collaborating with diverse, vulnerable and underrepresented populations.
