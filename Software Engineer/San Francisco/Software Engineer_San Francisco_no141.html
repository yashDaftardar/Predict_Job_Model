ROLE OVERVIEW

Our engineering team is comprised of the Applications Team, the Platform & Infrastructure Team, and the Data & Machine Learning Products Team. The Insights product lives within the Data & Machine Learning Products Team. We’re looking for engineers to build out our Insights capabilities; this is Benchling’s native visualization tooling that gives scientists, managers, and executives the answers they need from their data in Benchling.

YOU MIGHT WORK ON
  • Own projects end-to-end, from initial design, to prototype, to large-scale rollout.
  • Design and implement rich frontend components and architecture. We build and operate one of the largest, richest React applications out there and power complex scientific analyses.
  • Work closely with Sales, Marketing, Customer Experience, Product, and Engineering to establish best practices around usage of our data platform.
  • Help rapidly scale our product and team. As a member of the engineering team, you'll be an integral part of how we mature our tooling, best practices, engineering processes, and hiring.
Check out our engineering blog for some examples of past work. Here are some other examples of recent and future projects:
  • Complex querying and visualization tools - The big advantage of keeping all your data stored in one system is that you can query that data all at once, without digging up multiple sources. This works best when you can query Benchling the same way you can query a database: by choosing from a large number of possible filters on any related objects, joining together various sources, and performing aggregations. We want to enable this in a user-friendly experience, without leaving the application.
  • Support point-and-click query building - Our analytics product right now supports technical users who want to ask questions about their data through SQL. We’d like to extend our interface so that non-technical users can also derive insights from the data in Benchling. We’ll be utilizing our deep understanding of the types of data our scientists are working with to build a tool that allows these non-technical users to ask complicated, custom questions easily.
  • Support scientific analytics - Our analytics product supports users writing queries in SQL, but doesn’t yet support scientists trying to answer very specific questions about their data that SQL doesn’t support as well - statistical tests, linear regression, curve fitting, normalization, and others. In collaboration with the platform team, build the system by which users can execute arbitrary functions against their data.
