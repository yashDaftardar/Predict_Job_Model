Full Job Description

Minimum qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Electrical Engineering/Computer Science, related technical discipline or equivalent practical experience.
  • 5 years of experience as a firmware engineer in consumer electronics, computer engineering or related field.
  • 3 years of experience with driver development for embedded devices.
  • 3 years of experience software development and programming using C.


Preferred qualifications:

  • Experience in hardware and software development methodology.
  • Experience with BSP development and new board bring up
  • Experience and in depth knowledge of factory floor and assembly process and methodologies along with manufacturing tests
  • Familiar with PDM/PCM, USB, Serial Communication Protocols (I2C, SPI, UART), MIPI, and SDIO
  • Comfortable with electrical test tools such as multimeters, oscilloscopes, data signal acquisition modules.
  • Ability to proactively communicate with all team members, employees and vendors and work well in a fast paced environment.

About the job

In this role, you will develop and support a comprehensive hardware diagnostic tool suite for prototype hardware and system validation testing. You'll cover diagnostic tool development and maintenance and work as a member of a platform hardware development team. You will actively participate in all phases of development and support from architecture definition and design through implementation, debugging, testing and support. You will work collaboratively and alongside other hardware, firmware, software, and QA engineers, manufacturing test teams, and external development partners.

Fitbit’s diverse line of innovative products of smartwatches, trackers and smart scale help people lead healthier, more active lives by empowering them with data, inspiration and guidance to reach their health and fitness goals. Our products deliver personalized experiences, insights and guidance through leading software and interactive tools to support our users’ health and wellness. Partnering with Google will expand our resources and global platform, and we will be able to accelerate innovation in the wearables category to scale faster, and make health even more accessible to everyone.

Responsibilities

  • Be responsible for mapping test coverage, priorities, and schedule of the Manufacturing Firmware. Lead cross-functional coordination with various firmware teams in charge specific pieces of the manufacturing firmware
  • Be responsible for supporting, troubleshooting, conducting diagnostics, evaluating electronic circuitry, subsystems, or other hardware elements.
  • Define and verify the functionality of manufacturing test firmware and drive automation of firmware test cases to help scale development and manufacturing test process
  • Understand schedule, risk, priorities, and factory timelines before, during, and after the build.
  • Travel to Fitbit manufacturing sites and other Fitbit development offices 25% of the time as required.
