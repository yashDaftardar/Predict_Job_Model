Full Job Description

The Virginia Employment Commission (VEC) is seeking a Java Programmer to join our Information Technology Division team in our Central Office located in Henrico. The VEC Information Technology (IT) Division provides complete, accurate, available and accessible data, application, platform and security architectures in technological diverse environments, and provides quality services and support to all customers and stakeholders. This Division also develops, maintains, and supports a comprehensive inventory of IT systems, as well as manages and maintains infrastructure for in- and out-of-scope computing environments, manages operations, and works diligently with external service providers to provide effective IT services.

As an entry level Java Programmer, this position will work on multiple IT projects and have responsibilities in Analysis, Code, Test, and Deployment. The Java Programmer will use JAVA, Eclipse, Spring, Hibernate, JBoss, Subversion, and related tools for new development, testing, deployment, enhancements, and maintenance.

Additional duties of this position include:

Use interfaces and reports efficiently, and follow security best practices.

Prepare data requirements and program specifications that are complete, concise, appropriate and easily understood.

As assigned, ensure unit tests are written for new business functions and classes, and are run successfully before deployment.

Ensure changes are thoroughly tested and documented, and the system is deemed acceptable by the customer with nominal defects found after deployment.

Prepare application documentation (ex: use cases, software requirements specifications, interface documents, and wireframes), user documentation, and user training materials.

Perform systems analysis and design for moderately complex applications/systems, and conduct training as needed.

Confer with analysts, domain experts and users to understand needs and document requirements.

Access Federal Tax Information (FTI) to administer, maintain and/or audit activity that supports the integrity of the Unemployment Insurance program.

This salaried, exempt position offers Commonwealth of Virginia employee benefits such as 12 paid holidays, health benefits, and more. Please visit https://www.dhrm.virginia.gov/employeebenefits for more information. For more information about the VEC, please visit www.vec.virginia.gov.



Minimum Qualifications

Basic knowledge of Java related technologies, SQL, and data systems.

Basic knowledge of program coding and documentation, as well as IT security guidelines.

Strong interpersonal, oral communication, written communication, analytical, and computer skills.

Ability to write code and programs and to update, repair, modify, and expand existing programs.

Ability to perform work on multiple IT projects, and to support and maintain existing systems as required.

Ability to interact effectively in a team environment, as well as to work independently, typically with strict deadlines, heavy workloads and changing priorities.

Ability to recognize and prevent errors that can cause substantial delay, and handle routine IT issues.

NOTE: The selected candidate must successfully complete a FBI finger print background investigation and state law enforcement background check, and subsequent reinvestigation(s) that must be conducted within 10 years from the date of the previous background investigation.



Preferred Qualifications

Graduation from an accredited college or university with a degree or major in Computer Science, Information Systems or related field, or an equivalent combination of training and experience.

Prefer experience working with JAVA, Eclipse, Spring, Hibernate, JBoss, and Subversion and/or related tools.
