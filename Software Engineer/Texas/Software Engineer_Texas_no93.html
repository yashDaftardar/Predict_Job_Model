ABOUT FIREFLY AEROSPACE
Come be a part of the 21 st * Century Space Race.* At Firefly, we are focused on the leading edge of space systems progression. Our company is committed to providing economical and convenient access to space for small payloads through the design, manufacture, and operation of reliable launch vehicles and spacecraft. To get there, we are looking for incredibly passionate, bright, and determined people to join our team.
SUMMARY
In this role you will help design, develop, test, and integrate the embedded software for computer vision-based pose estimation for a Firefly spacecraft. This software will determine the spacecraft’s relative position, attitude, and linear and angular velocities in real time from image data. You will work in a group of software engineers through all stages of the spacecraft’s computer vision software development processes and collaborate with other engineering teams.
RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Assist the Spacecraft Computer Vision Software Team to complete the development, test, and integration of the computer vision-based pose estimation software on time, meeting all functional and performance requirements
  • Utilizing OpenCV, develop algorithms for attitude, velocity, and position estimation of the spacecraft from monocular camera and stereo images
  • Develop algorithms for feature detection and tracking, object detection, classification, and image segmentation
  • Work closely with the other Spacecraft engineering groups for the success of the program
  • Adhere to the Aerospace Software Engineering Group’s processes, procedures, and best practices

QUALIFICATIONS
Required

  • Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Sciences, Aerospace Engineering, or a related field with 3-5 years of relevant experience.
  • Good understanding of the C++ programming language
  • Good knowledge of computer vision algorithms for feature matching, tracking, and pose estimation
  • Experience with OpenCV
  • Ability to work closely with other teammates and in a fast-paced environment to meet strict deadlines
  • Willingness to travel domestically up to 25% of time to support collaborative work

Desired

  • Master’s degree in Computer Sciences, Aerospace Engineering, or a related field with 1-2 years of relevant experience.
  • Advanced understanding of C++ programming language techniques
  • Experience with the development of navigation, sensor fusion, and machine learning algorithms
  • Knowledge of Orbital Mechanics
  • Familiarity with Agile Software Development Processes, Git version control and workflows
  • Familiarity with the Linux operating system
