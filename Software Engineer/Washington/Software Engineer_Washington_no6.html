Position Summary

Why It's a Great Opportunity:
As a junior level IT professional with interest in full-stack development, you will have the opportunity to work with 45 Pierce County Software Engineers to build and enhance applications for Pierce County citizens and employees. You will learn agile software development methodologies from a solid, experienced software team. Through a series of checks and balances you will be assigned to write software code for a feature which will be reviewed for accuracy and standards. After review and testing you will make corrections or mark complete prior to going into production.

This is truly hands on learning and over time, you will demonstrate the ability to independently produce high-quality code, work collaboratively with other Software Engineers and Business Analysts, and communicate technical work concisely and accurately.

You will have the opportunity to work on a variety of frameworks, tools, and languages, including, but not limited to, Angular, Grails, Groovy, Java, Sybase or MySQL SQL, and Karma, Protractor, Spock and Jasmine tests.

Here are some examples of applications we have created or supported.

Why It's a Great Department:
The IT Division with over 130 staff, has an excellent reputation and is committed to providing top-notch service to our customers. We are a national leader in delivering public services through innovation. Our diverse technology solutions are all supported by a robust hybrid infrastructure of on-premises and cloud-based solutions and a secure network. We are a team that prominently endorses a positive and collaborative approach where we support one another and the work each one of us provides. The work you do in this Division matters because many Pierce County public services are provided through and supported by the technologies built, operated, and maintained by the IT team. IT and this unit exemplify a positive teamwork-based approach and support one another and the work each staff member provides. We focus on a continuous learning mindset for employee development and are excited for you to join our team!

Learn more about the IT Division here.

How to be Successful in the Role:
We are looking for an adaptable, life-long learner who is a collaborative problem solver with high expectations. Someone who is a good listener, takes feedback well, is self-confident and hard working. We value building consensus, diversity of views and as a whole, focus on organizational goals over the individual. Your role models are successful software engineers, so you are learning from the best!

Your Future in this Role:
The IT Division has access to numerous training opportunities, which include peer, industry, vendor, and official Pierce County training as well as access to established procedures, standards, practices, and system documentation. Our training program is designed to help you develop as both a professional in your role as an IT Software Engineer and as a Pierce County employee. Your work will directly impact your community and it offers a great foundation for future positions within the IT Division and Pierce County.

Core Daily Responsibilities:


  • With assistance from senior-level, lead, and other co-workers, use the County's software tools, languages, frameworks, and standards to enhance and fix County system functions
  • Write software code
  • Write automated tests
  • Estimate work remaining on assigned tasks
  • Attend daily team standup meetings with Software Engineers and Business Analysts

Qualifications

Recruiting Requirements:


  • Associate of Arts of Associate of Science degree in computer sciences or related field AND
  • Two or more years of information technology experience
  • Additional education or experience may substitute for the recruiting requirements
Preferred Qualifications:
  • Experience in writing software code for web browsers, application servers, and database servers using current programming languages and frameworks
Special Requirements/Qualifications:
