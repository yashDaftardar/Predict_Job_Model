Full Job Description

The Office of Information Technology (OIT) manages the administrative and academic information technology resources for Norfolk State University. OIT provides ubiquitous access to technology over a highly secured wired and wireless network, utilizing a multi-gigabit fiber optic backbone for voice, video, data, and security. OIT invites applicants for the position of Enterprise Applications (EA) Web Application Developer.

The EA Web Application Developer is responsible for creating and maintaining web applications and using current web technologies. The EA Web Application Developer also provides high performance, high availability database systems that will support University web applications, and vendor provided client-server applications. Routine and on demand Microsoft SQL Server database and system administration duties, to include assuring databases are properly tuned at all times will be required. Works with considerable independence and autonomy under the direction of the team leader and the department director who review work through conferences, meetings, reports, and results achieved.

Duties and responsibilities include but not limited to:

1. Write custom code and rebrand existing websites and applications.
2. Design and recommend improvements to new and existing features.
3. Assist in troubleshooting issues on web-based systems.
4. Test and give feedback on new and existing technologies.
5. Create and maintain software documentation.
6. Monitor web application to provide health of the application.
7. Take the initiative to find answers independently.
8. Must be dependable (attendance and punctuality) and manages time effectively and efficiently.
9. Provide technical presentations and specialized training when required.
10. Prepare or assist in the preparation of system and user documentation.
11. Communicate verbally and in writing with both professional and technical staff.
12. Dependable (attendance and punctuality) and manages time effectively and efficiently.
13. Follows established safety practices or corrects unsafe practices on the job.
14. Weekly meetings with the staff to identify goals and progress.
15. Performs other duties as assigned by the Director of Enterprise Applications or Chief Information Officer.

Candidates are encouraged to visit https://www.nsu.edu/About/Administrative-Offices-Services/its for more information on the NSU’s Office of Information Technology.

Minimum Qualifications

1. Demonstrated ability to develop complex enhancements to web applications in support of Norfolk State University with minimal timeframe.
2. Considerable knowledge of the following technologies: HTML, CSS, PHP, JavaScript/JQuery, GitLab, Wordpress, cross-browser compatibility and accessibility.
3. Considerable knowledge of .NET and API programming.
4. Considerable knowledge of Microsoft SharePoint and PowerApps programming.
5. Considerable knowledge of Microsoft Dynamics programming.
6. Considerable hardware capabilities should include: file servers, networked data storage, data communication devices (routers, firewalls, switches, hubs, wireless access points, etc.), network management appliances and disaster recovery equipment
7. Considerable software capabilities should include: SQL Server, Visual Studio, and IDES such as Eclipse or similar software.
8. Demonstrated ability to communicate clearly and effectively at all levels, both verbally and in writing; comprehend and follow detailed instructions provided in verbal, written and graphic formats; understand and adhere to etiquette principles for voice and data communications.



Preferred Qualifications

1. Bachelor degree in Information Technology, or related field and two years of industry experience.
2. Demonstrated Microsoft SharePoint Administration experience.
3. Experience working with Higher-Ed ERP systems (Ellucian ERP products highly preferred).
4. Experience with Project Management (e.g. Waterfall, Agile).
5. Experience with Enterprise — level Applications that integrate with Higher-Ed ERP Systems.
6. Experience following System Development Life Cycle and iterative processes.
7. Experience in the coordination and delivery of computing services and technology in an academic environment.
