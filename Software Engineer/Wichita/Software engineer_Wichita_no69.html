Summary

This position is responsible for developing new or maintaining existing application systems, using structured methodologies for systems development and advanced database design. Work entails design, implementation and management, including the direction, analysis, documentation, development and testing of application systems for the KTA.

Definition and Examples of Work

  • Works with other departments and end users to determine requirements for application projects and develops programming specifications
  • Designs, develops, tests, and documents complex applications, which may interact with other systems, applications or modules
  • Modifies and maintains existing application programs, databases, and related processes
  • Analyzes areas where IT and application development can be effectively utilized to enable employees to better perform their job functions.
  • Gathers requirements from non-technical staff and conducts thorough problem analysis to determine a solution.
  • Coaches and provides professional advice to less experienced developers in the resolution of application and database development
  • Answers questions pertaining to IT policies and procedures.
  • Additional duties may be assigned as business needs require.

Latitude and Impact of Position

This position works daily to obtain information, provide input, and problem solve independently and as part of a team, while researching and using extensive technical and industry knowledge. Most work projects are assigned, then the employee arranges his/her work tasks for completion within established timeframes. This position may direct the work of others while providing coaching and training in an informal manner. Decision making may be done independently related to own work tasks and when decisions do not impact other departments. All purchase requests require supervisory approval. Successful completion of job tasks impacts internal systems, processes and procedures, operating costs, customer satisfaction, and company liability.

QUALIFICATION

Required Knowledge, Skills and Abilities

  • Ability to design and develop complex applications, which may interact with other systems, applications or modules
  • In-depth knowledge of IBM Power Systems / IBM i system architecture, its capabilities, development environment, languages and utilities (e.g., Rational Developer for i, RPG IV, CL, DDS, SQL, etc.)
  • Ability to code complex, interactive subfiles and modular ILE applications, employing modern RPG IV techniques
  • Knowledge of importing and exporting data in the form of CSV or XML files, PDF documents, and spreadsheets
  • Understanding of business principles and objectives
  • Ability to evaluate technology problems requirements to translate to application specifications and documentation for manageable business solutions.
  • Excellent planning, organizational, and time management skills
  • Excellent interpersonal communications
  • Willingness to learn new technologies and expand skills
  • Proficient use of computers

Preferred Knowledge, Skills and Abilities
