Company Description

Job Description


Cash App’s mobile engineering organization is focused on building new customer-facing features and enhancing our existing products to scale. We’re developing industry-leading design systems and open source tools, all while adding exciting new functionality to our ecosystem.

As a mobile engineer on Cash’s Bitcoin Network team you will be improving and expanding our services around Bitcoin for the US and international markets, including Bitcoin P2P for Cash App customers, Bitcoin on-chain deposits and withdrawals, and Bitcoin trading around the world.

You will:

  • Build end-to-end features of the app: networking, persistence, business logic, and UI
  • Craft durable, well-tested code with an unwavering commitment to product quality
  • Invent thoughtful mobile tech and open source it
  • Re-architect legacy code and prep it to scale for the future of the product and the team
  • Partner with other mobile and backend engineers on delivering remarkable solutions
  • Have a meaningful impact on the future of the Cash product

Qualifications


You have:

  • Proficiency creating readable, well-crafted, and maintainable code
  • Experience measuring the impact of engineering work and how it translates to the business
  • The ability to incorporate customer feedback into product solutions (engineering makes substantive contributions to product definition)
  • A commitment to continuous improvement of yourself, your teammates, your software
  • Strong interest in Bitcoin and other blockchain technologies, including their intersection with consumer finance. (*Knowledge of blockchain technology is not essential, but highly desirable)

Technologies we use and teach:

  • Swift, Objective-C
  • Kotlin, including multiplatform (believe it! — we use it both Android and iOS)
  • Unit tests, snapshot tests, integration tests
  • UIKit, CoreAnimation, Combine, Swift UI, Compose, Coroutines, RxJava, SQLDelight, Retrofit, OkHttp, Wire, Moshi, Okio, Whorlwind, Picasso, KotlinPoet
  • Accessibility
  • Protocol buffers
