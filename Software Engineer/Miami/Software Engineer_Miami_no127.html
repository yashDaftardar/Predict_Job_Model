Full Job Description

The ERP Application Developer II works with and/or leads other developers, business analysts, and users to design, implement, and support the University's administrative applications.

  • Develops programs and scripts to support Acclaim's integration with PeopleSoft.
  • Develops programs and provides support for Continuing education and cocurricular initiatives.
  • Analyzes, designs and creates specifications for PeopleSoft and Acclaim application functionalities and/or requirements.
  • Analyzes and understands vendor interfaces and related business processes.
  • Develops, customizes, reports, and interfaces new processes using PeopleSoft and Acclaim tools as well as other 3rd party tools.
  • Interacts with database administrators, security analysts, and business system users. Coordinates with vendors when necessary to resolve issues.
  • Collaborates with various groups (customer's help desk, technical infrastructure, etc.) to identify and resolve Acclaim and PeopleSoft application issues.
  • Provides production support including problem analysis and resolution, job scheduling, monitoring, tuning, and end-user support.
  • Provides assistance to all phases in the project set up & prototyping, design, development, conversion, testing, deployment.
  • Understands and implements change control methodology.
  • Creates accurate and detailed documentation for supporting Acclaim and all integrations.
  • Assists with providing proof of concept through extensive prototyping, project stakeholders and end users and proposes technical solutions.
  • Assists in addressing problems related to systems integration, compatibility, and multiplatform integration between Acclaim and all integrations. This includes guidance related to general and portal-specific software design, processes development, security, infrastructure, migration paths, and deployment best
  • practices.
  • Provides support for badging systems, such as Acclaim. Builds and maintains interfaces and customizations for that system.
  • Provides planning, implementation and support for the disaster recovery response for the systems under this memorandum.
  • Ensures that personnel related to the provision of services under this memorandum attend conferences and are otherwise engaged in professional development activities related to Acclaim and related applications.
  • Performs related duties as required or as directed. The omission of specific duties does not preclude the supervisor from assigning duties that are logically related to the position.

Minimum Qualifications


Bachelor's degree in computer science, information technology/systems or engineering. Four (4) years of design and development experience with PeopleSoft Campus Solutions, Financials/Contracts and Grants, Human Capital Management, Enterprise Portal or other large scale ERP system; four (4) years of experience in systems analysis, design, development, implementation, and support.

OR

Master's degree in computer science, information technology/systems or engineering. Two (2) years of design and development experience with PeopleSoft Campus Solutions, Financials/Contracts and Grants, Human Capital Management, Enterprise Portal, or other large scale ERP system; two (2) years of experience in systems analysis, design, development, implementation, and support.
