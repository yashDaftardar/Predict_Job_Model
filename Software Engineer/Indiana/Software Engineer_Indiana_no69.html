Full Job Description

Job Summary

Purdue University College of Education is accepting applications for the WordPress Web Content student assistant to provide support to the Director of Communication, the Web Developer, and the communication team. The ideal candidate should be an excellent communicator who is adept with website content maintenance tasks, including administrative and design under the guidance of the Director of Communication and the Web Developer.

Job Responsibilities

Job Responsibilities Include, but are not limited to:

Under the supervision of the Director of Communication and Web Developer, the WordPress Web Content student assistant will engage in College of Education website tasks and projects as assigned, including:

  • Configuring and maintaining WordPress webpages.
  • Working in WordPress to manage content, create documentation, create articles and posts, and create and maintain pages.
  • Working on blogs (create/add/update news and topics).
  • Meeting regularly with the Director of Communication.
  • Other duties related to the role as needed.

Qualifications

Required:

  • Minimum 1 year experience working in WordPress.
  • Ability to analyze user needs to implement web site content, graphics, performance, and capacity.
  • Experience in MS Word, Excel, PowerPoint.
  • Understanding of filesharing platforms (Google drive, Dropbox).
  • Must be able to work independently to complete projects in a timely manner.
  • Strong problem-solving skills.
  • Exceptional team player with strong time management and a willingness to learn new skills and ideas.
  • Must have excellent written and verbal communication skills, a strong work ethic, and enthusiasm.

Preferred:

  • Graphic design, including creating and reconfiguring graphics for the website.
  • Experience in Photoshop, including photo editing and retouching.
  • Experience in HTML, including creating email marketing templates.

Additional Information

Note: This position is currently remote but will move to on-campus in the future.

Student Requested : Work Study or Regular Student Worker, Part Time

Hours Worked : Applicants should be available to work 10 hours per week, typically during the hours of Monday – Friday, 8:00 am – 5:00 pm.

Desired Start Date : Immediately

Requested Document Notes: Submit your resume via the online application process. Applicants will be contacted for a telephone interview if selected.

Education: High School Diploma or Equivalent
