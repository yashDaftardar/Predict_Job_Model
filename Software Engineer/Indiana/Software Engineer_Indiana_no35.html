Software Integration Engineer III

Location: Lafayette, IN, US, 47904-1863

Req ID 16317 | CSS Lafayette Staff, United States


We are looking for a new colleague in our Truck & Van Driveline Technology Engineering Team at our location in Lafayette, Indiana


Responsibilities:

  • The Software Integration Engineer is the primary technical liaison for all software functional changes with regards to ZF transmission technology and is responsible for leading the coordination and execution of all software releases in the region according to internal and external requirements.
  • Responsible for the entire software product cycle in the region: new customer functional development initiatives, software development, calibration activities, prototype support, validation, engineering release, truck assembly plant support, and field troubleshooting.
  • Represents ZF throughout software product development programs at the customer and provides guidance/expertise for all technical and business-related issues.
  • Primary technical liaison to customers for software functional changes and software change management processes. Provides clarification and technical explanation of ZF product specifications, calibration solutions and component functions.
  • Leads software program deliveries by tracking action items, calibration program timing, and software/calibration releases (including, but not limited to, the creation and execution of system-level development and validation test plans, development of functional test procedures and scripting, and performance of verification testing).
  • Review local market and research new technologies that may improve Powertrain control principles and develop recommendations accordingly. Innovate and develop patentable designs – support IP activities.

Qualifications:

  • B.S. in Mechanical Engineering or related field (Master’s Degree preferred).
  • A minimum of 5 years of engineering experience in a product development environment (Experience in transmission engineering and direct experience with OEM customer sales and/or engineering is preferred).
  • Working knowledge of powertrain technology and the technical aspects of applications engineering within product design/development stages as well as knowledge of manufacturing processes and the business/customer interface to supplier operations is required. Ability to develop and convey complex technical solutions to OEM customers is also required.
  • Familiarity with the durable goods manufacturing industry and an understanding of economic factors that impact key business decisions (specifically within the automotive industry) necessary.
  • Solid knowledge of Class 3-8 North American OEMs, their products, and the technical requirements specific to North America and familiarity with transmission components and the technical characteristics of Powertrains (Engine to tires) required.
  • Must have the ability to utilize PDM systems (e.g. Axalant) and proficiency with Microsoft Office software (Experience with data acquisition tools (e.g. ETAS INCA, CANalyzer, etc.) is a plus)
  • Ability to travel both domestic and internationally, up to 25% of the time.
