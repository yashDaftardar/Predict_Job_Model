Cloud Engineer

University of Notre Dame

The University of Notre Dame (ND.jobs) is accepting applications for a Cloud Engineer .

Applications will be accepted until 5/22/2021.

The University Enabling Technology Services division of Notre Dame's Office of Information Technologies (OIT) is looking for a talented cloud engineer. Do you want to apply your IT skills for more than a paycheck? Would you like to work for a mission-based organization that positively impacts the world through the students it educates and the research it conducts? Would you like to work for an organization that encourages you to thrive both personally and professionally and has been recognized as a top IT place to work for the past five years by IDG's Computerworld magazine? Our IT organization has been honored by EDUCAUSE with the Cloud Leadership Award and is a leader in higher education in adoption of the Cloud with over 80% of its workloads in the Cloud!

If any of these describe you, please consider applying for a cloud engineer position. The OIT's Cloud Platform Services team manages our on-premise and cloud platforms with hundreds of servers and services in a complex and diverse environment. As a cloud engineer you will be responsible for the infrastructure that drives one of the top universities in the US, and have an opportunity to build the technologies that help students, faculty, and staff at Notre Dame change the world. Working at Notre Dame you will be supported in your desire for well-being, life-work integration, and professional growth & development. You will have opportunities for flexible work schedules. All new employees are assigned a sponsor to help navigate being new to Notre Dame.

The Cloud Engineer will have practical, hands-on experience with all aspects of cloud infrastructure design and management in an enterprise environment. This role is expected to provide strong technical leadership and recommend changes and create practices with regard to the design, implementation, and support of the enterprise cloud environment. This includes designing, provisioning, monitoring, and maintaining the cloud automation framework. As a cloud engineer you will be responsible for the infrastructure that drives one of the top universities in the US, and have an opportunity to build the technologies that help students, faculty, and staff at Notre Dame change the world.

Furthermore, we are looking for cloud engineers that listen well, are intellectually curious, partner effectively with others, and enjoy a challenging yet supportive environment committed to diversity and inclusion.

Of course, the real question is not what we are looking for, but rather, What are you looking for and could Notre Dame be it?

Some of your responsibilities will be:
  • Develop code and scripts to automate provisioning of AWS services and configure services.
  • Manage customer interactions and relationships.
  • Maintain and expand a secure, scalable, business continuity and disaster recovery plans and efficient cloud infrastructure.
  • Design, analyze, and provide operational metrics and cost optimization recommendations.
  • Collaborate with Solution Architects to define best practices and standards around cloud operations.
  • Stay up-to-date on cloud technology trends.
  • Provide technical feedback and leadership to other team members.



Minimum Qualifications:
  • Bachelors degree in Information Systems, Computer Science, or a related field or equivalent experience.
  • Knowledgeable about Amazon Web Services.
  • Strong understanding of AWS best practices for security, networking, and architecture.
  • Demonstrates a detailed understanding of core network protocols and services.
  • Experience with coding languages such as Bash, PowerShell, or Python.
  • Experience with Linux and Windows operating systems.
  • Experience creating and maintaining infrastructure documentation.
  • Superior written and verbal communication skills.



Preferred Qualifications:
AWS Certified Solutions Architect Associate or Professional.
Experience with virtualization technologies.
