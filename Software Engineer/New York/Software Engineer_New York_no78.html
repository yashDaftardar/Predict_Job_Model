We are seeking a DevOps engineer to join our Developer Experience team.

About this Role:

As an engineer on the DevEx team, you are responsible for maintaining and building the internal tools that power the development, test, and sandbox environments. As part of this role, you are encouraged to research and test modern infrastructures to improve developers' workflows, while collaborating with the many other teams that service VoxMedia. In addition to these duties, you handle developer's support requests, and thus face a lot of context switching. Sometimes we know the answers, other times we don't, and that's okay. We expect you to collaborate with coworkers to the best of your ability and to maintain a curious disposition. Bonus points if you can laugh at yourself.

What You'll Do:

  • Build internal tools that assist the company's engineers in their workflows leading up to the point of merging & deploying their code
  • Provide support for engineers on the tooling we provide for development, testing, and general automation, including:
    • writing skeleton code and configuration templates
    • writing and updating documentation
    • making yourself available to engineers to troubleshoot issues
  • Take part in our weekly on-call rotation, where you will respond to alerts in the case of a service's outage

What You'll Bring:

  • Proficient with at least one scripting language (eg: Bash, JavaScript, Ruby) willing to learn new ones as required
  • Familiar with, or eagerness to learn about, automation tooling like Github Actions
  • Experienced with how language frameworks, release, and use dependencies such as node modules or Ruby gems
  • Excited about the potential of cloud computing platforms (bonus: experience with administration on AWS, Google Cloud, or similar)

Additionally, you:

  • Are ready to work online with a fully remote team
  • Have a history of working across teams, regularly reaching out to and/or fielding requests from people you don't already know
  • A self-starter, willing to take on challenges that will improve your users' experience
  • Are ready to teach and communicate to people the "why" behind technical decisions
  • Have empathy for your users and teammates

For more on the things we're building, the problems we're solving, and what it's like to work with the Product, Design & Technology teams, see our Product Team blog. In an effort to provide an inclusive and respectful working environment, all team members adhere to our code of conduct.

Are you passionate about this opportunity, but worried that you don't have 100% of the experience we're looking for? We still want to hear from you!
