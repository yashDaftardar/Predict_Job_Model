Detailed Job Description
PLEASE NOTE: This is a one-year, temporary position

Design, develop, and deploy software solutions designed specifically to promote inclusion and accessibility.
Software Engineer responsibilities include gathering user requirements, defining system functionality and writing code in various languages, such as JavaScript, TypeScript, and Java . Ideal candidate is familiar with the software development life cycle (SDLC) from preliminary system analysis to tests and deployment, and have experience with cloud technologies such as Google Cloud Platform or AWS.
Ultimately, the role of the Software Engineer is build high-quality, innovative, accessible and fully performing software that complies with coding standards and technical design.

Responsibilities:
  • Create proper documentation and demonstration of solutions by use of flowcharts, charts, diagrams, documents (both physical and electronic), layouts and code comments
  • Produce specifications and integrate software components into a fully functional software system
  • Track progress in various projects to ensure quality deliverables are met in a timely fashion
  • Collaborate with team to brainstorm and create new products, deploy programs and evaluate user feedback
  • Develop software verification plans and quality assurance procedures
  • Engage in continuing education and training by studying cutting edge development tools, computer equipment and programming techniques
  • Recommend necessary improvements to procedures and policies with the goal of upgrading operations within CAT
  • Mentor software engineers (students) with decision making, mentorship and educational opportunities
  • Ensure all project and company information remains confidential throughout every step of the process.
  • OTHER DUTIES AS ASSIGNED

Required Minimum Qualifications
REQUIREMENTS
  • Bachelors degree or equivalent experience in Software Engineering, Computer Science, or related field
  • Three years of experience with software development in a UN*X environment using the following technologies: JavaScript, Node.js, Git, and at least one front-end framework such as React, Angular, or Vue.js.
  • One year of experience with API integration (REST, GraphQL, etc.) and cloud computing (Google Firebase, AWS, etc.)One year of experience writing unit tests.
  • One year of experience with Agile development
SPECIALIZED SKILLS
  • Front-end experience using React, TypeScript, Jest/Enzyme, GatsbyJS, GraphQL, Bootstrap, and Styled Components.
  • Experience building PWAs using JavaScript, APIs, and markup (JAMStack).
  • Experience designing and implementing a database schema (relational or NoSQL).
  • Experience building and deploying Docker containers to the cloud.
  • Solid analytical and problem-solving skills and strong attention to details.
  • Ability to problem-solve and implement solutions.
  • Command of standard development lifecycle (SDLC)
  • A winning attitude, self-motivated, personally accountable and determined team player, able to accept direction.
  • Awareness of risk management and mitigation techniques.

Preferred:
Ability to communicate with wide range of deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals.

Additional Details

Rochester Institute of Technology, the 10th largest private university in the U.S., is among the worlds leading technological universities. Its 18,000 undergraduate and graduate students from all 50 states and more than 100 countries are enrolled in over 200 academic programs, including 7 interdisciplinary Ph.D. programs. A pioneer in experiential education, RIT was the first to offer undergraduate degree programs in biotechnology, information technology, software engineering, and microelectronic engineering. As home to the National Technical Institute for the Deaf, the university offers unparalleled academic opportunities and services for the deaf and hard-of-hearing. Founded in 1829, RIT has 115,000 alumni throughout the United States and around the world. RIT has been named by the Chronicle of Higher Education as one of the Great Colleges to Work For.
