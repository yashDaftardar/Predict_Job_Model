GENERAL SUMMARY/ OVERVIEW STATEMENT: Summarize the nature and level of work performed. Please note that this section should be written in a paragraph format and provide a broad description of the role and its purpose as well as the reporting structure.

The individual in this position will be responsible for assisting faculty in the Institute for Technology Assessment (ITA) in conducting outcomes research (cost-effectiveness analysis, simulation modeling) to inform policies for addressing the rising burden of liver cancer, and policies to mitigate the opioid epidemic and/or COVID-19 pandemic. The research conducted at the ITA is quantitative, principally involving statistical analysis, computer simulation modeling, data visualization, and cost-effectiveness analysis. The candidate will be involved in all aspects of the investigators’ outcomes research, including data extraction, data analyses, data visualization, conducting literature reviews, working with programmers, assisting investigators will regulatory compliance (i.e. from the Institutional Review Board), and manuscript preparation and submission.

Primary anticipated responsibilities include:

  • Data analyses in R;

  • Contributing to computer simulation modeling efforts;
  • Visualization of data;

  • Support of manuscript preparation (e.g. communications with co-authors, Table/Figure development)

EEO Statement


The MGH Institute for Technology Assessment seeks a Research Associate to join a team conducting research on policies on liver cancer prevention, and to address the ongoing opioid epidemic and/or COVID-19 pandemic efforts using data-driven methods and computer simulations. Primary anticipated responsibilities include data collection and management, statistical analyses, data visualization, and literature review. Commensurate with experience and motivation, opportunity exists to participate in scientific manuscript preparation and writing. Creative thinking, strong interpersonal skills, a willingness to learn new software and skills, and the ability to handle multiple projects are expected. Research experience is not required, but preferred. Experience in computer programming is a plus, but not essential.
