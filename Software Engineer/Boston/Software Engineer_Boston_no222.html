About Zapata

Zapata computing is a software, services, and applications company whose mission is to advance humankind with revolutionary science and engineering by delivering quantum advantage to enterprise customers. We develop quantum-ready algorithms, applications, and solutions built on a workflow-based platform for enterprise data-analytics.


We are committed to creating an environment that accelerates our employee’s growth and positive impact in the world. Software development here is fast-paced, highly collaborative, and cross-disciplinary. Our world-class quantum researchers and engineers are creative, curious, and resourceful problem solvers with a revolutionary mindset.


About the job

The Zapata Quantum Software team is building a portfolio of next-generation quantum tools and algorithms to be deployed on state-of-the-art hardware through Orquestra, Zapata’s workflow management platform. As a Quantum Software Intern, you will work with the team to design, develop, deploy, and maintain these algorithms and tools. You can find some examples of the Quantum Software team’s work on the Zapata Computing Github account.


Example topic areas

  • Integrations with quantum hardware and simulators.
  • Near-term quantum algorithms for machine learning, chemistry, and discrete optimization.
  • Error mitigation tools for near-term quantum algorithms.

Internships during summer 2021 are preferred, but later dates will be considered on a case-by-case basis. Although internships can be performed remotely from any location, interns will be expected to be work during U.S. Eastern Daylight Time (EDT) or Central European Summer Time (CEST) working hours.


For internships related to quantum computing research, see Quantum Computing Research Intern.


Responsibilities

  • Design, develop, test, deploy, and maintain implementations of quantum algorithms and integrations with quantum computing libraries.
  • Support researchers at Zapata who use the tools that you build.

Team values

  • Cooperation in prioritization of work, science, and engineering decisions.
  • Team success as a shared accomplishment with diversity in thought and experience.
  • Open communication and willingness to respectfully engage with others.

Minimum qualifications

  • Currently enrolled undergraduate in computer science, physics, math, or related technical field.
  • Experience developing software for technical computing, especially in Python.

Preferred qualifications

  • Familiarity with fundamental concepts in quantum computing.
  • Familiarity with quantum computing frameworks such as Qiskit, Cirq, pyQuil, or Q#.
  • Experience developing complex technical software as part of a team.
  • Someone who is a self-starter, adaptable and ready to learn
