A Day in the Life

Our mandate is to develop the current and next generation Graphical User Interface for the Medtronic robotic-assisted surgical system. You will be involved in designing or architecting new GUI features for existing or future releases of the product, fix software defects, and other software development activities. You may be called upon for cross-team software development or support. Software development will be in the context of a large medical manufacturing environment. Beyond writing code — you will contribute to reviewing requirements, running unit tests, hiring, mentoring junior engineers, helping to solve build issues, integrating the GUI with other subsystems, participating in software reviews, and collaborating with other groups.

You will:

  • Work within the Medtronic-Covidien R&D engineering team to develop and support the launch and next-generation robotically assisted minimally invasive surgical platform.
  • Focus on graphical user interface software development.
  • Translate written requirements into a well-designed implementation.

Must have: Minimum qualifications :

Bachelor’s Degree and minimum of 2 year’s of experience, or Advanced Degree and 0 year’s of experience.

Nice to have:

  • A BS in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Biomedical Engineering, or Equivalent Degree with appropriate experience. MS preferred.
  • 2-5 years of software development experience; an ideal candidate has some practical programming experience delivering a real product and responding to feature requests and defect reports.
  • Recent experience implementing GUIs, and/or graphics and rendering.
  • Moderate level of C++ (C++11 or newer) experience.
  • Experience with Qt and/or QML is a plus.
  • Proficiency in developing software on Linux and Windows.
  • The ability to write and communicate ideas clearly in correspondences and design documents.
  • A commitment to solid, high-quality code; experience with writing, running, and analyzing unit tests preferred.
  • Experience with revision control software such as git.
  • Exposure to QNX or embedded operating systems a plus.
  • A passion for learning new things and enjoys variety.
  • Excellent communication and team skills.

Working Conditions:

Standard Office setting in the Seaport District of Boston, MA. Work within a commutable distance to our Boston/Seaport office with the option to work remotely a portion of the week, as business needs allow.
This position requires a person to be able to lift small loads and maneuver in laboratory spaces for accessing the robotically-assisted surgical equipment when testing.
Work travel is anticipated to be low for this position; some travel may be required for software design, collaboration, meetings, or debugging offsite.
