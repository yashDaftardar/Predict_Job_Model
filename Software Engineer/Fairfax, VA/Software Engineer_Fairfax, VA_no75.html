Who we are:
Raytheon BBN Technologies (BBN) is seeking a Software Engineer to join our world-class group in Arlington, VA. Our team of scientists and programmers is working with a broad range of government agencies to resolve exciting, technically challenging problems of national importance.
We offer an exciting, fast paced, collaborative environment, with the opportunity to contribute to innovative applied research. Career growth is encouraged through support for publications and participation in leading research conferences, training, mentoring, and tuition reimbursement.
The ideal candidate will have excellent verbal and written communication skills, be highly motivated, self-directed, and comfortable working in an agile development environment.

BBN Physical Sciences & Systems, Maritime Systems
The BBN Maritime Systems team is an industry-leading group of applied physicists and mathematicians, computer scientists, and mechanical and electrical engineers who work in undersea and cross-domain sensing; positioning, navigation, and timing (PNT); and communications for maritime applications. We work collaboratively across three offices. Our extensive background in undersea sensing, including acoustical, magnetic, and gravity modalities, provides a basis for this work. We analyze and model propagation in complex environments, evaluate background noise, clutter sources and characteristics, and develop sensor elements, systems, platform integration, and related signal processing to enhance system performance for specific applications.
The Arlington, Virginia maritime team is focused on advanced systems to support the operational Navies of the US and UK. We develop systems and algorithms that exploit acoustic and other signals for detection, localization, and tracking of undersea threats.

  • Design and implement user interfaces for data visualization.

  • Software requirements development.
  • Work with a team in a professional software development environment.

  • Comfortable working within Windows and Linux (RHEL) environments including knowledge of the Linux command-line and shell scripts.
