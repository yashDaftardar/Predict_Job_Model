Full Job Description

The Motley Fool is searching for early-career software engineers who are excited to work with an award-winning company in a professional and fun setting. If you’re ready to grow your SWE skillset in an environment that will foster your professional growth, provide mentorship from skilled SWE’s, and provide applicable real-world experience within a high-growth company, then this could be the perfect opportunity.


The Motley Fool’s purpose is to make the world smarter, happier and richer. We primarily do that by delivering world-class investing advice and solutions, and empowering people to take charge of their future. With this Software Engineering Development Program, we see another opportunity to make the world smarter, happier and richer. We believe that there’s an underserved community of talented engineers who have limited access to a real-world software development setting. We’d like to do something about that.


Tell me more about this SWE Development Program

Internally, this program is called FOLD, or Fool-On Learning & Development. It’s our take on an experience that should provide ample room to learn and grow into a confident, capable, and productive software engineer. We have structured a 6 month program with capacity for 10 aspiring Fools that will include mentorship, guided self-learning, directed learning (from Fools and others), and an opportunity for hands-on experience with 5 development teams. We’ve run iterations of this program for many years and with great success. Many of our current Tech Leaders are graduates of this internal program. We’re excited to launch FOLD to find and develop future Fool leaders.


Is this SWE Development Program right for me?

The Fool believes this program will be best suited for software engineers with roughly 0-2 years experience. We say “zero” because we understand that many engineers get their start in many different ways. Some started by tinkering with their family computer as a young child, some go to school for computer science, some switch careers and enroll in a boot camp, and others take on the tech in their current role as a “side job”. All of these descriptions are fitting of one or more thriving and excelling engineers at the Fool and we think it likely describes many of our future engineers.


What happens after the structured 6 month program ends?

At the end of the 6 month program, we will extend full-time offers to the engineers who have demonstrated the coding skills necessary to contribute to our current SWE teams, as well as other key attributes that make successful, long-term Fools: adaptable, communicative, collaborative and competitive spirit, honest, innovative, and who aren’t afraid to have fun amidst all of our hard work.

While our earnest hope is to extend offers to all of our FOLD participants, we may find at any point during this program that this isn’t a good match- for you, for us, or for both. Our promise to you: we will give you honest feedback at every step so you know where you stand, and whether you continue with The Motley Fool or not, you will have been given access to a SWE curriculum and SWE mentorship that will be valuable no matter where you land.

Are FOLD participants paid while going through the program?

Yes! This is a full-time commitment, so throughout the entire duration of your program, you'll receive a competitive salary and be eligible for our world-class benefits. FOLD Program participants will receive an annualized salary of $80,000 USD.
