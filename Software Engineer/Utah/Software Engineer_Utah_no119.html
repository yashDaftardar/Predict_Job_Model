Full Job Description

The IAM Software Design Engineer is a primary force in designing and implementing solutions to transform the way the University of Utah and University of Utah Health achieve our missions. This role will require a thinker, a creator, a dreamer. In this role, you’ll work closely with other IAM Software Design Engineers, and you’ll take on projects that are as challenging as you can handle. This role is a leader in the effort to improve our security and privacy efforts and digitally transform both. As part of the Information Security Office, security is always on your mind, and you’re constantly looking for ways to improve the way we work. You find problems and fix them, within the standards of a large and mature organization. You’ll work with stakeholders to identify business needs and then with other IT and security colleagues to design the best possible solutions for their requests. You find ways to improve on their ideas, and you’re not happy with a satisfied customer – you want a delighted customer. You find ways to do that in safe and secure ways. This position will require some after-hours/weekend work to accommodate the change schedules permitted by the business but allows the right person the location and scheduling flexibility and the support to build a work-life integration that fits. The ideal candidate is passionate about this work, a self-starter, capable of working closely with others, including in geographically dispersed environments. You’ll work with a team of very smart, very capable colleagues, a wide variety of world class tools, and you’ll do it for a leading health care and higher education entity. We’ll push you to show your skills and to improve them, and we’ll invest in you and help you succeed.

About UIT: University Information Technology (UIT), the central IT service provider for campus, reports to the Chief Information Officer and is responsible for many of the University of Utah’s most critical common IT resources including the campus network; the Campus Information Services (CIS) portal; UMail, telephone, and online collaboration services; high performance and research computing; information security; teaching and learning technologies; software licensing; and a host of other systems and services. For more information about UIT visit http://www.it.utah.edu.

About the University: Located in Salt Lake City, in the foothills of the Wasatch Mountains, the University of Utah is the flagship institution of the State of Utah’s system of higher education and a member of the PAC-12 Conference. Salt Lake City combines the amenities of a major metropolitan area of more than one million people with the friendliness and ease of living of a small, Western city. Seven major ski resorts are within an hour’s drive from campus, and opportunities to pursue activities from biking to hiking to fishing abound. Salt Lake is also home to the Utah Symphony and Opera, the Utah Ballet, several professional sports teams, and a wide range of other cultural and recreational activities. http://www.employment.utah.edu/staff/work.php

This is a Category IIA position: Not required on campus and able to telework during the duration of the University’s COVID-19 response. Employees and/or functions where employees are able to work remotely in order to maintain campus systems, campus operations, clinical operations, and patient care.

Responsibilities
  • Design and support creative, transformative solutions that improve our ability to provide world-class health care and higher education. Solve real-world problems affecting real people using world-class tools and current technologies including IGA and PAM market leaders.
  • Support the day-to-day and maintenance of IAM platform solutions that meet business requirements, policies, and standards
  • Prepare accurate and detailed requirement specifications documents, including workflows, processes, requirements, and gap analysis
  • Follow the business approval processes to test, validate, and evaluate new applications and functions to ensure information adheres to requirements and business needs
  • System administration for Identity and Privileged Access Management solution
  • Troubleshoot IAM systems/components
  • This list is not inclusive – you will be asked to create, to solve, to troubleshoot, to collaborate. You will be asked to bring everything you have to this role, and we’ll try to help you become even better

Minimum Qualifications

Requires a bachelor’s degree in a related area or equivalency and 2-4 years of experience in the field or in a related area.

Department Specific Qualifications:
  • Bachelor’s degree or equivalent in computer science, information technology or related field
  • 2+ years industry experience (Java, JavaScript, Angular JS, XML, System Administration, Support, Cloud Administration, etc.)
  • Strong knowledge of software development lifecycle
  • Understanding of cybersecurity/Identity & Access Management
  • Web and Application Servers
  • Experience with relational databases
  • Extremely proficient at working with user base while guiding them in best practices
  • Solid understanding of data analysis techniques
  • Experience managing Incidents, Service Request and Changes within defined SLAs
  • Absolute passion for creating, helping, learning, protecting, and solving.

Applicants must demonstrate the potential ability to perform the essential functions of the job as outlined in the position description.

Preferences

The following are helpful, but not required:

  • Experience with OS, Application servers, ERP, etc.
  • API programming experience (REST) and OAuth 2.0
  • Experience with directory management such LDAP/Active Directory solution
  • Experience managing Incidents, Service Request and Changes within defined SLAs
  • Experience with Identity & Privileged Access Management in automating access control processes such as User provisioning, Onboarding, Role-Based Access Control (RBAC), Attribute-Based Access Control (ABAC), authorization models, Single Sign-On(SSO), Active Directory
