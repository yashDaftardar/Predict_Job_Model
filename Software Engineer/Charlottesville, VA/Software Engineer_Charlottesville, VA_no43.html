The Web Programmer Analyst


The Albemarle County Public Schools Department of Technology is actively recruiting a Web Programmer Analyst to join our team. The successful candidate will be responsible for the visual presentation, development, maintenance, functionality, design, and performance of School Division web resources including the school division's public facing web presence, internal web sites, teacher and student web sites, and custom web applications. Research new and emerging opportunities in electronic communications including content management systems, interactive Web applications, social media technologies, web accessibility, and services for both internal and public sites. Provides leadership, consultation, documentation, staff development and technical support services to staff in the areas of troubleshooting and technical guidance for all web technologies. Responsible for the determination of user requirements and for the high-level design of complex web-based systems. Works collaboratively with the Database Programmer Analyst to build custom solutions to support division initiatives.


The Expectations

Essential functions and responsibilities of Web Programmer Analyst include, but are not limited to, the following:

  • Plans and maintains the division's Internet presence.
  • Administers and provides user support for division content management systems.
  • Plans and maintains a division-wide intranet, allowing data manipulation for each internal staff member.
  • Develops and maintains new and existing web applications.
  • Develops and maintains database-driven web interfaces for rapid, real-time information sharing.
  • Plans and manages user permissions for web resources.
  • Designs and implements responsive, standards-based, accessible, user-focused templates and interfaces.
  • Performs testing of new web applications and systems, engaging users as necessary.
  • Gathers user requirements and feedback to formulate requirements for new or changed web applications and systems.
  • Develops web portals that allow users to input and retrieve accurate information.
  • Assists in deploying, managing, and maintaining web servers.
  • Manages large, multi-user projects and implementations of new systems.
  • Communicates project status, issues, and resolutions to others.
  • Conducts user acceptance testing and reports results.

Performs related tasks as required.


THE QUALIFICATIONS


Education and Experience


Bachelor's degree and three years related experience or equivalent combination of education and experience required. Experience managing multi-user content management systems (CMS) preferred. Experience with integrating SQL databases into web applications preferred. Experience with Microsoft SQL Server 2008 and/or 2016 and Microsoft SQL Server Reporting Services (SSRS) desired. Experience with setup and administration of SharePoint Farms, Web applications and site collections preferred.


Knowledge, Skills and Abilities

Experience with administration and support of content management systems. Experience with web technologies including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Angular JS, AJAX, IIS, ASP.NET, Web Services, Claims-Based Authentication, and XML. Experience with WordPress hosted on IIS with MySQL 5.1+ and PHP 5.3+; familiarity with the management of multisite WordPress environments. A solid understanding of Web Accessibility standards (WCAG 2.0, Section 504) and best practices. Experience building web applications to read and write to relational databases, including experience with Microsoft SQL Server. Knowledge of SharePoint 2016 on-premises including: the development and deployment SharePoint solutions, the coding of web parts, the SharePoint Object Model, Web Services, Timer Jobs, etc. Experience in developing on the SharePoint platform for internal users by creating lists and page content types, master page and content page layouts, themes, workflows, etc. using Visual Studio 2010 or 2017, SharePoint Designer and/or C#. Familiarity with Microsoft PowerShell and PowerShell scripting. Must have excellent written and oral communication skills. Project management experience with an eagerness to learn and apply new technologies. Demonstrated training and presentation skills are highly desirable. Demonstrated organizational and problem-solving skills. Must be able to respond and address user requests with diplomacy and tact. The ability to work independently, react quickly and work effectively under pressure. Demonstrated ability to learn new computer hardware and software. Accuracy, attention to detail, and the ability to work quickly are essential. Demonstrated ability to work as a member of a team and to coordinate the efforts of others. Ability to plan, organize and schedule complex projects. Ability to lead a large user-group including guidance in implementing new products, site design and architecture.


THE PHYSICAL CONDITIONS AND NATURE OF WORK CONTACTS

Work is typically performed in an office, computer room, computer classroom laboratory, and/or conference rooms. Continuous ability to communicate by phone or email is necessary. Regular use of computers and input devices is necessary. Occasional operation of county vehicles to travel to locations throughout the county is required. Regular contact is made with internal departments and senior persons at all levels of county government and the school division. Contacts are usually cooperative, however, require tact and diplomacy. External contacts with vendor companies and other information services agencies are necessary. Occasional need to perform urgent tasks to meet organizational deadlines. Occasional work activity after normal duty hours and occasional weekend duties are necessary.
