What does a Wireless Software Engineer at Corning do?

This is a meaningful role where you’ll participate in the design, development and maintenance of embedded software for 3G/LTE radio access network and core network protocols and subsystems. We want someone with a strong work ethic who is responsive and brings strong attention to detail.


What does a typical day look like for a Wireless Software Engineer?

You will…

  • Work on the integration of radio interface functions with policy, session management and configuration subsystems.
  • Support internal test and QA teams and external field support engineers to complete interoperability and acceptance testing in wireless operator networks.
  • Participate in architecture, design and code reviews and provide peer feedback.
  • Debug field issues through log analysis and other field data

What does career growth potential look like?

  • Potential career paths include but are not limited to progression into a Technical Lead role or Management, etc. based on the individual.

What Qualifications do we look for?

  • If you have a Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Science, Telecommunications (or related field) and bring 2+ years’ experience in embedded C/C++ programming for design and development of communication protocols OR New Grad with advanced Degree and solid in C/C++ programming, you sound like a great fit!
  • Knowledge of 3GPP specifications, particularly RRC & stellar debugging skills!
  • Good understanding and track record in development experience with UMTS and/or LTE radio interface protocols.
  • Bring hands-on experience with layered cellular protocol stack implementation on resource constrained embedded processors using multi-threaded programming model
  • A proven ability to use debug tools and knowledge of runtime environment to efficiently isolate and fix bugs.
  • It’s a Plus if you bring knowledge of NAS, S1AP, 3GPP LTE E-UTRAN and Evolved Packet System
  • Working knowledge of IP protocol stack.
