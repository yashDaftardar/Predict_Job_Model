Full Job Description

Minimum qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree in a technical discipline or equivalent practical experience.
  • 2 years of experience in testing the security of software systems.
  • 2 years of experience in software security analysis, code auditing, and product reviews.

Preferred qualifications:

  • Experience in one or more of the following languages: Java, C/C++, C#, Go, Objective C, Python, JavaScript.
  • Experience in analyzing and responding to vulnerabilities.
  • Experience in security vulnerability testing and security evaluation (Web, apps, APIs).
  • Technical knowledge of Web technologies, software services, authentication protocols, and network security.
  • Effective interpersonal and communication skills.

About the job

Software systems and services are a critical area in the Devices and Services product areas. This is an opportunity to define a high, standard software security bar for multiple products and tools developed by this area. As a Product Security Engineer, you will be able to set the technical direction of our software security program, identify, develop and customize tools for the job, conduct security research, and drive the adoption of best practices throughout the organization to ensure that web, mobile app, and backend software are secure.

The goal of the Devices & Services Security and Privacy team (DSSP) is to ensure that all business units within our Product Area, including Google Nest, Devices & Services and Stadia, develop and maintain trustworthy products.

Our team drives the security development lifecycle of new products in collaboration with product/engineering teams. We review/test software services, on-device software/firmware, Web interfaces, apps, and components to detect and fix vulnerabilities that could allow bad actors to compromise our users’ data or Google systems. Our team also builds software security technologies, co-designs features and services, supports vulnerabilities and incident response, and provides technical consulting services to a number of teams.

Responsibilities

  • Conduct threat modeling, design reviews, and code reviews for software services, APIs, infrastructure components, and tools.
  • Plan and execute functional security testing and vulnerability testing and update testing plans to ensure known vulnerabilities will not resurface.
  • Analyze vulnerabilities, rate their severity, propose and review fixes.
  • Provide technical guidance to ensure that product features and services are built with security in mind.
  • Respond to incidents in collaboration with other product, security, and privacy teams at Google.
