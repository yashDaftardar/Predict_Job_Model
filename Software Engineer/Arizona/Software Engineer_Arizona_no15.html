Job Description


Intel's Logic Technology Development (LTD) is looking for a Software Modeling Engineer who will be working on a dynamic team that develops and applies software tools, scripts, and physical models to analyze microprocessors, chip-package interactions, and semiconductor process for thermo-mechanical failure modes and reliability.

Software Modeling engineer is responsible for (but not limited) developing software flows, finite-element models, physical models, and data analyses to evaluate issues encountered in cutting-edge technology development. Also, you will collaborate in a cross-functional team including experimental, product integration, process integration, and quality and reliability teams to solve thermo-mechanical problems and help define industry-leading products.

The candidate should also exhibit the following behavioral traits:

  • Effectively communicate, collaborate and align with team members, customers, and partnership skills

  • Team player, willing to work in a cross-functional environment

  • information to create a focused plan of action considering trade-offs

  • Demonstrated data analytics skills to communicate complex data in a simple, actionable way

This is an entry level position and will be compensated accordingly.


Qualifications


You must possess the below minimum qualifications to be initially considered for this position. Preferred qualifications are in addition to the minimum requirements and are considered a plus factor in identifying top candidates. Knowledge and/or experience listed below would be obtained through a combination of your school, work and/or classes and/or research and/or relevant previous job and/or internship experiences.


Minimum Qualifications:

  • The candidate must possess Master’s or PhD degree in Mechanical Engineering, Materials Science, Physics, or a related field.

  • Minimum of 1 year experience in numerical modeling in one or more areas including: stress/strain in solids, fracture mechanics, materials behavior, thermal transport and heat transfer.

  • Minimum of 1 year experience in programming or scripting languages such as C/C++, Python, MATLAB.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Minimum of 6 months of experience in:
  • working knowledge of semiconductor process and packaging technology
  • working with finite-element packages such as ABAQUS, ANSYS, COMSOL, etc.
  • Minimum of 1 year experience in numerical methods such as Finite element, Finite difference, and spectral methods
  • Proven track record of academic research, with relevant 1st author publications
