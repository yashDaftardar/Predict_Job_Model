Are you an ambitious systems engineer who enjoys working on progressive new product design? If so, we may have the perfect opportunity for you! As a result of our continued growth and commitment to innovation, this newly created position will be an opportunity to contribute to many of our exciting initiatives.

ASSA ABLOY is the worldwide leader in access solutions. As Embedded Systems Engineer, you will work with product management and a cross-functional engineering team to design system outlines and technical specifications for current and future products. In addition, the Embedded Systems Engineer will verify that any changes made to the product firmware do not negatively impact the stability and user experience of the product.


What you will be doing


The Embedded Systems Engineer will facilitate successful collaboration between engineering, product management, and other key stakeholders through the full life cycle of our products. Specific functions will include:


  • Lead product specification development for new and existing products.
  • Work with product development teams on overall system design to meet requirements for software and firmware products.
  • Document the system design of current products to be used as the basis for future product development.
  • Serve as the subject matter expert on product firmware behavior; train other team members on architectural best practices and system intricacies.
  • Apply advanced technologies, scientific principles, theories and concepts to develop system solutions to accomplish long-term goals and objectives.
  • Investigate system, software, and firmware issues and provide detailed analysis reports.


What we are looking for


The ideal candidate will have at least 5 years of experience in product development or system design, and will be proficient with embedded technology languages. This role requires excellent communication skills and the ability to work effectively with internal and external partners. Additional qualifications include:


  • Bachelor’s degree in Engineering, Computer Science, or similar field.
  • Familiarity with firmware development, testing, and test automation development.
  • Ability to set up and troubleshoot wired and wireless networks.
  • Understanding of embedded firmware development and requisite programming languages (C, C++, or similar).
  • Knowledge of the software development lifecycle (Agile/SCRUM).
  • Ability to manage one’s own projects, schedules, and priorities.
  • Proficient in Microsoft Office Suite (Word, Excel, Power Point).
