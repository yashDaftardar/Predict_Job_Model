Job Description

The research team at Detect designs and builds the engine behind our scalable diagnostic platform for COVID-19 and other molecular targets. We envision a testing platform so simple that it can be ultimately used in the home, allowing individuals to self-test on a routine basis and monitor their health & wellness over time.

Developing next-generation molecular diagnostic testing platforms requires extensive laboratory experimentation and data generation. Laboratory automation and operations engineers give our scientists superpowers by automating manual processes and allowing our experimental platforms to scale beyond human capacity, processing hundreds to thousands of samples each day. You are responsible for integrating commercial and custom hardware and software to create those end-to-end automated workflows that will accelerate our research efforts. You feel at home working alongside robotic liquid handling platforms and conceiving of integrated processes that encompass everything from walk-up use to data handling and analysis. Engineers at Detect have a deep passion for laboratory operations and love knowing that they can apply their domain expertise to improve the scale and overall efficiency of our research program. You enjoy communicating about your work and are eager to closely collaborate with interdisciplinary teams of scientists and engineers that will be the end users of the automated platforms you create. You search for creative solutions to the problems you encounter. You feel at home in a fast-paced pre-commercial startup and are comfortable with rolling up your sleeves to contribute in any capacity.

As part of our team, your core responsibilities will be to:

  • Leverage commercial and customized hardware and software platforms to automate experimental workflows that scale the technology team's ability to test molecular diagnostic assays and devices with high throughput
  • Validate, QC and regularly maintain automated platforms/systems
  • Regularly engage automation end users to consult on workflows, best practices and system improvements that further enhance application performance
  • Work with your team to troubleshoot root causes of automation system errors, escalating serious concerns and ensuring prompt issue resolution
  • Collaborate with software engineers and developers to write programs that drive laboratory instrument operation and automate data acquisition, retrieval, and processing and integration with electronic laboratory notebook systems.
  • Independently plan projects and subtasks such that milestones and resources may be monitored and actively tracked.
  • Prepare presentations and present data to an interdisciplinary technology team with diverse scientific backgrounds

The distinction from Engineer I to Engineer IV includes an increased ability to work independently, to manage multiple workstreams, and to draw upon in-depth knowledge in operations technology and share impactful technical insights. A Laboratory Automation and Operations engineer at the Engineer IV level is expected to develop innovative solutions to technical challenges, independently plan, track and execute on complex projects that meaningfully impact the technology program and possess the capability of leading project teams.
