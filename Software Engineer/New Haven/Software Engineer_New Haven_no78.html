Full Job Description

Raptor Scientific, a Connecticut-based aerospace test system manufacturer has a full-time position for a software programmer in its engineering department. We are seeking a highly skilled, object-oriented software programmer capable of coding in the visual languages. This position is for those with working experience in machine control, sensor data acquisition and processing.

Who are we?

· A small, healthy company with an avant-garde culture featuring flexible work hours.

· A world leading high technology manufacturer and solutions provider.

· A creative, diverse, and focused group of industry experts who embrace an entrepreneurial spirit.

Who are we looking for?

  • Required
  • Demonstrated minimum of 3 years proficiency in industrial or scientific based visual object-oriented programming.
  • AS or BS Degree in Computer Engineering, Computer Sciences , or similar
  • Knowledge of Microsoft Windows OS setup and configuration management.
  • Strong working knowledge of common industrial automation and control interface hardware
  • Preferred
  • Microsoft C# , Visual Basic, LabVIEW
  • Strong mathematics background with good problem solving and analytical skills.
  • Familiarity with the basics of electronics theory , electronic test-equipment use, and testing methods.

What will you do?

  • Software development and debugging for machine automation, data acquisition, and data processing of aerospace test instruments.
  • Electro-mechanical testing and troubleshooting of software-controlled machine systems and sub systems.
  • System performance checkout of instrumentation
  • Configure deliverable computer systems to operate with new and existing system.
  • Software development support for system upgrades and improvements.
  • Travel to customer site for installation of new equipment as well as service and support for previously installed equipment.

How will you grow?

· You will get the chance to be creative. To apply this creativity toward harmonizing software with mathematics, electronics, and precision mechanics ; with the goal of pushing the envelope of what has been previously done.

· To learn new things; expand and improve your skillset .

· To make a difference . To make your ideas heard by working within a small company.

· You are given the opportunity to succeed .

· To be part of something larger . You will get to work with companies in “New Space” as well as established giants like Boeing, Lockheed, and the National Laboratories.

· To work in an interesting and diverse range of end-use markets and applications

What are we like?

· We are individuals heavily focused on “the goal” with little ancillary distraction.

· We have a culture of problem-solving with focus on solutions, not blame.

· Our management team is very accessible.

· We are not micromanagers - You are given individual or small collaborative group ownership of tasks.

· We support each other to succeed . A company driven to achieve common goals while sharing in the responsibilities.

Competitive salary is commensurate with qualifications and experience.
