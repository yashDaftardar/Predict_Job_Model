Introduction
The operational models built to support static, on-premise systems are increasingly under strain by the proliferation of cloud technologies that introduce many challenges relating to scale and complexity. Businesses of all shapes and sizes recognize that the way they observe and react to their workloads in the new cloud paradigm requires a unique perspective supported by modern habits and techniques necessary to affect that change.

To support the needs of our clients, we’re seeking experienced Site Reliability Engineers who have lived the transformation process, understand the tenets of SRE, and have the skills necessary to teach and mentor using a co-creation model.

Your Role and Responsibilities
You will have the unique opportunity to work with IBM’s largest clients to demonstrate the habits, methods and tools that make critical systems more resilient, automated, and self-healing.

Working in an innovative and highly effective co-create model, you will provide solutions and guidance on a wide range of topics. Operational Readiness, ChatOps, Day 2 Operations, AI for IT Operations, and Build to Manage are a sampling of the operations management topics that we address for our clients as they evolve their operations teams into a modern DevOps/SRE model.

AI is critical to the future of IT Operations, and IBM has an industry leading solution that drastically improves Incident and Problem identification. You will be hands on and a trailblazer in this fast growing and highly impactful new market segment.

Your role in the IBM Garage and the work you do will heavily influence a client’s eagerness to purchase IBM solutions and services. Your talent and expertise will aid in delivering meaningful outcomes in the form of proof points using IBM platform, software, and professional services.IBMReferred_NorthAmerica

Required Technical and Professional Expertise
  • At least 3 years performing formally in the role of Site Reliability Engineer
  • At least 5 years of large-scale systems management experience
  • At least 5 years working with complex architectures that include cloud, containers, cluster management and microservices
  • Knowledge of and ability to competently use modern programming languages (Examples: Python, Go, JAVA)

Preferred Technical and Professional Expertise
  • Demonstrable knowledge of AI for IT Operations (AIOps)
  • Demonstrable knowledge of IT Service Management Frameworks (ITIL, COBIT, Lean IT, etc.)
  • Demonstrable knowledge of IBM Software relating to IT Service Management (Netcool, Agile Service Manager, Predictive Insights, MultiCloud Manager, etc.)
