Principal C++ Software Engineer - Common Libraries

Motional
Las Vegas, NV

Our Principal Software Engineers play a pivotal role in our autonomous vehicle stack, leading the process of developing reliable and well-designed libraries for use throughout the system. Implement and optimize cutting-edge algorithms in high-performance, production-grade C++. Work in a complex and growing software ecosystem where new features are being added and evaluated rapidly and cross-functional collaboration is essential.

The Motional Las Vegas office is located less than 10 miles from the Las Vegas strip at 730 E Pilot Road, the Las Vegas office is home to one of the largest fleets of self-driving vehicles. The city's grid system of roads and being one of America's "smart cities" allows for extensive research and development testing.

What You'll Do:

  • Use your experience and expertise to help the whole team develop better software practices, skills, and capabilities.
  • Collaborate with experts throughout the organization to design the architecture and interfaces for libraries that will sit at the core of the autonomous driving stack.
  • Explore different options for algorithm implementations, balancing performance optimization with the need for stable and maintainable code.
  • Write clear and thorough design documentation that can satisfy regulatory requirements in a safety-critical field.
  • Help develop practices for keeping the code base robust and scalable and avoiding the buildup of technical debt.
  • Show initiative and be a valued team member in our innovative, fast-paced, entrepreneurial environment where cooperative teamwork is the norm.

What You'll Bring:

  • Fluency in C++14 or higher, including a deep understanding of how the compiler implements templates, inheritance, linking, etc.
  • Familiarity with modern multithreading techniques and ability to reason through possible execution sequences to ensure that your code is thread-safe.
  • Strong mathematical background and the ability to implement and optimize complex algorithms and data structures.
  • Experience with modern software development practices (agile development, git, unit testing, Doxygen).
  • Ability to constantly learn new things and to teach others.
  • Experience with radar, lidar, computer vision, point cloud processing, object tracking, computational geometry, and/or machine learning is a plus.
