Job Title: Engineer I, Automation
Requisition ID: 155744BR

When you’re part of the team at Thermo Fisher Scientific, you’ll do important work, like helping customers in finding cures for cancer, protecting the environment or making sure our food is safe. Your work will have real-world impact, and you’ll be supported in achieving your career goals.

Location/Division Specific Information

Viral Vector Services, Alachua, FL

How will you make an impact?

The Automation Engineer is professional support staff for instrumentation and controls of manufacturing equipment, integration of equipment into enterprise applications (data historian, batch automation, and manufacturing execution system), and site continuous improvement efforts to achieve Pharma 4.0 goals. This includes the design, implementation, and documentation of automation projects and their lifecycle management in compliance with FDA and EU regulations. Identifies and resolves automation issues on a timely basis to avoid business interruption and make recommendations to management.

What will you do?

  • Work in a safe and responsible manner to create an injury-free and incident-free workplace.
  • Actively participate on project teams contributing as the automation subject matter expert regarding system capabilities and system design decisions.
  • Support the generation and execution of Validation life cycle documentation such as Systems Commissioning, Installation Qualification (IQ), Operational Qualification (OQ), Performance Qualification (PQ), Design Qualification (DQ) Validation Summary Reports, and Deviations reports for Manufacturing and process Equipment & Utilities as per Thermo Fisher guidelines.
  • Provide management with recommendations on technical advances to achieve company goals and maximize productivity.
  • Provide guidance and support in diagnosing and resolving difficult automation hardware, software, instrument, and process problems to maintain system readiness. Frequently, this effort requires collaboration with other site maintenance disciplines.
  • Design and develop system databases and application code requiring interlocking and sequencing not easily obtained through normal controls.
  • Ensure complete and detailed specifications and related technical documentation is provided that clearly defines user needs and system functionality for new automation systems or modifications to existing systems.
  • Provide supporting data from automation systems to support production or quality department requests.
  • Perform other duties as assigned.

How will you get here?


Education Requirements

  • Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Science, Engineering or related discipline

Required Skills
  • 1+ years of experience in the biotech engineering/manufacturing environment. Equivalent combinations of education, training, and relevant work experience may be considered.
  • Some experience with DCS and/or PLC coding
Preferred Skills
  • Experience with systems such as Syncade, OSI PI, Infobatch and Labwatch
  • Proficient with reading and creating schematics through AutoCAD
  • Experience with motors, controllers and power supplies

Knowledge, Skills, Abilities

  • Strong communication skills both written and verbal, including technical writing
  • Ability to work on multiple projects simultaneously.
  • Ability to read and interpret electrical schematics
  • Ability to work as a team with maintenance and production to resolve issues and provide guidance to prevent recurrence.
  • Ability to work independently, prioritize, multi-task and complete work according to schedules. Flexibility in the face of rapidly-changing demands.
  • Work Hours: 8:00 am to 5:00 pm. This position requires overtime, early mornings and weekends/holidays, as needed. Flexibility is required for schedule.
