Full Job Description

Company:

This is a great opportunity to join Dycom’s IT Department as a Software Engineering Manager.

Dycom is a leading provider of specialty contracting services to the telecommunications and infrastructure industry. These services, which are provided throughout the United States and in Canada, include engineering, construction, maintenance and installation services to telecommunications providers, underground facility locating services to various utilities, including telecommunications providers, and other construction and maintenance services to electric and gas utilities and others. Dycom’s IT environment is constantly changing, growing to meet the needs of our subsidiary companies and their customers. If you are looking for a career to work with a great group of people and have a real impact on the business, then Dycom’s IT Department is the place for you.

Overview:

The Software Engineering Manager role is to ensure the streamlined operation of the technology organization in alignment with the business objectives of the organization. This individual will plan, coordinate, direct, and design Technology-related activities of the organization, as well as provide administrative direction and support for daily operational activities of the department. The Technology Manager will work closely with decision makers in the business and other technology portfolios to identify, recommend, develop, implement, and support cost-effective technology solutions for all aspects of the organization.

Responsibilities:

  • Lead portfolio operational and strategic planning, including fostering innovation, planning projects, and organizing and negotiating the allocation of resources.
  • Responsible for ongoing resource allocation tracking.
  • Benchmark, analyze, report on, and make recommendations for the improvement and growth of technology within the organization.
  • Research and drive technology direction.
  • Independently manage and develop the customer and vendor relationships, ensuring complete trust through extremely effective communication.
  • Effectively allocate matrix resources to inter-team projects as needed.
  • Create and implement day to day technology solutions to business problems.
  • Independently develop business case justifications, cost/benefit analyses and budget for technology spending and initiatives.
  • Negotiate and administer vendor, outsourcer, consultant contracts, and service agreements.
  • Independently manage the deployment, monitoring, maintenance, development, upgrade, and support of all systems, such as software systems, servers, PCs, and operating systems.
  • Consistently work with stakeholders to define business and systems requirements for new technology implementations.
  • Keep current with the latest technologies.
  • Oversee projects within multiple portfolio areas.
  • Practice asset management for technology hardware, software, and equipment.
  • Coach, mentor and manage technology staffing, including supervision of managers and technology staff, scheduling, development, evaluation, and disciplinary actions.
  • Establish and maintain regular written and in-person communications with the organization’s business system owners, department heads, and end users regarding pertinent technology activities.
  • Partner with the project management office to help develop and adhere to SDLC standards, guidelines and procedures.
  • Display proper management behaviors in all scenarios.
Qualifications:
  • Bachelor’s degree in computer science or management information systems and/or 5+ years equivalent work experience.
  • Strong technical knowledge of software systems within sphere of responsibility.
  • Strong knowledge of current technology standards and trends in area of responsibility.
  • Extensive technology support experience with business system owners.
  • At least 2 years experience managing multiple direct reports.
  • Proven history of vendor contract negotiation and relationship management.
  • Proven experience in technology planning and development.
  • Drive implementation of the organization’s goals and objectives.
  • Strong understanding of human resource management principles, practices, and procedures.
  • Strong understanding of project management principles.
