Northrop Grumman Mission Systems is actively recruiting a Cyber Software Engineer team member to support a Cyber Security program based in Tampa, Florida.

You will be working with our experienced engineering team developing workflows to support Department of Defense Cyber efforts. These workflows are tailored engineering efforts for networking products integrating current and future cyber systems.

We're looking for a highly motivated individual with an impeccable work ethic and a strong ability to work in a collaborative fast-moving dynamic team environment. The primary team will be located in Tampa, Florida. Our ability to communicate / coordinate highly technical requirements with team members is a priority due to competing priorities and demanding deadlines. Ideally, the candidate should be familiar with the use of various tools to enable a DevSecOps lifecycle, to include build, release and configuration management, and has demonstrated the ability to move DevSecOps processes from concept to implementation. You should be willing to work in a team environment using a Scrum/Agile methodology. You should be able to work within a strict change management environment with the ability to document and test all changes prior to implementation. You will perform technical, operational, and security risk analysis for new changes and technologies and articulate risks in a succinct manner. You will be expected to quickly adapt new technologies to avoid limitations and obsolescence of both tools and systems. This person designs, develops, documents, tests and debugs applications software and systems that contain logical and mathematical solutions. They will conduct multidisciplinary research and collaborate with equipment designers and/or hardware engineers in the planning, design, development, and utilization of electronic data processing systems for product and commercial software. They will determine computer user needs; analyzes system capabilities to resolve problems on program intent, output requirements, input data acquisition, programming techniques and controls; prepares operating instructions; designs and develops compilers and assemblers, utility programs, and operating systems. The will ensure software standards are met.
You should apply general frequent use and application of technical standards, principles, theories, concepts and techniques and demonstrate the skill and ability to perform moderately complex professional tasks. You should be able to provide solutions to a variety of technical problems of increasing scope and complexity as assigned. Your work will be reviewed for soundness of technical judgment, overall adequacy and accuracy. You will work under general supervision. You will contribute to the completion of milestones associated with specific projects.

  • Familiar with Application Containerization (e.g. Docker)

  • Automation testing (e.g. Selenium, Unit)
  • Software Containerization (e.g. Docker)
