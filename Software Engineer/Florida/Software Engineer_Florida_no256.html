Organization Summary:

The Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) has grown significantly. The needs for computing infrastructure structure has grown more than four times since the last time this position was defined. The current position description was written when CSE had 30 faculty and staff members serving 450 students. Today, the CSE computing infrastructure support the needs of 45 faculty and staff members serve classes of more than 2100 students. Most of the more than 100 CSE course sections each semester use the computing infrastructure that has been built and maintained in the department. Access to computing infrastructure is to computer science as access to chemicals is to chemistry. This infrastructure is our bread and butter, our sandbox. This infrastructure includes Unix servers, Hadoop clusters, high-end GPU clusters, special networks for cybersecurity research, logic labs, computer systems lab, image processing lab, AR devices, brain-computer interfaces, and specialized computing robotics platforms in research labs. The students do not just use these devices; they probe, investigate their "guts", and even build them.

Position Summary:

The Systems Administrator III applies professional experience and formal education or training to administer various types of computer operating systems in a complex engineering environment. The Systems Administrator III duties include installment of hardware and software, ensuring network connectivity, performance analysis, ensuring a secure computing environment, and management of data storage. Due to the critical nature of computing infrastructure for departmental operations, this position will require at times, the availability after business hours to respond to emergencies. The person may also be required to lift light to moderate loads (25-50 pounds), in an indoor environment as related to technical work assignments

DUTIES:

  • Manages, delegates and solves issues on large production, teaching and research network. This includes PCs, laptops, and workstations running various operating systems: Windows, Unix, Linux (all varieties), and Mac OS. Provides technical support to teaching infrastructure, department classrooms, and computer labs.

  • Ensures security patches are current on all servers and workstations. Writes shell scripts to validate and delete old accounts. Manages user accounts and groups. Writes shell scripts to automate daily tasks and monitor the load and resources usage of servers. Implements mirroring, RAID,and fault tolerance solutions. Maintains and upgrades the following services: DNS, DHCP, NFS, Web (and proxy), sftp, ssh, Samba, container,and others. Admin & maintenance of Identity System, Software, Service Provisioning & Configuration, and VDI solutions. Manages the department GPU cluster and associated infrastructure.

  • Plans and implements new projects. Deploys and synchronizes new installations into the network. Installs servers, storage units, racks, switches, firewalls, and other IT equipment as needed. Performs and monitors regular backups and disaster recovery plans.

  • Purchases hardware or software and provides guidance and recommendation to faculty and staff as needed during the purchasing of technical equipment, services, and/or software. Maintains an inventory of all department equipment (servers, storage, PCs, switches and printers, laptops, tablets, and other technical physical and virtual assets). Supervises TAs assigned to the CSE IT Support group. Works as a liaison between the department and other technical resources in the college and central USF IT.

  • Assists faculty and staff with connections, and troubleshooting department resources, including remote access. Installs and maintains specialized software on workstations and servers. Responds to emergencies as needed, even if it is after business hours.

  • Performs other duties as assigned.

QUALIFICATIONS (Education & Experience):

Minimum Qualifications:

Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, MIS or other field involving software and analytical training, and four years of IT related work experience, OR a Bachelor's degree with no specific required field and five years of IT related work experience, OR a Master's degree and three years of IT related work experience, OR a combination of eight years of IT related work experience and validated training. Preparation for a relevant IT certification is considered to be related training.

Preferred Qualifications:

Bachelor's degree in CS or IT with two years of experience; Or a Master's degree in directly related fields. Four years of experience providing technical support of multiple operating systems for a large, advanced computing system, hardware and software installation, and upgrades and maintenance of systems.
