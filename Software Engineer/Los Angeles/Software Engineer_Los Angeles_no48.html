About Ruggable:

Ruggable is a Los Angeles-based, venture-backed, rapidly growing e-commerce startup that is disrupting the $7B rug industry and revolutionizing the market for residential rugs. Our patented 2-Piece Rug System allows you to remove the top layer of your rug and wash it in your home washing machine, offering an affordable, stylish and convenient solution for young families, pet owners, and busy individuals. We're a small, entrepreneurial team with big ambitions. We have a strong brand, established traction, and amazing advisors fueling our steady growth. This is an opportunity to get in at the ground floor and solve for complex challenges, while charting your own career.

Job Summary:

Levity is seeking a driven and entrepreneurial Software Engineer I who is looking to grow in a fast-paced and highly collaborative environment. As the go-to software engineer on the Levity Technology team, you will play an integral role in creating new features for the website. This is a unique opportunity to grow with a new brand and take on a large amount of responsibility for the technology direction.

What You'll Do:

  • Work with the engineering and product teams to build consumer facing features and marketing pages
  • Build new features to help increase conversion like new landing pages, streamline checkout process, and iterate existing key features on our website
  • Collaborate with different departments to meet technical needs and increase efficiencies across the company
  • Iterate and improve on existing features to keep codebase performant and eliminate technical debt
  • Contribute to documentation for current and future processes

What You'll Need to Have:

  • 2+ years experience building highly-scalable interactive web applications (e-commerce preferred)
  • A detail oriented eye for creating interfaces based off of design files
  • Growth mindset and a desire to learn
  • Deep understanding of HTML, CSS, and JS
  • Vast knowledge of React (we use Next.js)
  • Ability to write code with performance and accessibility in mind
  • Comfortable interfacing with REST APIs
  • Interfacing with design programs like Figma, Sketch, or Zeplin
  • JIRA or other day-to-day workload management tools experience
