Job Summary
The Children’s Hospital of Philadelphia (CHOP) Research Institute and its Department of Biomedical and Health Informatics (DBHi) are seeking a software engineer to help build an enterprise-level data and informatics platform called “Arcus”. The Arcus team integrates with major scientific initiatives in the Research Institute strategic plan involving high-impact research areas such as lifespan, rare diseases, novel devices and therapeutics, and precision health.

This role will work with a team of software engineers and bioinformatics scientists focused on architecting and implementing cloud-native information and services to enable enterprise-wide accessibility of ‘omic data (genomics, proteomics, etc). Responsibilities will include data management, workflow development, and implementing variant warehouse storage and API access. We are looking for highly creative people who share our mission to advance child health and who will thrive in a continuous learning environment, acquiring and applying both new technical skills and biomedical domain knowledge.
Job Responsibilities
  • Make ‘omics data accessible to researchers.
  • Implement current best practices for big data workflows, processing, storage, and manipulation of multidimensional data.
  • Use and integrate with cloud services offered by Google Cloud Platform or Amazon Web Services.
  • Communicate solutions to stakeholders, including bioinformatics management, bioinformatics scientists, information systems staff, and principal investigators.
  • Contribute to the design and implementation of APIs making ‘omic data more accessible.
  • Establish policies and standards for performance benchmarking and system stability.
  • Promote sustainability and enterprise adoption through proactive documentation.
  • Adapt existing ETL/workflows to operate in the cloud at scale and optimize robustness and cost.
Required Education and Experience
Required Education: Bachelor’s degree

Required Experience:
  • BS/BA in a software or systems engineering or related discipline.
  • At least five (5) years of programming and/or large-scale system development.
  • At least three (3) years of experience in a big data processing role.
  • Knowledge of high performance/parallel computing environments and containerized workflow development.
