Who we are:

CPI makes the technology that powers your everyday life. If you’ve ever used a self-checkout, played the slots at a casino, gotten a drink from a vending machine, or paid to park your car, you’ve probably done so with the aid of one of the 10 million devices installed by CPI. From cash and coins to cards and mobile, we keep the world of payments moving with smart validation devices and business-management software. Our technology powers more than 4 billion transactions every week – and we want you to be a part of it.

Payment technology is constantly evolving, and our associates are up for the challenge. We pride ourselves in our ability to deliver excellent service and support for our vast existing field base, while pushing the envelope of innovation to design and deliver new solutions that reflect the modern payment landscape.

At CPI you will become a valuable part of our culture of inclusion and collaboration, working in partnership with more than 3,000 global associates supporting 110 countries and currencies. If you are interested in making a global impact, we are looking for a full-time Cloud DevOps Engineer to join us.


About the role:

The Cloud DevOps Engineer will have a proven track record in designing and deploying services and infrastructure to the public cloud, cloud management and monitoring with focus on automation. The Cloud DevOps Engineer will help manage our production application environments in the cloud and assist in making technical decisions regarding deployment architecture and implementation. Successful candidates will have knowledge and experience in operations, cloud computing, networks, security, databases and software development lifecycle. The Cloud DevOps Engineer must have excellent problem solving skills to diagnose root causes, evaluate and resolve complex software issues, and when appropriate, escalate or assign issues to appropriate team members.


About the team:

The cloud infrastructure team is composed of a globally dispersed group of 6:

  • DBA (Washington, DC)
  • Cloud DevOps Engineer (Ukraine)
  • Cloud DevOps Engineer (Malvern, PA)
  • Infrastructure Engineer (Malvern, PA)
  • Infrastructure Engineer (Williston, SC)
  • Infrastructure Manager (Malvern, PA)

This group is anchored out of our global HQ in Malvern, PA, however, this role will primarily be remote with some onsite requirements. The ideal candidate will be within 1.5 hour drive from Malvern, PA.


Why work with us:

With a 160-year history of innovation and growth, more than 3,000 patents, and an unmatched commitment to success, you are joining a company that has shaped the global landscape of technology. At CPI, you will become a valuable part of an inclusive culture of collaboration and innovation, all while directly impacting global payment solutions. Come see your ideas make change.


Responsibilities and Duties

  • Design, build, maintain, document, and automate technical processes and operations using AWS cloud services
  • Manage live production and dev environments, ensuring system availability and scalability
  • Monitor systems performance, and troubleshoot errors/debug
  • Implement and maintain end to end cloud security program for AWS infrastructure
  • Manage permissions using AWS IAM policies
  • Operate the platform within the Crane Co. security and privacy guidelines
  • Implement and manage CI/CD pipelines for application deployment
  • Work with and support various team members across the organization, including development, support, and product teams
  • Develop and implement best practices around security management, availability management, and release management.
  • Create and maintain documentation on Linux server builds, policies and procedures, ensuring they are accurate and current.
  • Perform other duties/projects as assigned

Qualifications and Required Skills

  • Bachelors Degree in Computer science or related field
  • 5 or more years of related information technology experience
  • Demonstrated experience in network design and operations of enterprise systems
  • 2-5 years of experience using AWS cloud computing
  • Proficiency with Terraform
  • Managing and architecting AWS Cloud environments
  • Configuration management tools such as Chef, Puppet, Ansible, etc.
  • System monitoring tools such as Datadog or New Relic
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills
  • Experience implementing security best practices associated with the AWS platform
  • High energy level and comfortable performing multifaceted projects in conjunction with day-to-day activities
  • Project management oriented with ability to lead project planning through implementation
  • Detail orientation, and the ability to record, organize, and communicate
  • Ability to work flexible schedules when required
  • A strong understanding of networking is required
  • AWS certification is preferred, but not required
