Job Description


The Technology Development Analytics and Automation (TDAA) Oregon Automation Operations group is seeking a Software Support Engineer to provide sustaining support for Factory Automation software in our Assembly, Test, and Advanced Wafer Prep factories. Factory Automation software is used to control factory equipment, track the product as it moves through the factory, measure product features, detect defects, handle materials, collect and analyze data, optimize factory scheduling and enable efficient factory operations.

This role will require the ability to:

  • Take ownership of customer issues reported and seeing problems through to resolution
  • Research, diagnose, troubleshoot and identify solutions to resolve system issues
  • Follow standard procedures for proper escalation of unresolved issues to the appropriate subject matter expert.
  • Participate in the site on-call rotation, with a frequency of about 1 week every 2 months, providing on-call remote after-hours support as needed.
  • Participate in the all phases of the Software Development Life Cycle.
  • Install and maintain factory automation software applications
  • Work with factory customers to test software
  • Report issues identified to appropriate teams and track to closure.
  • Train additional support personnel on issue identification, troubleshooting and resolution.
  • Deep dive/investigate and issues raised by factory business partners
  • Identify trends and opportunities for improvements.
  • Work with various partner organizations to identify short-term and long-term problem resolution and improve software quality.
  • Create and maintain troubleshooting documentation
  • Configure Statistical Process Control (SPC) parameters.
  • Automate routine tasks.

The ideal candidate should exhibit the following behavioral traits:

  • Written and oral communication skills.
  • Analytical problem-solving skills.
  • Teamwork and collaboration skills.
  • Tolerance of ambiguity and self-motivation.
  • Demonstrated track record of effectiveness in a high paced and highly matrixed business environment
  • Ability to follow business processes in a disciplined manner.

This is an entry level position and will be compensated accordingly


Qualifications


You must possess the below minimum qualifications to be initially considered for this position. Preferred qualifications are in addition to the minimum requirements and are considered a plus factor in identifying top candidates. Experience listed below would be obtained through a combination of your schoolwork/classes/research and/or relevant previous job and/or internship experiences.

Minimum Qualifications:

BS or MS in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, or related field. Must have the required degree or expect the required degree by June 2021

The candidate must have a 6+ months of experience in the following areas:

  • Windows operating system
  • Software engineering practices and testing methodologies
  • Writing and reading XML files
  • Troubleshooting software issues through log files

Preferred Qualifications:

6+ months experience in the following areas:

  • Object-Oriented software languages (e.g., C Sharp)
  • Windows technologies (e.g., WCF, WPF, . NET, etc.)
  • Software versioning (e.g., TFS, Git, etc.)
  • Databases (e.g., PL/SQL, Oracle DB)
  • Intel Assembly/Test manufacturing environment and business processes
  • Intel Assembly/Test factory automation systems (e.g., Station Controller, Unit Level Tracking, Manufacturing Execution System, Recipe Management, Experiment Management, etc.)
