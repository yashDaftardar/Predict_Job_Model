Full Job Description

Strategic Analysis, Inc, (SA) is a mid-sized business operating for over thirty years to successfully provide our U. S. Government and private sector clients with the very best in scientific and engineering analysis, software development, strategic planning, and program management support.

Currently we are seeking a summer Engineer intern (June-Sept) for a Staff Scientist/Engineer position in the Energy Analysis Division of Strategic Analysis Inc. The position is well-suited for a college student (undergrad or graduate) in engineering or the hard sciences (Physics, Chemistry, Material Science, etc.) or for more experienced applicants willing to undertake a temporary position. The position is full-time, paid, and to be conducted remotely under the supervision of SA personnel. Daily hours of work are flexible as well as the employment start and end dates.

We seek stellar applicants able to provide support to multi-disciplinary analysis (typically a blend of electrochemistry, material science, mechanical engineering, and chemical engineering) on a variety of cutting-edge issues focused on fuel cell power generation and associated alternative energy systems. The position is viewed as entry-level assistance in the technical evaluation, system modeling, performance estimation, techno-economic analysis, and Design for Manufacturing & Assembly (DFMA) capital cost estimation of advanced/emerging technology energy systems. Tasking, responsibilities, and pay will be aligned with the successful applicant’s experience and capabilities.

Requirements
  • Must be a US Citizen
  • Pursuing a Bachelor’s or advanced degree in engineering or other hard science: Mechanical, Electrical, or Chemical Engineering preferred. Preferably a rising junior (3rd year) or senior (4th year).
  • Strong interest in alternative energy systems and/or manufacturing science
  • Excellent oral and written communication skills
  • Self-motivation: ability to work both independently and as a team player
  • Familiarity with alternative energy system preferred
  • Familiarity with system performance estimation/analysis preferred
  • Familiarity with techno-economic analysis preferred
  • Microsoft Word, PowerPoint & Excel skills required. Familiarity with Python, data base, ASPEN/HYSYS, and CAD preferred
Job Description
  • Assist in Design for Manufacture and Assembly (DFMA) cost analysis
  • Assist in creation of system layout drawings/schematics
  • Assist in system performance calculations
  • Assist in obtaining component/material price quotations
  • Assist in report and presentation preparation
  • Assist in database creation and maintenance
  • Assist in preparation of Python and Excel modeling
  • Assist in Alternative Energy marketplace research
