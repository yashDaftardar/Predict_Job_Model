Trimble is recruiting for a Senior GNSS Signal Processing Engineer

The GNSS Signal Processing Engineer will be responsible for developing and implementing new signal processing algorithms. The person will be working effectively in the development team with other international experts, and demonstrating a set of fundamental engineering skills that result in predictability in engineering, scheduling and the quality of products developed.

Key Responsibilities:
Design and implement low level GNSS signal processing algorithms
Investigate and develop multipath and interference mitigation algorithms
Optimize algorithms for speed and power consumption
Assist in RF antenna and front end specification and design
Contribute to existing signal processing development and maintenance
Work in an international team and cooperate with people across the world.
Clearly document and communicate newly designed algorithms to accurately describe the technical details

Skills & Experience Required:
BS in Electrical Engineering, Computer Science, Computer Engineering or related field with 5+ yearsâ€™ experience. M.Sc. or Ph.D. preferred.
Deep knowledge and understanding of low level GNSS signal processing including correlation, signal acquisition and tracking, and multipath mitigation
Experience with tracking all GNSS signal structures and the various design trade-offs
Understanding of RF front end design and filtering
Proficiency in digital design techniques with VHDL/Verilog
Knowledge of Matlab, Linux, and embedded real time operating systems
Work well with various international engineering groups such as firmware, hardware, RF, QA and product management
