Job Description

The National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL), located at the foothills of the Rocky Mountains in Golden, Colorado, is the nation’s primary laboratory for research and development of renewable energy and energy efficiency technologies. Our Power Systems Engineering Center develops methods for real-time operations and controls of power systems at various scales to support a more reliable and efficient electric grid.

NREL’s intern program provides a diverse opportunity for students and recent graduates. In addition to mentorship and real, hands-on experience, students and recent graduates will participate in professional development activities that will help strengthen their technical and professional skills.

The Grid Automation and Control Group is seeking a summer intern to work on multiple projects focused on developing advanced control and optimization algorithms for managing distributed energy resources (DERs) such as photovoltaic (PV), mobile battery energy storage systems, and building loads to improve grid resilience and stability. The successful candidate will support NREL researchers to model the system, implement the algorithm and develop a co-simulation platform to run analysis

Ideal candidates will have a strong background in Python programming (or other similar languages). The candidate with knowledge and background in power system modeling and simulation is more preferred. The successful intern will help develop codes and software tools to conduct distribution grid modeling and co-simulations (potentially using NREL’s high performance computing system), and develop tools to visualize results and data.

.
