### What we're up to

**We're helping creators become entrepreneurs!**

We're building the best way for creators to have an online presence with their brand front and center. Instead of promoting a mishmash of different tools, creators have a single website with all the tools they need (paid memberships, newsletters, podcasts, chat, analytics, donations, etc...).

You can think of it like Shopify, but focused on what creators need instead of shops : )

What we're building is incredibly exciting - not only does it have a ridiculously high ceiling, we get to work on something fun! We get to help creators break free from being cogs and become entrepreneurs themselves. Creators are awesome, and we want to build an equally awesome company to help them!

We want any creator who wants make money online to be able to do it successfully through Spore!

**We're looking to bring on engineers who can own an entire segment of our product.** The segments are pretty large, with a lot of interesting problems to solve. If that sounds fun to you, you're a great fit! As our team grows, the goal is for you to be leading the team focused on that part of the product (if that's something you want!)

We of course have the infrastructure in place to make it possible for a small team to be able to successfully work on each part of the product.

We're just getting started, so you'll likely start out working on a few of these, and as we hire more engineers, you'll settle in to focusing on one.

### Segments

- **Monetization**
- Our most important KPI (by far!) is the amount of money we help creators make. So the goal here is to build out different ways for creators to make money through their website. This means paid memberships for exclusive content, the ability to sell digital goods, paid DMs, etc... We'd like to experiment with crypto at some point too
- **Chat / Communities**
- Communities are incredible to watch form and grow, and are going to be a big part of how we help creators. One of the features creators can have in their website is a Discord/Slack-style chat - this is one of the more complicated products we're offering, but also a very fun one to work on : )
- **Newsletters**
- Newsletters are an awesome medium for creators to grow and audience and monetize. There are obviously a few companies built entirely around helping creators maintain a newsletter, but we think there are big advantages to having it baked into their website
- **Analytics**
- This is the backbone of a lot of the value Spore provides. Basically a CRM & analytics for creators to better understand their audience, and to be able to segment their audience based on how they interact with a creator's content
- **Merch & Shops**
- Creators like to create : ) And that includes merchandise. We want to help them with this, and enable them to have a merch shop directly in their site. We'd also like to help them sell digital merch (whether that's an NFT, or something like a course or ebook)
- **Podcasts**
- We have a subset of features that are useful for podcasters, and we want to continue to improve on them!
- **Site customization**
- We let creators customize what their site looks like. Right now that means letting them choose the components they want to show, rearrange them, and customize colors, fonts, and images. Longer-term this will look more like more powerful site builders like Shopify & Squarespace.
- **Infrastructure and scaling**
- We need someone who can make sure we stay in a good position to scale upward. Our current infrastructure stack has scaled to 10mm MAU and 150,000 concurrent with a previous startup, but there will likely still be road bumps along the way!
- **Security & Troll management**
- With any online community, there's going to be trolls, and we need to have tools in place to keep them at bay to help creators' communities thrive. We also need to make sure we're adhering to security best-practices!

### Our stack

No worries if you're not familiar with some of these - as long as you're a fast learner, you'll be good. We're going to do our best to document everything to make it easy for new engineers to understand everything!

**Frontend**
- React
- Our "version" of React is a little non-traditional. We used hooks, but instead of useState, we use [RxJS](https://rxjs-dev.firebaseapp.com/guide/overview) streams. The benefit is a single stream can be passed through components that is able to do the equivalent of reads and writes (so the equivalent of '[this, andThis] = useState()')
- We also use [hyperscript](https://mithril.js.org/hyperscript.html) instead of JSX

**Backend**
- Node.js
- Websockets
- ScyllaDB (Cassandra, but written in C++ instead of Java for better performance)
- Elasticsearch
- Redis
- Kubernetes (GKE)

### Our approach

A lot of companies spend too much time thinking about the best way to build something, and not enough time actually building. The best learning experiences coming from just writing code and figuring out what works and what doesn't.

We optimize for speed - the first version of a new feature doesn't need to be perfect... the goal is to get it out there, learn, and quickly iterate to improve it.

We'll write tests for mission-critical features, and eventually features that we know aren't going to change a whole lot, but in general we'll probably write less tests than a lot of companies.
