Job Description

The National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL), located at the foothills of the Rocky Mountains in Golden, Colorado is the nation's primary laboratory for research and development of renewable energy and energy efficiency technologies.

The Complex Systems Simulation and Optimization Group in the NREL Computational Science Center has an opening for a summer intern, with an emphasis on open discretization and finite element method (FEM) frameworks. The growing capability of scientific computation is opening the door to larger-scale engineering modeling and simulation. Although there are many high-quality commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) codes for model creation and solution at workstation scales, they may not present adequate capability or capacity relevant to many-user HPC installations. Surmounting the unique challenges presented by emerging exascale environments require intensive application co-design. In addition, many COTS codes limit access to the underlying model and algorithmic parameters, making it impractical to customize calculations as needed. We are thus looking for an experienced researcher with a strong technical background to assist in introducing users to the FEM world beyond the COTS desktop.

The successful candidate will collaborate to design multi-faceted example problems and construct simulations suitable for discretization and solving via select open and customizable tools ( e.g. , Gmsh, FEniCS, CEED, MFEM) in support of NREL and EERE mission and goals. The candidate will instantiate performant frameworks on NREL’s high-performance computing system Eagle, achieve a core understanding of the required workflow to move from engineering model to numerical solution, and assist in documenting this understanding.

This intern will collaborate with NREL computational scientists to

  • instantiate a software capability on NREL HPC centered on available open tools for domain meshing, partial differential equation formulation and solution;
  • define a limited test set of exemplar physics and models of variable sophistication to demonstrate movement from desktop scales to the exascale;
  • create, discretize, and solve these models; and,
  • document their studies to help NREL-HPC users move their work to customizable and scalable workflows.
