About Our Job

About Technology Services

The Technology Services Department (TS) of the City and County of Denver is using state-of-the-art technologies and methodologies to deliver and improve the systems, applications and operations that we deliver to our customers. Technology Services supports the people, agencies and ideas that make the City and County of Denver world class city. The City offers a unique opportunity to work with a diverse business and technology environment on a large scale as we employ over 13,000 people, of which 9,000+ are daily technology consumers in support of a diverse population in excess of 700,000 citizens.

About Application Development

The Application Development team provides software solutions that increase efficiency, automate processes, improve customer service, and achieve city objectives. Applications partners with city agencies/department to assess technology needs, identify solutions, ensure regulatory compliance, and validate that expectations are met. From citywide platforms to business specific tools, this division handles the procurement, development, implementation, integration, quality assurance/testing, maintenance, and support for more than 300 applications, including their underlying systems and databases.

Diversity in the City

The City and County of Denver is committed to cultivating a culture of equity, diversity, and inclusion. This commitment is woven into our values and belief that we are strongest when we embrace and celebrate our differences. We aim to have employees who are as diverse as our residents, with different perspectives and unique ways of thinking. If you share these values and our enthusiasm for equity, we encourage you to apply to join our team.

As the Application Development Manager in the City and County of Denver, you will be in a key leadership role that will coach and mentor a team of 13 application and software developers focused on publicly facing web applications and large-scale systems integration. Specific development platforms include Salesforce, Open Cities (content management), Angular (JavaScript framework), GIS, and Accela.

Additionally, the Application Development Manager:

  • Is responsible for ensuring implementation of DevOps best practices (continuous integration/continuous delivery, test driven development) while empowering the team to take accountability and ownership of processes.
  • Collects performance data and makes data driven decisions for operational and process improvements.
  • Develops team key performance indicators (KPIs) around code standards, quality standards, and reliability standards while building alignment with organizational strategic objectives.
  • Represents the Technology Services agency through meetings with other agencies, at conferences, and in negotiations for project scope with vendors
  • Creates presentations and proposals for agency leadership and executives based on technology trends, best practices, and architectural improvements
  • Acts as an agile Product Owner and develops roadmaps for Angular/.NET applications and technical debt remediation efforts for denvergov.org and pocketgov.org
  • Ensures timely software development delivery by working with cross functional teams and stakeholders

About You

We are looking for someone with some or all the following skills and experience:

  • Experience with ADO or equivalent SDLC tool
  • A proactive, hands-on, go-getter attitude
  • An influential leader with a commitment to team engagement
  • The ability to be collaborative with other managers and individual contributors within the agency, the city, and externally

We realize your time is valuable so please do not apply if you do not have at least the following required minimum qualifications:

  • Education requirement: Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science, Mathematics, Business, or a related field.
  • Experience Requirement: Three (3) years of professional level information technology work.
  • Education/Experience Equivalency: Two (2) years of the appropriate type and level of experience may be substituted for each required year of post-high school education.
  • Additional appropriate education may be substituted for the minimum experience
