Powered by endlessly curious people with an unwavering mission focus, Ball Aerospace pioneers discoveries that enable our customers to perform beyond expectation and protect what matters most.


We create innovative space solutions, enable more accurate weather forecasts, drive insightful observations of our planet, deliver actionable data and intelligence, and ensure those who defend our freedom go forward bravely and return home safely.

Project Engineer II - Ground Software Lead


What You’ll Do:


  • Perform as Chief Product Owner in an Agile work environment using AWS cloud.
  • Lead Software team, manage risks, and remove roadblocks.
  • Work closely with product managers and story owners to design features and prioritize tasks.
  • Assemble releases, manage software development content, and drive improvements in release processes by content prioritization and other Agile development methodologies.
  • Work with program SEIT in ensuring proper systems engineering and systems test.
  • Ensure adherence to Software Development Plans and Software Standards.
  • Support Software Configuration Control Boards (CCB) and Engineering Review Boards (ERB).
  • Analyze and implement key program interface documents.
  • Perform trade studies, performance analyses, and risk management.
  • Support the creation, refinement, and implementation of program training materials required for the education and instruction of the program users and operators.
  • Oversee and guide the management of requirements – allocation, verification, and validation.
  • Provide leadership / mentorship of technical teams to define, direct implementation, and operate complex mission systems which include spacecraft, instruments, and ground systems.
  • Provide engineering support across multiple and diverse collection scenarios by developing concepts to meet customer mission needs.
  • Develop software engineering execution plans – schedules, staffing, and cost.
  • Develop metrics to evaluate concepts that represent mission utility for the customer.
  • Prepare and give presentations to peers/management/customers, especially executive level management.
  • Support technical proposal development as needed.
  • Maintain a regular and predictable work schedule.
  • Establish and maintain effective working relationships within the department, the Strategic Business Units, Strategic Support Units and the Company. Interact appropriately with others in order to maintain a positive and productive work environment.
  • Perform other duties as necessary.


What You’ll Need:


  • BS degree or higher in Engineering or a related technical field is required plus 12 or more years related experience.
  • Each higher-level degree, i.e., Master’s Degree or Ph.D., may substitute for two years of experience. Related technical experience may be considered in lieu of education. Degree must be from a university, college, or school which is accredited by an agency recognized by the US Secretary of Education, US Department of Education.
  • Demonstrated knowledge of ground systems for spacecraft operations.
  • Resource and Cost management: Management of IPT resources and costs, and subsequent performance reporting of each area.
  • Experience using tools and metrics to manage development activity, including EVMS and IMS.
  • Experience in Schedule and Risk management.
  • Experience with Software Development Plans and Software Standards.
  • Support teams by answering questions and solving problems.
  • Maintain rapport with software team through solid technical communication, motivation.
  • Active practitioner of software development lifecycle concepts: Agile, DevSecOps, Continuous Integration (CI), and Continuous Deployment (CD) making use of Jenkins, test automation, and other tools.
  • Experience with satellite data analysis, data exploitation algorithms, and/or data processing systems.
  • Cost Account Manager (CAM) experience on full Earned Value Management (EVMS) compliant contract desired.
  • Demonstrated ability to effectively work with and communicate with all levels of management and individual contributors on the program team.
  • Knowledge of related disciplines in software engineering, hardware engineering, integration, test engineering, quality engineering, configuration/data management.
  • Strong software development background.
  • Understanding of software development lifecycle models and program lifecycle models.
  • Proven written and oral communication skills, including presentation skills.
  • An advanced degree is preferred.


Working Conditions:


  • Work is performed in an office, laboratory, production floor, or clean room, outdoors or remote research environment.
  • May occasionally work in production work centers where use of protective equipment and gear is required.
  • May access other facilities in various weather conditions.
  • Travel and local commute between Ball campuses and other possible non-Ball locations may be required.
