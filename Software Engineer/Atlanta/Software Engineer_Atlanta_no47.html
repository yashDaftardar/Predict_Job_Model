Full Job Description

Lottery:
Lottery games are one of the biggest consumer products on the planet and Scientific Games is the world’s largest supplier. Our lottery games have been entertaining and thrilling players since 1973. Most importantly our games fund good causes like education, health and welfare, senior citizens, roads, bridges and the environment.

Scientific Games supports lotteries in more than 50 countries by delivering industry leading marketing and insights, unmatched retailer innovation, developing first class mobile-based products and most recently launching our virtual SG Sports betting platform.

We are looking for employees interested in helping Scientific Games strengthen our position as the world’s foremost leader in lottery products and services.

Position Summary

The Associate DevOps Software Engineer (Intern) will be part of the lottery division’s engineering team at Scientific Games. Your work on this team will help enable the funding of good causes like education, health care, senior citizens, roads, bridges, state parks and other environmentally friendly improvements.

The main objective for an Intern role at SG will be to create a true win/win relationship where the intern will be exposed to the SG Lottery Engineering Organization in roles and functions that will provide the intern valuable industry experience as well as Lottery business experience. SG also expects to receive material contributions in whatever areas the intern may contribute in. The hope is to lay a foundation for a long-term relationship that would extend past the intern period.

In addition to the good causes lottery’s fund, lottery in general has proven to perform well through recessions. Even in 2020, we experienced growth in some verticals within the lottery division.

Intern roles at SG will provide a real-world environment and every attempt will be made to challenge the intern. The projects and efforts may be unique to meet the requirements of the team or manager assigned. Where possible assignments will be adjusted to fit the duration and timing of the intern’s work period. The reporting structure and actual assignments should be expected to potentially need flexibility and may need to change.
