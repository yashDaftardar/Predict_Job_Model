Position Overview

Autodesk Construction Solutions is looking for an Android or iOS Engineer to help us build the future faster. Our users are building some of the most amazing construction projects in the world, and our platform is their central information point. On the Mobile Platform team, our mobile engineers are your customer, and your role will be to make those engineers more productive in our mobile codebases. Whether that’s reusable components, shared APIs, shared infrastructure like our sync engineer, or any other initiative that you or the team focuses on to improve developer productivity for everyone. Our stacks are diverse, so you will g et exposure to many technologies including Swift, Kotlin , Kotlin Native, and more.

We are open to remote engineers , and furthermore, this team is almost entirely remote, so there is little worry of feeling left out.

Responsibilities

  • As an Android or iOS Engineer, you’ll tackle difficult and interesting challenges and help build and improve the overall health of our mobile platform to enable our engineers to be hyper-productive

  • Building near real-time collaboration and mark-up for construction drawings

  • Enhancing our shared library that is used across all three of our mobile platforms (iOS, Android, and Windows Desktop) written in Kotlin Native

  • Building slick and responsive UI that can handle millions of user generated data points (like photos and annotations)

  • Building tools to streamline communication and collaboration on the construction site

  • Tackling synchronization and consistency that allows our users to work and edit offline

  • Replacing Excel workflows with simple, powerful tools

Minimum Qualifications

  • Have at least 2 years of engineering experience

  • Have an interest in working on and ramping up on our mobile platforms, particularly both iOS and Android

  • Have the passion and dedication to write highly scalable, reliable code

  • Love solving tricky problems

  • Have experience building Android apps in Java and Kotlin

  • Team player — you love to teach others about cool new tricks you learned and absorb tips and tricks from developers; love solving problems as a team across platforms
