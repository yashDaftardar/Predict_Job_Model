Job Title: Software Engineer - Localization
Job Location: Novi, MI

Who we are:
Arriver™ is a new software unit and brand, fully focused on developing perception, fusion and drive policy software for the next generation cars. It will deliver an open, scalable and flexible architecture solution running on Qualcomm® Snapdragon Ride™ System on a Chip (SoC) platform. Arriver™ has 800 people in 5 countries – China, Germany, Romania, Sweden, USA – and builds on more than a decade of experience in active safety software development. The company is wholly owned by Veoneer.

As a Software Engineer you will design, develop, integrate, and validate software for localization. Our Algorithm Engineers will develop algorithms that will estimate the vehicle's pose, and deduce critical information from HD-map data relevant to this estimated location and our Software Engineer help express these algorithms in C++. The software will interface with the incoming sensor information, carry out inference, and provide the information necessary for downstream decision-making processes.

This position is a flexible remote role which means employees have the flexibility to work remotely, but are expected to report to the Novi-MI office occasionally.

What you'll do:
During the course of development, you will be expected to work in an agile fashion, live-and-breathe good coding practices, and engage with the team members. You will be able to participate in all parts of the development cycle, from fine-tuning requirements, to writing production-quality software, participating in peer code reviews, and finally, validation and iterative improvement. All localization members are encouraged to be proactive in both, developing their own skillset, and in presenting creative ideas to improve the software and workflow.

Required Qualifications:
A Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, Computer Engineering or Electrical Engineering is required. A Master’s Degree is preferred.

Minimum 3 years of experience developing ADAS Software in C++ for Automotive Production.

Excellent written and verbal communication skills.

Flexible and willing to accept a change in priorities as necessary.

Nice to Have:
Prior experience with vehicle autonomy, UAV or robotics

Understanding of probabilistic methods like Bayesian filtering and graphical inference.

Understanding of common modeling concepts: vehicle state, sensor etc.

Hands-on experience (academic or industry) with sensor fusion or estimation, and sensors like camera or radars

Exposure to standards compliance (ISO-26262, ASPICE)

Tools & Technology:
Experience with Agile Scrum

Must have prior experience with Git.

Must be comfortable working in a Linux environment.

Confluence, JIRA
