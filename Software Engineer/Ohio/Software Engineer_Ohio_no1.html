About Us

Do you want an opportunity to work with top companies solving interesting business challenges through technology? As an employee-owned software consultancy, we consider software development a craft. Our team of expert developers and designers hold the title of ‘Crafter’ to reflect our dedication to quality, our emphasis on continual learning, and our belief that software is truly a craft—something that we take pride in, and value highly. We value colleagues who enjoy learning and teaching, which creates our collaborative organisation.

About Our Training Programme

We know that getting that first software job can be tough. Many organisations require you to have years of experience or a computer science degree for that first role. At 8th Light, we invest in people even if you haven’t had the chance yet to demonstrate and grow their software skills.

Our paid training programme is a focused period of learning centered around becoming a disciplined, skilled, well-rounded software crafter capable of delivering high-quality software and consulting to our clients.

Under the guidance of a team of experienced mentors, Trainee Software Crafters will embark on a process designed to hone their programming, client service, collaboration, leadership and communication skills. They learn on their own while committing to, and completing a variety of projects in different languages. They learn to lead by example, provide and receive honest feedback and answer the questions a client might not ask.

Towards the end of the programme, Trainee Software Crafters spend time shadowing and pairing with 8th Light crafters on client projects. The programme ends with a demonstration of their work that is evaluated by a review board of 8th Light crafters.

Applicants

You've built software before, maybe not in a professional setting, but you’re an avid learner and are highly driven to improve your skills. Successful applicants generally have:

  • An understanding of at least one programming language.
  • A basic understanding of Test-Driven Development.
  • Professional working experience (not necessarily in software).

We understand that sometimes lists of qualifications might cause feelings of imposter syndrome. We truly hope to hear from you.

Expectations of a Crafter

Upon successful completion of the training programme, you'll join 8th Light's team of developers and designers as a Crafter. In this role, you will:

  • Pair and collaborate with teammates to deliver quality software.
  • Mentor Trainee Software Crafters and client employees based on 8th Light best practices.
  • Work with clients to report progress and plan future projects.
